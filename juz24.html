Surah 39 · Makkan · 75 Verses

Az-Zumar — The Groups

الزُّمَر
📍 Makkah 🔢 75 Verses 📖 Juz begins at verse 32

This surah draws its name from the "groups" (zumar) of people driven to Hell and Paradise on the Day of Judgment. Its central message is the call to sincere worship, repentance, and trust in Allah's mercy.

The Truthful vs. The Liar

Az-Zumar 39:32–35
فَمَنْ أَظْلَمُ مِمَّن كَذَبَ عَلَى اللَّهِ وَكَذَّبَ بِالصِّدْقِ إِذْ جَاءَهُ ۚ أَلَيْسَ فِي جَهَنَّمَ مَثْوًى لِّلْكَافِرِينَ ۝ وَالَّذِي جَاءَ بِالصِّدْقِ وَصَدَّقَ بِهِ ۙ أُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الْمُتَّقُونَ ۝ لَهُم مَّا يَشَاءُونَ عِندَ رَبِّهِمْ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ جَزَاءُ الْمُحْسِنِينَ ۝ لِيُكَفِّرَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُمْ أَسْوَأَ الَّذِي عَمِلُوا وَيَجْزِيَهُمْ أَجْرَهُم بِأَحْسَنِ الَّذِي كَانُوا يَعْمَلُونَ
"So who is more unjust than one who lies about Allah and denies the truth when it has come to him? Is there not in Hell a residence for the disbelievers? And the one who has brought the truth and [they who] believed in it — those are the righteous. They will have whatever they desire with their Lord. That is the reward of the doers of good. So that Allah may remove from them the worst of what they did and reward them according to the best of what they used to do." (39:32–35)

These verses present a sharp moral contrast: on one side stands the person who invents lies against Allah — attributing partners, sons, or false attributes to Him — and who rejects the truth when it arrives. On the other stands the one who carries, embodies, and confirms the truth. Most scholars of tafsir identify "the one who brought the truth" as the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, and "those who believed in it" as his Companions and the faithful Muslim community.

The reward described is remarkable in its generosity. Not only will the believers receive whatever they wish from their Lord, but Allah will additionally expunge their worst deeds from the record — rewarding them instead according to their best actions. This reflects the divine principle that good multiplies while sins are erased through sincere faith and repentance.

📚 Ibn Kathir

Ibn Kathir explains that "the one who brought the truth" is the Prophet ﷺ who came with the Quran, and "those who confirmed it" are the believers of every era. He stresses that among the greatest injustices is speaking falsehood about Allah — attributing partners to Him — because it is an assault on the very foundation of existence.

📚 Al-Jalalayn

Al-Jalalayn note that the "worst of what they did" which Allah removes refers to sins committed before Islam or during periods of heedlessness. Allah erases these, replacing them with the reward of their finest deeds — an act of divine generosity that transcends simple justice.

📚 As-Sa'di

As-Sa'di highlights that these verses are a beacon of hope: even believers who have sinned will find their record cleansed. The condition is truthfulness — carrying and confirming the truth in both speech and action. He emphasises that "the doers of good" (al-muhsinun) are those who perfect their worship and their dealings with others.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • 🚫 Lying about Allah — attributing to Him what He did not say — is among the gravest injustices.
  • ✅ The Prophet ﷺ and the believers are the custodians of truth; following them is the path to salvation.
  • 🤲 Allah rewards believers not just fairly, but generously — erasing their worst and rewarding their best.
  • 💎 Sincerity in faith (sidq) is what distinguishes the righteous from the hypocrite or denier.

Allah is Sufficient — No Need for Other Protectors

Az-Zumar 39:36–38
أَلَيْسَ اللَّهُ بِكَافٍ عَبْدَهُ ۖ وَيُخَوِّفُونَكَ بِالَّذِينَ مِن دُونِهِ ۚ وَمَن يُضْلِلِ اللَّهُ فَمَا لَهُ مِنْ هَادٍ ۝ وَمَن يَهْدِ اللَّهُ فَمَا لَهُ مِن مُّضِلٍّ ۗ أَلَيْسَ اللَّهُ بِعَزِيزٍ ذِي انتِقَامٍ ۝ وَلَئِن سَأَلْتَهُم مَّنْ خَلَقَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ لَيَقُولُنَّ اللَّهُ
"Is Allah not sufficient for His servant? And yet they threaten you with those [they worship] other than Him. And whoever Allah leaves astray — for him there is no guide. And whoever Allah guides — for him there is no misleader. Is not Allah Exalted in Might and Owner of Retribution? And if you asked them who created the heavens and the earth, they would surely say, 'Allah.'" (39:36–38)

The Quraysh attempted to intimidate the Prophet ﷺ by threatening him with their idols — warning that the false gods would harm him for speaking against them. These verses answer that threat with a rhetorical question so powerful it silences all doubt: "Is Allah not sufficient for His servant?"

The argument is then sealed by the polytheists' own admission. Even those who worshipped idols, when asked who truly created the heavens and the earth, would answer: "Allah." Their own tongues testified against their actions. This is the logical trap of shirk — the idolater acknowledges the Creator, yet prostrates to the created.

📚 Ibn Kathir

Ibn Kathir explains this as a direct comfort to the Prophet ﷺ: Allah personally guarantees His servant's protection. The idols the Quraysh threatened with have no power whatsoever, and their own acknowledgement of Allah as Creator exposes the absurdity of their idol-worship.

📚 Al-Jalalayn

Al-Jalalayn note that "His servant" here refers to the Prophet ﷺ in particular, but the principle extends to every sincere believer. Whoever Allah protects cannot be harmed, and whoever He guides cannot be misled by any created being.

📚 As-Sa'di

As-Sa'di reflects beautifully on this verse: if you have Allah, you have everything; if you lack Allah, you have nothing. The threats of disbelievers are hollow, because those they worship cannot even help themselves, let alone harm the people of faith.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • 🛡️ Relying entirely on Allah is the believer's greatest source of strength and protection.
  • 🕳️ The logic of shirk collapses under scrutiny — even polytheists admit Allah is the Creator.
  • 🔑 Guidance and misguidance are in Allah's hands alone; no person or force can override His decree.
  • 💪 Threats from the enemies of truth should not frighten the believer who has Allah as his guardian.

Allah Takes Souls at Sleep and at Death

Az-Zumar 39:42
اللَّهُ يَتَوَفَّى الْأَنفُسَ حِينَ مَوْتِهَا وَالَّتِي لَمْ تَمُتْ فِي مَنَامِهَا ۖ فَيُمْسِكُ الَّتِي قَضَىٰ عَلَيْهَا الْمَوْتَ وَيُرْسِلُ الْأُخْرَىٰ إِلَىٰ أَجَلٍ مُّسَمًّى ۚ إِنَّ فِي ذَٰلِكَ لَآيَاتٍ لِّقَوْمٍ يَتَفَكَّرُونَ
"Allah takes the souls at the time of their death, and those that do not die [He takes] during their sleep. Then He keeps those for which He has decreed death and releases the others for a specified term. Indeed in that are signs for a people who give thought." (39:42)

This profound verse teaches that sleep is a minor form of death — a daily reminder of our mortality. Every night, Allah "takes" the soul in a partial withdrawal from the body. If He decrees death, the soul does not return; if life continues, the soul is released back until its appointed time.

The classical scholars explain there are two types of tawaffi (taking): the major tawaffi (death itself), and the minor tawaffi (sleep). Both are entirely in Allah's control, demonstrating His absolute sovereignty over life and its continuation. This verse is frequently invoked in the du'a before sleep: "In Your name, O Allah, I die and I live."

📚 Ibn Kathir

Ibn Kathir reports that Ibn 'Abbas explained the soul departs during sleep and ascends. The souls of the living and the dead meet, and then only the souls of the living are returned. He notes this is why dreaming of a deceased person may feel so vivid — the souls genuinely meet.

📚 Al-Jalalayn

Al-Jalalayn explain that "takes the souls" means Allah causes them to die fully at the moment of death, and causes a partial cessation of consciousness during sleep. Both demonstrate His complete mastery over existence — neither death nor life escape His will.

📚 As-Sa'di

As-Sa'di beautifully notes that every night a human being experiences a rehearsal for death, yet few reflect on this. He encourages the believer to prepare for both — to go to sleep in a state of dhikr and to live each day as though it may be their last, since the appointed term is known only to Allah.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • 😴 Sleep is a "little death" — a daily reminder that life is temporary and in Allah's hands.
  • 🙏 Sleeping and waking in a state of dhikr (remembrance) is a prophetic Sunnah rooted in this verse.
  • ⏳ Every extra day of life is a gift — the ajal (appointed term) is set, and each morning is a blessing.
  • 🤔 Reflection on sleep and death builds tawakkul (trust in Allah) and motivates righteous action.

The Great Verse of Hope — Do Not Despair of Allah's Mercy

Az-Zumar 39:53–55
قُلْ يَا عِبَادِيَ الَّذِينَ أَسْرَفُوا عَلَىٰ أَنفُسِهِمْ لَا تَقْنَطُوا مِن رَّحْمَةِ اللَّهِ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوبَ جَمِيعًا ۚ إِنَّهُ هُوَ الْغَفُورُ الرَّحِيمُ ۝ وَأَنِيبُوا إِلَىٰ رَبِّكُمْ وَأَسْلِمُوا لَهُ مِن قَبْلِ أَن يَأْتِيَكُمُ الْعَذَابُ ثُمَّ لَا تُنصَرُونَ ۝ وَاتَّبِعُوا أَحْسَنَ مَا أُنزِلَ إِلَيْكُم مِّن رَّبِّكُم
"Say: 'O My servants who have transgressed against themselves [by sinning], do not despair of the mercy of Allah. Indeed, Allah forgives all sins. Indeed, it is He who is the Forgiving, the Merciful.' And return [in repentance] to your Lord and submit to Him before the punishment comes upon you; then you will not be helped. And follow the best of what was revealed to you from your Lord before the punishment comes upon you suddenly while you do not perceive." (39:53–55)

This is widely regarded as the most hope-giving verse in the entire Quran. Ibn Mas'ud رضي الله عنه called it "the most comprehensive verse of relief in the Quran." Notice that Allah addresses the gravest sinners with breathtaking tenderness: "O My servants" — not "O you who have sinned," but My servants. Even in transgression, the bond of servitude and care remains.

The phrase "who have transgressed against themselves" is deeply insightful — sin ultimately harms the sinner. Then comes the declaration that transcends all despair: Allah forgives ALL sins. The scholars emphasise this is for those who repent with sincerity. The only sin that cannot be forgiven is shirk if one dies upon it — and even that is covered if repented from before death.

The condition for receiving this mercy is clear in verse 54: return, and submit, before the punishment comes. The window of repentance is open — but it will close. The urgency is real.

📚 Ibn Kathir

Ibn Kathir records that 'Umar ibn al-Khattab رضي الله عنه said this is the most hopeful verse in the Quran. Ibn Kathir emphasises that this includes even the sin of shirk if the person repents before death, citing other Quranic verses and hadiths that confirm no sincere repentance goes unaccepted.

📚 Al-Jalalayn

Al-Jalalayn note the stylistic tenderness of "O My servants" — the possessive addresses the sinner as still belonging to Allah, still under His care. The completeness of forgiveness ("all sins") is conditioned on sincere tawbah, submission, and following the Quran.

📚 As-Sa'di

As-Sa'di writes that this verse is a direct lifeline to the despairing soul. He explains that qanut (despair of Allah's mercy) is itself a great sin, because it reflects poor opinion of Allah. The believer must always hold good thoughts about Allah — that He is ready and willing to forgive, no matter how heavy the burden of sin.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • 💛 Despair of Allah's mercy is forbidden — this verse is a direct command to maintain hope in Allah's forgiveness.
  • 🌊 Allah's mercy is broader than all human sin combined — no sin is too great for sincere repentance.
  • ⚡ However, repentance must happen before the moment of death — urgency is real and time is limited.
  • 📖 "Follow the best of what was revealed" — the Quran and Sunnah are the path out of sin and into mercy.
  • 🤝 "O My servants" — even in sin, Allah's relationship with the believer is one of ownership, care, and love.

The Regret of the Heedless

Az-Zumar 39:56–59
أَن تَقُولَ نَفْسٌ يَا حَسْرَتَىٰ عَلَىٰ مَا فَرَّطتُ فِي جَنبِ اللَّهِ وَإِن كُنتُ لَمِنَ السَّاخِرِينَ ۝ أَوْ تَقُولَ لَوْ أَنَّ اللَّهَ هَدَانِي لَكُنتُ مِنَ الْمُتَّقِينَ
"Lest a soul should say, 'Oh, [how great is] my regret over what I neglected in regard to Allah and that I was among the mockers.' Or [lest] it say, 'If only Allah had guided me, I would have been among the righteous.'" (39:56–57)

These verses describe the harrowing scene of self-reproach on the Day of Judgment. Notice two types of excuses the soul will make — both futile. The first is genuine remorse: "I fell short in the side of Allah," meaning they neglected His rights, His worship, His obedience. The second is deflection: "If only He had guided me" — blaming Allah for their own choice to mock and reject the message.

The Quran pre-empts these excuses here in this life, so that no soul can claim ignorance on that Day. The message, the guidance, the mercy — all have been presented. The choice to accept or reject is the human being's own.

📚 Ibn Kathir

Ibn Kathir explains "what I neglected in regard to Allah" means neglecting the rights of Allah — worship, obedience, and devotion. The phrase "side of Allah" (janb-Allah) indicates the immense importance of what was lost — not a small thing, but one's relationship with the Most High.

📚 As-Sa'di

As-Sa'di explains the second excuse (blaming Allah for misguidance) will be refuted by the record of deeds — guidance was offered and rejected by their own free choice. The Quran is mentioning these excuses now so that people may avoid making them by acting while time remains.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • ⏰ The regret of the heedless on the Day of Judgment will be immense — and too late.
  • 🙅 Blaming Allah for one's misguidance is a hollow excuse — guidance was available; the choice was ours.
  • 😔 Mocking the truth in this life compounds the tragedy — those who laughed will weep.
  • 📌 Read this verse as a prompt: what am I neglecting "in regard to Allah" right now?

The Trumpet is Blown — The Majesty of Allah

Az-Zumar 39:67–70
وَمَا قَدَرُوا اللَّهَ حَقَّ قَدْرِهِ وَالْأَرْضُ جَمِيعًا قَبْضَتُهُ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ وَالسَّمَاوَاتُ مَطْوِيَّاتٌ بِيَمِينِهِ ۚ سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَىٰ عَمَّا يُشْرِكُونَ ۝ وَنُفِخَ فِي الصُّورِ فَصَعِقَ مَن فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَمَن فِي الْأَرْضِ إِلَّا مَن شَاءَ اللَّهُ ۖ ثُمَّ نُفِخَ فِيهِ أُخْرَىٰ فَإِذَا هُمْ قِيَامٌ يَنظُرُونَ
"They have not appraised Allah with true appraisal, while the entire earth will be in His grip on the Day of Resurrection, and the heavens will be folded in His right hand. Exalted is He and high above what they associate with Him. And the Trumpet will be blown, and whoever is in the heavens and whoever is on the earth will fall dead except whom Allah wills. Then it will be blown again, and at once they will be standing, looking on." (39:67–68)

These are among the most awe-inspiring verses in the Quran — a depiction of Allah's absolute greatness that dwarfs all human imagination. The entire earth, all its mountains, oceans, and continents, will be in Allah's grip like a ball in a hand. The heavens — vast beyond comprehension — will be folded in His right hand. This is the scale of the One we worship.

Then the scene shifts to the Trumpet of Israfil (peace be upon him). The first blow causes every soul — except those Allah wills to spare — to fall dead. The scholars differ on who is spared; many say it is the martyrs, the angels, or those already in a state of death. The second blow resurrects everyone instantly: they stand, looking, bewildered, as the Day of Judgment begins.

📚 Ibn Kathir

Ibn Kathir explains that the hadith of the Prophet ﷺ confirms: "A Jewish rabbi came and said, 'O Muhammad, verily we find that Allah holds the heavens on one finger, the earths on one finger…' and the Prophet ﷺ laughed until his teeth showed, affirming what the rabbi said." This verse thus aligns with authentic prophetic tradition on Allah's attributes, affirmed without likening them to creation.

📚 Al-Jalalayn

Al-Jalalayn state that the phrase "they did not estimate Allah with His true estimation" is a rebuke to the polytheists — and to all who belittle Allah in their imagination, reducing Him to a being whose qualities can be compared to created things.

📚 As-Sa'di

As-Sa'di reflects that this verse is the answer to all arrogance, all heedlessness, and all sin: if people truly understood who Allah is — that the entire cosmos fits in His grip — they would never disobey Him. The problem is we have not "appraised Allah with true appraisal."

🔑 Key Lessons

  • 🌌 The universe is infinitely vast, yet it is nothing in comparison to Allah's greatness.
  • 😤 Much of human sin stems from failure to truly appreciate who Allah is — ma qadaru-Llaha haqqa qadrihi.
  • 📯 The Trumpet is real — death will come collectively and suddenly; resurrection will follow instantly.
  • 🤲 True knowledge of Allah (ma'rifa) leads to proper awe, worship, and avoidance of disobedience.

Hell and Paradise — The Final Verdict

Az-Zumar 39:71–75
وَسِيقَ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا إِلَىٰ جَهَنَّمَ زُمَرًا ۖ حَتَّىٰ إِذَا جَاءُوهَا فُتِحَتْ أَبْوَابُهَا ۖ وَقَالَ لَهُمْ خَزَنَتُهَا أَلَمْ يَأْتِكُمْ رُسُلٌ مِّنكُمْ يَتْلُونَ عَلَيْكُمْ آيَاتِ رَبِّكُمْ وَيُنذِرُونَكُمْ لِقَاءَ يَوْمِكُمْ هَٰذَا ۚ قَالُوا بَلَىٰ وَلَٰكِنْ حَقَّتْ كَلِمَةُ الْعَذَابِ عَلَى الْكَافِرِينَ ۝ وَسِيقَ الَّذِينَ اتَّقَوْا رَبَّهُمْ إِلَى الْجَنَّةِ زُمَرًا
"And those who disbelieved will be driven to Hell in groups until, when they reach it, its gates are opened and its keepers will say, 'Did there not come to you messengers from yourselves, reciting to you the verses of your Lord and warning you of the meeting of this Day of yours?' They will say, 'Yes, but the word of punishment has come into effect upon the disbelievers.' And those who feared their Lord will be driven to Paradise in groups…" (39:71–73)

The surah ends with the two great "processions" — the groups (zumar) that give the surah its name. The disbelievers are driven (sa-qa — a word implying force and urgency) to Hell in waves. When they arrive, the gates swing open as if waiting for them. The keepers — the angels of Hell — ask a final, devastating question: "Didn't the messengers come to you?" The answer is yes. They cannot deny it.

Then the believers arrive at Paradise — also in groups, also driven, but with what dignity! The gates of Paradise open before them and the angels say: "Peace be upon you! You have done well, so enter here to abide eternally." (39:73). They enter praising Allah, saying: "Praise to Allah who has fulfilled His promise to us and given us this land as inheritance."

📚 Ibn Kathir

Ibn Kathir notes that the word "zumar" (groups) indicates that both the righteous and the disbelievers will be sorted and arrive in categories — perhaps by nation, era, level of faith, or deeds. The contrast of their receptions is deliberate: one group faces accusatory questions, the other receives angelic salutations of peace.

📚 As-Sa'di

As-Sa'di emphasises the justice embedded in the question posed by the keepers of Hell — the proof of prophethood is established even at the gates of Hell, so no one can claim unfair treatment. The believers' entry into Paradise is described with the Arabic word for "inheritance" (warthna) — they earned it, and it is truly theirs.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • ⚖️ Hell and Paradise are real destinations — we are all in a procession moving toward one of them.
  • 📣 The Messengers' warnings will be cited as evidence on the Day of Judgment — "Yes, they came to us."
  • 🌿 Paradise is an inheritance — something earned and deserved, not just a gift to be taken lightly.
  • 🎉 The believers' entry into Paradise is marked with peace, honour, and eternal gratitude: "Alhamdulillah."

Ghafir — The Forgiver

غَافِر
📍 Makkah 🔢 85 Verses 📖 Also known as Al-Mu'min (The Believer)

This surah opens the seven "Hawamim" — surahs beginning with the letters Hā Mīm (حم). Its central narrative is the remarkable story of a secret believer from within Pharaoh's court who risked everything to speak truth to power. Key themes include the acceptance of repentance, the power of du'a, and the fate of nations who rejected their messengers.

Opening — The Forgiver, Acceptor of Repentance

Ghafir 40:1–6
حم ۝ تَنزِيلُ الْكِتَابِ مِنَ اللَّهِ الْعَزِيزِ الْعَلِيمِ ۝ غَافِرِ الذَّنبِ وَقَابِلِ التَّوْبِ شَدِيدِ الْعِقَابِ ذِي الطَّوْلِ ۖ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ۖ إِلَيْهِ الْمَصِيرُ
"Ḥā Mīm. The revelation of the Book is from Allah, the Exalted in Might, the Knowing — Forgiver of sin, Acceptor of repentance, Severe in punishment, Owner of abundance. There is no deity except Him; to Him is the destination." (40:1–3)

The surah opens with four divine attributes presented in stunning balance. "Ghafir al-dhanb" (Forgiver of sin) and "Qabil al-tawb" (Acceptor of repentance) are paired with "Shadid al-'iqab" (Severe in punishment) and "Dhi al-tawl" (Owner of abundance/bounty). This is deliberate: Allah is not only merciful but also just; not only forgiving but also capable of retribution. The believer holds both in their heart — hoping in His mercy, fearing His justice.

The name "Ghafir" (The Forgiver) gives the surah its identity and sets its tone. Before the story of Pharaoh's arrogance unfolds, Allah announces His essential nature: He forgives. Even amid scenes of divine punishment, the door of repentance is always mentioned first.

📚 Ibn Kathir

Ibn Kathir distinguishes between "Ghafir al-dhanb" (covers sin, past tense completed) and "Qabil al-tawb" (actively receiving repentance, ongoing). The first forgives the deed already done; the second accepts the turning back of the heart. Both are needed — one for past transgressions, one for the ongoing relationship of return and renewal.

📚 Al-Jalalayn

Al-Jalalayn note that "dhi al-tawl" (Owner of abundance/bounty) refers to Allah's immense gifts and graces — material, spiritual, and beyond. His capacity to forgive is matched by His capacity to bestow. This is an invitation not just to fear, but to recognise what we stand to gain by returning to Him.

📚 As-Sa'di

As-Sa'di writes that these opening attributes are a complete portrait of the relationship between Allah and His servants: He forgives, He accepts return, He rewards abundantly — and only to Him do all affairs ultimately return. This is the foundation upon which the rest of the surah's narrative stands.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • ⚖️ Hold mercy and justice in balance — Allah is both the Forgiver and the Severe in punishment.
  • 🌱 "Acceptor of repentance" is an active, present tense — Allah is always ready to receive our return.
  • 🔚 All things return to Allah — this reality should orient every decision we make.
  • ✨ The name "Ghafir" framing this surah signals: no matter what Pharaoh did, forgiveness was always possible had he chosen it.

The Angels' Intercession — A Du'a for the Believers

Ghafir 40:7–9
الَّذِينَ يَحْمِلُونَ الْعَرْشَ وَمَنْ حَوْلَهُ يُسَبِّحُونَ بِحَمْدِ رَبِّهِمْ وَيُؤْمِنُونَ بِهِ وَيَسْتَغْفِرُونَ لِلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا ۖ رَبَّنَا وَسِعْتَ كُلَّ شَيْءٍ رَّحْمَةً وَعِلْمًا فَاغْفِرْ لِلَّذِينَ تَابُوا وَاتَّبَعُوا سَبِيلَكَ وَقِهِمْ عَذَابَ الْجَحِيمِ
"Those [angels] who carry the Throne and those around it exalt [Allah] with praise of their Lord and believe in Him and ask forgiveness for those who have believed, [saying], 'Our Lord, You have encompassed all things in mercy and knowledge, so forgive those who have repented and followed Your way and protect them from the punishment of Hellfire.'" (40:7)

This is one of the most tender images in the Quran: the mightiest of all angels — those who bear the Throne of the Most High, whose own worship glorifies the heavens — are praying for you. For the believers on earth. For you, if you have repented and followed the path of Allah.

The angels' prayer continues in verse 8–9 asking Allah to admit the believers into the Gardens of Eden, along with whoever was righteous among their parents, spouses, and offspring. This is an extraordinary du'a — one that believers are encouraged to make for themselves, drawing on the very words the angels use.

📚 Ibn Kathir

Ibn Kathir records that the Throne-bearers are among the greatest of creation — of such magnificence that the distance between an earlobe and shoulder of one of them would take 700 years to traverse. Yet these mighty beings spend their time making du'a for the repentant believers on earth. This is a mark of the dignity Allah has given to the people of faith.

📚 As-Sa'di

As-Sa'di highlights the conditions embedded in the angels' du'a: they pray for those who have "repented and followed Your way" — not for sinners who persist in their sins, but for those who turn back and align themselves with the Quran and Sunnah. This teaches the believer that sincere tawbah has cosmic support.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • 👼 The greatest angels are praying for sincere believers — you are not alone in your struggles.
  • 🤲 Learn the angels' du'a and make it yourself: "Our Lord, encompass them in Your mercy and knowledge…"
  • 👨‍👩‍👧 The angels pray for believers' families too — righteous deeds benefit not just us but those we love.
  • ✅ The qualifying condition is tawbah and following the way — these are within our reach.

The Disbelievers in Hell — A Deeper Despair

Ghafir 40:10–12
إِنَّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا يُنَادَوْنَ لَمَقْتُ اللَّهِ أَكْبَرُ مِن مَّقْتِكُمْ أَنفُسَكُمْ إِذْ تُدْعَوْنَ إِلَى الْإِيمَانِ فَتَكْفُرُونَ
"Indeed, those who disbelieve will be addressed [at the time of entry into Hell], 'The hatred of Allah for you was [even] greater than your hatred of yourselves, as you were invited to faith but you refused.'" (40:10)

In the most painful irony, the people of Hell will hate themselves — loathing their past choices. But they are told: even that self-hatred is less than Allah's hatred of them when they rejected faith in this world. The word maqt (hatred/loathing) is the strongest Arabic word for disgust. It is rarely attributed to Allah and its use here emphasises the gravity of rejecting the message.

In verses 11–12 the people of Hell admit twice: "Our Lord, you have caused us to die twice and given us life twice…" This refers to the life of the world, the death at its end, and the resurrection. They beg for a way out, but the gates are closed — because they rejected Allah when He was One and attributed partners to Him.

📚 Ibn Kathir

Ibn Kathir explains that this announcement in Hell is not to cause greater suffering but to make the disbelievers understand the true nature of what they did. They will feel the full weight of their rejection — not just the consequences, but the moral reality of having turned away from the One who called them.

📚 As-Sa'di

As-Sa'di notes the contrast: the believers in Paradise will praise Allah; the disbelievers in Hell will hate themselves. The believer's journey ends in praise; the rejecter's journey ends in self-loathing. The difference between these two destinations begins with the choice made in this brief world.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • 😔 The people of Hell will hate themselves — let that awareness motivate accountability now, not later.
  • 📢 Rejecting the call to faith when it comes is among the gravest acts — the invitation was clear.
  • 🚪 The time to accept the call is this life — after death, the doors of opportunity are sealed.
  • 🤍 Contrast this with the believer's end: gratitude and praise. Choose the ending you want.

Musa and Pharaoh — Truth Confronts Tyranny

Ghafir 40:23–27
وَلَقَدْ أَرْسَلْنَا مُوسَىٰ بِآيَاتِنَا وَسُلْطَانٍ مُّبِينٍ ۝ إِلَىٰ فِرْعَوْنَ وَهَامَانَ وَقَارُونَ فَقَالُوا سَاحِرٌ كَذَّابٌ ۝ وَقَالَ فِرْعَوْنُ ذَرُونِي أَقْتُلْ مُوسَىٰ وَلْيَدْعُ رَبَّهُ ۖ إِنِّي أَخَافُ أَن يُبَدِّلَ دِينَكُمْ أَوْ أَن يُظْهِرَ فِي الْأَرْضِ الْفَسَادَ
"And We did certainly send Moses with Our signs and a clear authority — to Pharaoh and Haman and Qarun; but they said, 'A sorcerer and a liar.' And Pharaoh said, 'Let me kill Moses and let him call upon his Lord. Indeed, I fear that he will change your religion or that he will cause corruption in the land.'" (40:23–26)

Three figures of oppression are named: Pharaoh (political tyranny), Haman (religious/governmental power), and Qarun (economic arrogance). They represent the three faces of worldly corruption that consistently oppose prophetic messages. Musa alayhissalam came with signs (miracles) and clear proof — yet they dismissed him as a sorcerer and liar.

Pharaoh's rhetoric is recognisable across history: "I fear he will change your religion or cause corruption." He frames the prophet as the dangerous one — using fear as a political tool to maintain control. This inversion of truth and falsehood is the hallmark of tyranny. The one speaking truth is accused of spreading corruption.

📚 Ibn Kathir

Ibn Kathir notes that Pharaoh's statement "let me kill Moses and let him call upon his Lord" was said with a contemptuous challenge — as if to say, "Let's see if his God saves him." This arrogance was deliberate, designed to undermine the faith of the people in Musa. But it is precisely this moment that the believing man in Pharaoh's court chose to speak.

📚 As-Sa'di

As-Sa'di reflects that tyrants always claim their oppression serves the people — Pharaoh dressed his murderous intent as concern for religion and public order. When those in power use these justifications against truth, it is a sign of their weakness, not strength. Truth does not need political protection; falsehood does.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • 🏛️ Tyranny often comes in three forms: political power, religious manipulation, and economic control.
  • 🔄 Those who speak truth are historically accused of causing "corruption" by those who actually do.
  • 💪 The prophetic mission continues regardless of threats — Musa did not stop because Pharaoh threatened him.
  • 🌍 History repeats: every generation has a Pharaoh, a Haman, and a Qarun — and every era needs its Musa.

The Believing Man of Pharaoh's Family — Courage of a Hidden Believer

Ghafir 40:28–35
وَقَالَ رَجُلٌ مُّؤْمِنٌ مِّنْ آلِ فِرْعَوْنَ يَكْتُمُ إِيمَانَهُ أَتَقْتُلُونَ رَجُلًا أَن يَقُولَ رَبِّيَ اللَّهُ وَقَدْ جَاءَكُم بِالْبَيِّنَاتِ مِن رَّبِّكُمْ ۖ وَإِن يَكُ كَاذِبًا فَعَلَيْهِ كَذِبُهُ ۖ وَإِن يَكُ صَادِقًا يُصِبْكُم بَعْضُ الَّذِي يَعِدُكُمْ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَا يَهْدِي مَنْ هُوَ مُسْرِفٌ كَذَّابٌ
"And a believing man from the family of Pharaoh who concealed his faith said, 'Do you kill a man [merely] because he says, "My Lord is Allah," while he has brought you clear proofs from your Lord? And if he should be lying, then upon him is [the consequence of] his lie; but if he should be truthful, there will strike you some of what he promises you. Indeed, Allah does not guide one who is a transgressor and a liar.'" (40:28)

One of the most heroic figures in the Quran steps forward here — an unnamed believer, hidden within Pharaoh's own court, who had concealed his faith for years. At the critical moment when Musa's life is threatened, he speaks. This single act of courage consumes eight verses (28–45) — one of the longest recorded speeches by a non-prophet in the Quran.

His argument is brilliantly logical and restrained: "If he's a liar, his lie is on him. If he's truthful, some of what he warned will hit you." This is a rational, calm appeal to self-interest — not a theological sermon. He avoids revealing his own faith initially, appealing first to reason, then gradually revealing his beliefs.

In verse 29 he reminds Pharaoh's court of the previous nation of Egypt's power — and their destruction. In verse 30 he reveals more: "I fear for you a day like those past nations." In verse 31 he invokes Yusuf (Joseph), who came before with signs, and whose message was also rejected. By verse 35, he is fully committed, openly warning them against pride before Allah's revelations.

📚 Ibn Kathir

Ibn Kathir records several opinions on who this man was. Some say he was a nephew of Pharaoh, others say a senior official named Sham'un or Kharbil. His identity is kept anonymous by Allah perhaps to make his example universal — there is always a believer even in the court of tyrants, and Allah honours that person without needing to name him.

📚 Al-Jalalayn

Al-Jalalayn explain that his concealment of faith (yaktumu imanaahu) was not cowardice but strategic — he preserved his access to power so that when the critical moment came, he could intervene. His timing shows wisdom: he waited until the stakes were highest, then stepped forward with full commitment.

📚 As-Sa'di

As-Sa'di draws the lesson that faith can exist in the most unexpected places — even in Pharaoh's household. And he notes the man's rhetorical wisdom: he began with a logical argument that even a disbeliever could accept, then escalated to a moral and spiritual appeal. This is a model for calling to truth wisely.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • 🦁 Speaking truth at the court of a tyrant is one of the highest acts of courage — the believing man is a model.
  • 🧠 Wise da'wah begins with reason and logic before moving to faith — appeal to what people already know.
  • 🌱 Faith can grow and survive even in the most hostile environments — even Pharaoh's household had a believer.
  • ⏳ Strategic patience (concealing faith when necessary) is not betrayal — it is wisdom, followed by courage at the right moment.
  • 🕊️ Allah does not name him — making the believer's example belong to every era and every hidden believer in history.

The Believer's Warning Continues — And Allah Saves Him

Ghafir 40:36–45
وَقَالَ الَّذِي آمَنَ يَا قَوْمِ اتَّبِعُونِ أَهْدِكُمْ سَبِيلَ الرَّشَادِ ۝ يَا قَوْمِ إِنَّمَا هَٰذِهِ الْحَيَاةُ الدُّنْيَا مَتَاعٌ وَإِنَّ الْآخِرَةَ هِيَ دَارُ الْقَرَارِ ۝ فَوَقَاهُ اللَّهُ سَيِّئَاتِ مَا مَكَرُوا
"And the one who believed said, 'O my people, follow me; I will guide you to the way of right conduct. O my people, this worldly life is only [temporary] enjoyment, and indeed, the Hereafter — that is the home of [permanent] settlement… And Allah protected him from the evils of what they plotted…'" (40:38–39, 45)

The believing man's speech reaches its emotional peak: he calls his people to follow him and openly states the most fundamental of Islamic truths — this dunya is just temporary enjoyment; the akhirah is the real home. He is speaking in the court of Pharaoh, the man who claimed to be God. The bravery required cannot be overstated.

In verses 40–41 he issues a profound comparison: those in Hell receive nothing beyond what they earned; those in Paradise are given more than what they deserved. He pleads: "O my people, how is it that I call you to salvation while you call me to the Fire?"

The climax comes in verse 45: despite all of Pharaoh's scheming and plots against this man, Allah protected him. The Arabic is emphatic — "fa-waqahu" — Allah shielded him, deflected every harm. The story doesn't say he fled or fought his way out; Allah simply protected him. This is the promise for those who speak truth for His sake.

📚 Ibn Kathir

Ibn Kathir records that after his speech, the believing man escaped from Pharaoh's reach — some narrations say he left Egypt entirely. The point of verse 45 is that however Allah protected him, it was complete — Pharaoh, with all his power, could not harm the man who stood for Allah.

📚 As-Sa'di

As-Sa'di notes the contrast built into these verses: the believing man asks "How are you calling me to the Fire when I am calling you to salvation?" This rhetorical reversal is the ultimate response to those who frame belief as harmful — the one calling to truth is saving lives; the one rejecting it is the true danger.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • 🌏 "This dunya is only temporary enjoyment" — one of the most clarifying truths for every anxious and distracted heart.
  • 🏡 The akhirah is "dar al-qarar" — the permanent dwelling. Everything in this life is transit accommodation.
  • 🛡️ Allah's protection for those who stand for truth is real: "fa-waqahu" — He shielded him completely.
  • 🔄 The believer's da'wah reverses the oppressor's framing: "I am saving you; you are calling me to destruction."

Pharaoh's Family — Exposed to the Fire Morning and Evening

Ghafir 40:46
النَّارُ يُعْرَضُونَ عَلَيْهَا غُدُوًّا وَعَشِيًّا ۖ وَيَوْمَ تَقُومُ السَّاعَةُ أَدْخِلُوا آلَ فِرْعَوْنَ أَشَدَّ الْعَذَابِ
"The Fire — they are exposed to it morning and evening. And the Day the Hour appears [it will be said], 'Make the people of Pharaoh enter the severest punishment.'" (40:46)

This verse is the primary Quranic evidence for 'adhab al-qabr — the punishment of the grave. Pharaoh and his people, between their death and the Day of Judgment, are exposed to the Fire twice daily. This is not yet the final punishment — that comes when the Hour arrives. The punishment in the grave is real, ongoing, and proportionate to the crimes committed in this life.

The contrast with the believing man could not be sharper: he was protected in this life (verse 45), while Pharaoh is tormented between death and the Day of Judgment (verse 46), and will face the severest punishment at the final reckoning (also verse 46). Three phases of punishment for the greatest arrogance; three phases of protection and reward for sincere belief.

📚 Ibn Kathir

Ibn Kathir uses this verse as one of the key proofs for the punishment and blessing of the grave (barzakh). He cites hadiths in which the Prophet ﷺ confirmed the punishment of the grave is real, and that the people of Pharaoh are a specific example Allah gave us of those who suffer it most severely.

📚 As-Sa'di

As-Sa'di notes the dual punishment: exposure to fire during the barzakh (interval between death and resurrection), then entry into the severest fire at the Day of Judgment. This is a sobering reminder that death is not an escape from consequences — accountability begins the moment the soul departs the body.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • ⚰️ The punishment of the grave is real and Quranic — confirmed by this verse and authentic hadiths.
  • 📅 Every morning and evening, Pharaoh's family faces the fire — a daily, relentless consequence.
  • ⚡ Death is not an escape — the barzakh begins immediately and is shaped by what we did in this life.
  • 🙏 Seek refuge from 'adhab al-qabr regularly; the Prophet ﷺ taught us to do so in every prayer.

Allah's Promise — We Will Help Our Messengers and Believers

Ghafir 40:51–54
إِنَّا لَنَنصُرُ رُسُلَنَا وَالَّذِينَ آمَنُوا فِي الْحَيَاةِ الدُّنْيَا وَيَوْمَ يَقُومُ الْأَشْهَادُ ۝ يَوْمَ لَا يَنفَعُ الظَّالِمِينَ مَعْذِرَتُهُمْ ۖ وَلَهُمُ اللَّعْنَةُ وَلَهُمْ سُوءُ الدَّارِ
"Indeed, We will support Our messengers and those who have believed during the life of this world and on the Day when the witnesses will stand — the Day their excuse will not benefit the wrongdoers, and they will have the curse and they will have the worst home." (40:51–52)

This is one of the most reassuring divine promises in the Quran: Inna la-nansuru ruslana — We will most certainly support Our messengers. The emphasis particle "inna" and the emphatic lam (la-nansuru) make this a solemn, unbreakable divine oath. Support may not always look like immediate worldly victory — Musa was exiled, the Prophet ﷺ was driven from Makkah — but the ultimate victory, in this life and the next, belongs to the believers.

The "witnesses" (ashad) on the Day when they stand refers either to the angels, or the prophets who witness for their nations, or both. On that Day, excuses of the oppressors are worthless. The promise of Allah is fulfilled comprehensively.

📚 Ibn Kathir

Ibn Kathir explains that Allah's support sometimes comes in this world (as with the early Muslim victory after years of persecution) and always comes in the next. He notes this verse was a comfort to the early Muslims who were suffering greatly and needed to know that divine support was guaranteed — the timing was in Allah's wisdom, not theirs.

📚 As-Sa'di

As-Sa'di writes that "support" (nasr) includes: establishing the religion, making the truth victorious, protecting the believers' honour, and granting them victory over their enemies. This support is not conditional on our weakness or strength — it is Allah's promise, and Allah does not break His promise.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • 🤝 Allah's support of the believers is a divine promise — not dependent on their worldly power or numbers.
  • ⏳ Support may be delayed in this world but it is guaranteed — patience is the believer's requirement.
  • 🚫 No excuse will help the oppressor on the Day of Judgment — history always ends with justice.
  • 💫 "Nasr" encompasses honour, protection, truth-establishment, and final victory — all of these are promised.

The Du'a Verse — Call Upon Me and I Will Respond

Ghafir 40:60
وَقَالَ رَبُّكُمُ ادْعُونِي أَسْتَجِبْ لَكُمْ ۚ إِنَّ الَّذِينَ يَسْتَكْبِرُونَ عَنْ عِبَادَتِي سَيَدْخُلُونَ جَهَنَّمَ دَاخِرِينَ
"And your Lord says, 'Call upon Me; I will respond to you.' Indeed, those who disdain My worship will enter Hell rendered contemptible." (40:60)

This is the pinnacle verse on du'a in the entire Quran. Allah Himself — without intermediary — commands us to call upon Him and promises personally to respond. The Arabic is in the imperative and the conditional response is direct: ud'uni astajib lakum — "Call upon Me, I will respond to you." This is an open, unconditional invitation from the Lord of all creation.

The second half is striking: those who are "too arrogant" (yastakbirun) to call upon Allah will enter Hell humiliated. Not those who sinned, but those who were too proud to pray and make du'a. Scholars explain that du'a is 'ibadah (worship), so refusing to call upon Allah is a form of arrogance — implying self-sufficiency from the One who sustains all existence.

📚 Ibn Kathir

Ibn Kathir records a hadith that the Prophet ﷺ said: "Du'a is worship itself," then recited this very verse. He emphasises that the response from Allah takes many forms — it may be the exact thing asked for, or something better, or the removal of a harm equivalent to the blessing requested. The response is always real, even if not always visible.

📚 Al-Jalalayn

Al-Jalalayn note that "call upon Me" encompasses both du'a (supplication) and 'ibadah (worship) — they are inseparable. To worship Allah sincerely is to call upon Him; to call upon Him sincerely is the highest form of worship. Abandoning either is abandoning the other.

📚 As-Sa'di

As-Sa'di reflects that this verse is a mercy beyond measure. That the infinite Allah specifically invites the finite servant to call upon Him — and promises to respond — should fill the believer with awe and urgency. He encourages making du'a in all states: in ease, in difficulty, in gratitude, and in need.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • 🤲 Du'a is not optional — it is an act of worship, and abandoning it is arrogance before Allah.
  • ✅ Allah promises to respond — not sometimes, always. The form of response may vary, but it is always real.
  • 🙅 Self-sufficiency (feeling too busy or too rational for du'a) is a spiritual disease, not a strength.
  • 🌙 This verse is especially powerful in Ramadan — the month when du'a is most intensely answered.
  • 💬 Make du'a in ease, not just difficulty — calling upon Allah when things are good builds the relationship for when they are not.

The Blessings of Allah — Your Form, Your Provision

Ghafir 40:64–68
اللَّهُ الَّذِي جَعَلَ لَكُمُ الْأَرْضَ قَرَارًا وَالسَّمَاءَ بِنَاءً وَصَوَّرَكُمْ فَأَحْسَنَ صُوَرَكُمْ وَرَزَقَكُم مِّنَ الطَّيِّبَاتِ ۚ ذَٰلِكُمُ اللَّهُ رَبُّكُمْ ۖ فَتَبَارَكَ اللَّهُ رَبُّ الْعَالَمِينَ
"It is Allah who made for you the earth a place of settlement and the sky a ceiling and formed you and perfected your forms and provided for you from the good things. That is Allah, your Lord; then blessed is Allah, Lord of the worlds." (40:64)

After the scenes of judgment, Hell, and confrontation with Pharaoh, these verses return to gratitude — enumerating the basic blessings that surround every human being. The earth as a stable home, the sky as a structure (a canopy protecting and providing), the human form shaped in the best of proportions, and sustenance from wholesome things. These are not small gifts.

"Sawwarakum fa-ahsana suwarakum" — He formed you and perfected your forms. The human body is an ayah (sign) of divine care and artistry. Even the Quran draws our attention to our own creation as evidence of Allah's existence and wisdom.

📚 Ibn Kathir

Ibn Kathir notes that the verse ends: "Blessed is Allah, Lord of the worlds" — a declaration of His infinite bounty. He explains that the enumeration of blessings in this verse is a gentle rebuke to ingratitude: "This is what He gave you — why then do you refuse to worship Him alone?"

📚 As-Sa'di

As-Sa'di emphasises verse 65: "He is the Ever-Living; there is no deity except Him, so call upon Him with sincere devotion to Him." After listing the blessings, Allah draws the conclusion: call upon the One who gave you all of this, with pure hearts. Gratitude and du'a are the natural responses to recognising these gifts.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • 🌏 The earth, sky, your own body, your daily food — all of these are blessings from Allah deserving gratitude.
  • 🙏 Gratitude for blessings naturally leads to du'a — both are responses of the awake heart.
  • 🌟 "He perfected your forms" — your body is a divine gift; treat it with respect and care.
  • ☀️ "Blessed is Allah, Lord of the worlds" — let this be a regular phrase on your tongue, especially when you notice a blessing.

Previous Messengers and the Consequence of Rejection

Ghafir 40:78–85
وَلَقَدْ أَرْسَلْنَا رُسُلًا مِّن قَبْلِكَ مِنْهُم مَّن قَصَصْنَا عَلَيْكَ وَمِنْهُم مَّن لَّمْ نَقْصُصْ عَلَيْكَ ۗ وَمَا كَانَ لِرَسُولٍ أَن يَأْتِيَ بِآيَةٍ إِلَّا بِإِذْنِ اللَّهِ
"And We have already sent messengers before you. Among them are those [whose stories] We have related to you, and among them are those [whose stories] We have not related to you. And it was not for any messenger to bring a sign [or verse] except by permission of Allah…" (40:78)

The Quran closes Surah Ghafir by widening the lens: prophethood is a continuous thread through human history. Some messengers are named in the Quran; many are not. But all carried the same essential message, and all faced similar rejection. This universalises the experience of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ — he is not an anomaly, but the culmination of a long, patient prophetic mission.

Verses 83–85 then deliver one of the most sobering observations in the Quran: when the punishment of past nations arrived, they believed — but that belief was too late to benefit them. Faith that comes only when destruction is at the door is not accepted. This is why the invitation must be accepted while there is still time, while choices can still be made freely.

📚 Ibn Kathir

Ibn Kathir reflects on the phrase "those We have not related to you" — indicating that there were countless prophets Allah sent to peoples across the earth, whose names and stories were not included in the Quran. The diversity of the prophetic mission is itself a mercy: no people was left without guidance.

📚 As-Sa'di

As-Sa'di focuses on the final verses: the acceptance of faith at the moment of punishment is universally rejected across all past nations. This is the Sunnah of Allah (His consistent way) that does not change. The lesson: accept the truth while the door is open — before the punishment arrives and belief becomes forced, not free.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • 🌍 Prophethood spanned all human civilisations — no people was left without a messenger or warning.
  • ⏰ Deathbed or punishment-fear belief comes too late — faith must be chosen freely while life permits.
  • 🔄 The "Sunnah of Allah" (His fixed pattern in history) does not change — nations that reject their prophets face the same fate.
  • 📚 The Prophet ﷺ is the final link in a long chain — his message is both a completion and a final mercy.
Surah 41 · Makkan · 54 Verses

Fussilat — Explained in Detail

فُصِّلَت
📍 Makkah 🔢 54 Verses total 📖 Juz 24 covers verses 1–46 ☪️ Contains a Sajdah at verse 38

Fussilat (also known as Hā Mīm As-Sajdah) means "Explained in Detail" — referring to the Quran's clear, thorough exposition of guidance. It responds to the Quraysh's rejection of the Prophet ﷺ with powerful proofs from creation, the example of past nations, and a description of what the Quran truly is. It contains one of the Quran's fifteen prostration (sajdah) verses.

Opening — A Book from the Wise, the Praiseworthy

Fussilat 41:1–5
حم ۝ تَنزِيلٌ مِّنَ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ ۝ كِتَابٌ فُصِّلَتْ آيَاتُهُ قُرْآنًا عَرَبِيًّا لِّقَوْمٍ يَعْلَمُونَ ۝ بَشِيرًا وَنَذِيرًا فَأَعْرَضَ أَكْثَرُهُمْ فَهُمْ لَا يَسْمَعُونَ
"Ḥā Mīm. A revelation from the Most Merciful, the Especially Merciful — a Book whose verses have been detailed, an Arabic Quran for a people who know, as a giver of good tidings and a warner; but most of them turn away, so they do not hear." (41:1–4)

The surah opens by identifying the Quran with three qualities: it comes from the Most Merciful (al-Rahman al-Rahim), its verses are "fussilat" — explained in full detail, made clear, distinguished — and it is in Arabic for those who can understand. The mercy of Allah is the first attribute — even warning and accountability flow from divine compassion, not from anger.

Then the tragedy is stated plainly: most people turn away and stop listening. Not because the message is unclear — it is detailed and explained. Not because it lacks good news or warning — it has both. But because turning toward it requires willingness, humility, and the courage to change.

📚 Ibn Kathir

Ibn Kathir explains "fussilat ayatuhu" as meaning the Quran's verses have been made distinct and clear — each ayah addressing its subject fully, with proofs, stories, commands, and consolations all detailed (mufassalah). Nothing essential was left vague or incomplete.

📚 As-Sa'di

As-Sa'di notes that "for a people who know" does not exclude the unlearned — it means Arabic speakers can understand it directly, while others can access it through translation and learning. The Quran speaks to those who bring their minds and hearts, whatever their background.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • 📖 The Quran is fully detailed — nothing essential is missing. Our confusion often comes from not returning to it.
  • 💛 Even the warning in the Quran comes from "al-Rahman al-Rahim" — compassion drives every message.
  • 👂 "They do not hear" — the barrier to guidance is usually not lack of information, but lack of listening.
  • 🌐 The Quran was revealed in Arabic but its guidance is for all humanity who approach it with sincerity.

The Six Days of Creation — Earth and Heavens

Fussilat 41:9–12
قُلْ أَئِنَّكُمْ لَتَكْفُرُونَ بِالَّذِي خَلَقَ الْأَرْضَ فِي يَوْمَيْنِ وَتَجْعَلُونَ لَهُ أَندَادًا ۚ ذَٰلِكَ رَبُّ الْعَالَمِينَ ۝ وَجَعَلَ فِيهَا رَوَاسِيَ مِن فَوْقِهَا وَبَارَكَ فِيهَا وَقَدَّرَ فِيهَا أَقْوَاتَهَا فِي أَرْبَعَةِ أَيَّامٍ
"Say, 'Do you indeed disbelieve in He who created the earth in two days and attribute to Him equals? That is the Lord of the worlds.' And He placed on the earth firmly set mountains over it and blessed it and determined therein its sustenance in four days without distinction — for [the information] of those who ask." (41:9–10)

These verses describe the creation of the earth in two "days" (ayyam), then the placing of mountains, blessings, and setting of provisions over four "days" total (i.e., the additional two). Then in verse 11–12, the creation of the seven heavens from smoke (dukhan) is described — each heaven commanded into completion. The total comes to six "days" of creation, consistent with other Quranic references.

The "days" here are understood by scholars as periods or phases of divine creative activity — not necessarily 24-hour days, as the sun itself did not yet exist. The purpose is to convey the orderliness, deliberateness, and purposeful structuring of all creation.

📚 Ibn Kathir

Ibn Kathir clarifies that the four-day count includes the original two — i.e., two days for the earth's creation, then two additional days for its provisions, totalling four. He cites Ibn 'Abbas who explained that the "days" of creation are long epochs known only to Allah, and that the sequential creation demonstrates Allah's wisdom in establishing order, not necessity (He could have created everything instantaneously).

📚 As-Sa'di

As-Sa'di focuses on the phrase "determined therein its sustenance" — everything that any living being will ever need is already determined and placed within the earth by the Creator. The earth's resources, its fertility, its sustaining capacity — all pre-planned. No creature will starve for lack of divine provision; they may suffer from human injustice, but not divine neglect.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • 🌍 Creation was ordered, deliberate, and phased — not accidental. Every element of the earth was planned.
  • 🍃 Your provision (rizq) was determined before you were born — trust Allah's plan for your sustenance.
  • 🌌 The heavens were created from "dukhan" (smoke/matter) — a compelling convergence with modern cosmological models.
  • 🙅 Assigning equals to Allah who created all of this is the height of ingratitude and illogic.

'Aad and Thamud — When Arrogance Meets Its Limit

Fussilat 41:13–16
فَإِنْ أَعْرَضُوا فَقُلْ أَنذَرْتُكُمْ صَاعِقَةً مِّثْلَ صَاعِقَةِ عَادٍ وَثَمُودَ ۝ إِذْ جَاءَتْهُمُ الرُّسُلُ مِن بَيْنِ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمِنْ خَلْفِهِمْ أَلَّا تَعْبُدُوا إِلَّا اللَّهَ
"But if they turn away, then say, 'I have warned you of a thunderbolt like the thunderbolt [that struck] 'Aad and Thamud.' When the messengers had come to them before them and after them, [saying], 'Worship not except Allah'…" (41:13–14)

'Aad was the mighty civilisation given extraordinary physical strength and power — they asked arrogantly: "Who is stronger than us?" (41:15). Thamud carved their homes from mountains. Both civilisations had messengers sent to them with the same essential message, and both rejected them out of pride. The punishment that followed was comprehensive and total.

The Quran mentions these nations repeatedly not for historical interest alone, but as living warnings. The Quraysh, who rejected the Prophet ﷺ, were being told: you think your civilisation is permanent? So did 'Aad. You think your power protects you? So did Thamud. Look at what remains of them.

📚 Ibn Kathir

Ibn Kathir notes that 'Aad's pride was specifically in their physical might — they believed no power on earth could touch them. Allah sent the wind against them for seven nights and eight days continuously. The wind Allah owns answered the arrogance of those who thought they owned their own strength.

📚 As-Sa'di

As-Sa'di reflects: the messengers came to 'Aad and Thamud "before them and after them" — from all sides, from all angles. There was no gap in the prophetic message reaching them. Their destruction was thus complete justice, not a mistake. When guidance is comprehensively rejected, consequences are comprehensive.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • 💨 Physical strength, civilisational power, and architectural achievement — none of it protects against Allah's decree.
  • 🏚️ Visit the ruins of past nations with contemplative eyes — the Quran asks us to "travel the earth and see."
  • 🙅 Pride in one's own strength ("who is stronger than us?") is the beginning of 'Aad's fall — and a warning for all.
  • 📣 Messengers came from all directions — when guidance is repeatedly rejected, the proof is complete.

Angels Descend — "Do Not Fear and Do Not Grieve"

Fussilat 41:30–32
إِنَّ الَّذِينَ قَالُوا رَبُّنَا اللَّهُ ثُمَّ اسْتَقَامُوا تَتَنَزَّلُ عَلَيْهِمُ الْمَلَائِكَةُ أَلَّا تَخَافُوا وَلَا تَحْزَنُوا وَأَبْشِرُوا بِالْجَنَّةِ الَّتِي كُنتُمْ تُوعَدُونَ ۝ نَحْنُ أَوْلِيَاؤُكُمْ فِي الْحَيَاةِ الدُّنْيَا وَفِي الْآخِرَةِ
"Indeed, those who have said, 'Our Lord is Allah,' and then remained on a right course — the angels will descend upon them, [saying], 'Do not fear and do not grieve but receive good tidings of Paradise, which you were promised. We were your allies in worldly life and [are so] in the Hereafter.'" (41:30–31)

This is one of the most comforting passages in the entire Quran. Two conditions are given for receiving the angels' visitation: saying "Our Lord is Allah" (tawhid — sincere monotheism) and "remaining steadfast" (istiqamah — consistency, uprightness in practice). The angels then deliver three gifts: freedom from fear (of the future), freedom from grief (over the past), and glad tidings of Paradise.

The scholars differ on when the angels descend — some say it is at death, when the dying believer is reassured. Others say it is throughout life, as angels accompany and support the steadfast believer. Most hold that it applies at both times. The angels' statement "We were your allies in worldly life" suggests the latter — that the believer was never truly alone.

📚 Ibn Kathir

Ibn Kathir explains that "istaqamu" (remained steadfast) means they did not deviate after declaring their faith — they fulfilled the rights of their declaration through prayer, obedience, and avoidance of forbidden things. Mere verbal declaration is insufficient; it must be backed by a life of alignment.

📚 Al-Jalalayn

Al-Jalalayn explain "do not fear" refers to what lies ahead, and "do not grieve" refers to what has been left behind. At the moment of death, the believer is thus freed from both anxiety about the future and regret about the past. Only the present mercy of Allah remains.

📚 As-Sa'di

As-Sa'di says the angels serve as wilayah (protectors/allies) for the believer throughout life — defending them from shaytan, recording their good deeds, and making du'a for them. And at death they are the ones who receive the believer with peace and glad tidings. The believer is accompanied from beginning to end.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • 🗣️ Tawhid + Istiqamah = the two pillars that earn the companionship and support of the angels.
  • 😌 Freedom from fear and grief is a divine gift — not stoicism, but real spiritual liberation through faith.
  • 👼 The angels are allies to the believer in this life — you are not fighting your battles alone.
  • 🌅 The dying believer is met with comfort and glad tidings — death is not the end, but a welcome.

The Best Speech — Da'wah and Repelling Evil

Fussilat 41:33–36
وَمَنْ أَحْسَنُ قَوْلًا مِّمَّن دَعَا إِلَى اللَّهِ وَعَمِلَ صَالِحًا وَقَالَ إِنَّنِي مِنَ الْمُسْلِمِينَ ۝ وَلَا تَسْتَوِي الْحَسَنَةُ وَلَا السَّيِّئَةُ ۚ ادْفَعْ بِالَّتِي هِيَ أَحْسَنُ فَإِذَا الَّذِي بَيْنَكَ وَبَيْنَهُ عَدَاوَةٌ كَأَنَّهُ وَلِيٌّ حَمِيمٌ
"And who is better in speech than one who invites to Allah and does righteousness and says, 'Indeed, I am of the Muslims.' And not equal are the good deed and the bad. Repel [evil] by that [deed] which is better; and thereupon the one between you and him is enmity [will become] as though he was a devoted friend." (41:33–34)

The Quran identifies the best human speech: calling to Allah (da'wah), backing it with righteous deeds, and identifying openly with Islam. These three are inseparable — words alone without deeds ring hollow; deeds without identification with the truth community are incomplete; and identification without action is hypocrisy.

Then a profound social and spiritual strategy is given: repel evil with what is better. Not with equal evil (revenge), not with silence (passivity), but with something better. The result is astonishing: enemies can become close friends. This is the transformative power of good character and generous response — it disarms hostility in ways that confrontation never could.

📚 Ibn Kathir

Ibn Kathir explains "repel evil with what is better" means: when someone harms you, respond with kindness; when someone is ignorant toward you, respond with forbearance; when someone cuts you off, maintain ties. This is the prophetic character — al-khuluq al-'azim described in the Quran — applied to daily human conflict.

📚 Al-Jalalayn

Al-Jalalayn note that the transformation of an enemy into "a devoted, warm friend" (wali hamim) is one of the most dramatic social miracles possible — and the Quran says it happens through responding to evil with good. This is not naivety; it is a higher wisdom about human hearts and how they open.

📚 As-Sa'di

As-Sa'di adds that verse 35 clarifies this quality belongs to "those of great fortune" — it is not easy, and not everyone achieves it. Restraining anger, forgiving, and responding with goodness require spiritual maturity that must be actively cultivated. It is a sign of the highest level of Islamic character.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • 🗣️ Da'wah + good deeds + Muslim identity = the best speech. All three together, not just one.
  • 🔁 Responding to evil with better (not equal) evil is the Quranic method of conflict resolution.
  • 🤝 Enemies can become friends — but only through genuine goodness, not mere tolerance.
  • 💪 This character trait belongs to "those of great fortune" — it takes spiritual strength, not weakness.

Signs of Allah — The Sajdah Verse ۩

Fussilat 41:37–38
وَمِنْ آيَاتِهِ اللَّيْلُ وَالنَّهَارُ وَالشَّمْسُ وَالْقَمَرُ ۚ لَا تَسْجُدُوا لِلشَّمْسِ وَلَا لِلْقَمَرِ وَاسْجُدُوا لِلَّهِ الَّذِي خَلَقَهُنَّ إِن كُنتُمْ إِيَّاهُ تَعْبُدُونَ ۝ فَإِنِ اسْتَكْبَرُوا فَالَّذِينَ عِندَ رَبِّكَ يُسَبِّحُونَ لَهُ بِاللَّيْلِ وَالنَّهَارِ وَهُمْ لَا يَسْأَمُونَ ۩
"And of His signs are the night and day and the sun and moon. Do not prostrate to the sun or to the moon, but prostrate to Allah, who created them, if it should be Him that you worship. But if they are arrogant — then those near your Lord exalt Him by night and by day, and they do not tire." (41:37–38) ۩

This passage contains a powerful logical argument: the sun and moon are signs of Allah — not objects to be worshipped, but pointers toward the One who made them. The Quran redirects the impulse of awe and wonder — which is natural and correct — toward its proper target: the Creator of these things, not the things themselves.

Verse 38 contains the Sajdah al-Tilawah (prostration of recitation). When a reciter reaches this verse, both reciter and listener perform a prostration as an act of worship. The verse itself invites prostration — commanding it while the angels who do not tire are already in perpetual glorification. If we refuse to prostrate, the angels still glorify without ceasing. The One being worshipped loses nothing from our refusal; we lose everything.

📚 Ibn Kathir

Ibn Kathir explains that the context of this verse was partly in response to pre-Islamic Arab sun and star worship, but its message is universal. The natural human response to the beauty of the sky — wonder, reverence, awe — is correct; but it must be directed to Allah who made the sky, not to the sky itself. The sign points; it should not be mistaken for the destination.

📚 Al-Jalalayn

Al-Jalalayn note that the sajdah at verse 38's end is the occasion for prostration in recitation — a mark of submission at precisely the moment the verse speaks of angels who prostrate without tiring. The worshipper joins, even for a moment, in that cosmic act of glorification.

📚 As-Sa'di

As-Sa'di reflects that if humanity refuses to prostrate, the loss is entirely theirs — the angels "do not tire" in their glorification. Allah's worship is not diminished by human arrogance; only the arrogant ones are diminished. This is a reminder that our submission benefits us, not Him.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • 🌙 The sun, moon, day, and night are signs pointing to Allah — let their beauty increase your worship.
  • 🕌 Sajdah (prostration) is the highest act of submission — perform it with presence and awareness, not habit.
  • 👼 The angels glorify Allah night and day without tiring — our few moments of worship are nothing in comparison, yet immensely valued by Allah.
  • ⚡ Arrogance against prostration to Allah harms only the arrogant — Allah's glory is unaffected.

The Quran — A Mighty Book of Guidance Across All Times

Fussilat 41:41–46
إِنَّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا بِالذِّكْرِ لَمَّا جَاءَهُمْ ۖ وَإِنَّهُ لَكِتَابٌ عَزِيزٌ ۝ لَّا يَأْتِيهِ الْبَاطِلُ مِن بَيْنِ يَدَيْهِ وَلَا مِنْ خَلْفِهِ ۖ تَنزِيلٌ مِّنْ حَكِيمٍ حَمِيدٍ
"Indeed, those who disbelieve in the message after it has come to them — and indeed, it is a mighty Book. Falsehood cannot approach it from before it or from behind it; [it is] a revelation from a [Lord who is] Wise and Praiseworthy." (41:41–42)

"La ya'tihi al-batil min bayni yadayhi wa la min khalfih" — falsehood cannot approach the Quran from any direction. This is one of the most powerful statements of Quranic preservation and integrity. It means: the Quran cannot be falsified by anything that came before it (previous scriptures), nor by anything that comes after it (future events or claims). It is sealed in truth from all angles.

Verse 44 makes an important point: had the Quran been in a non-Arabic language, the same Quraysh would have complained about that too. Their objections were not genuine — they were excuses. The heart that does not want to submit will always find a reason not to.

Verse 46 closes with a profound principle of divine justice: "Whoever does righteousness — it is for his own soul, and whoever does evil [does so] against it. And your Lord is not ever unjust to [His] servants." This is the foundation of Islamic ethics: our deeds return to us; Allah gains and loses nothing from our choices.

📚 Ibn Kathir

Ibn Kathir explains "a mighty Book" (kitab 'aziz) means it is honoured, protected, and beyond attack — no one can corrupt it, and no claim of corruption against it will succeed. History has confirmed this: 1,400 years of attempts to discredit or alter the Quran have failed entirely. Its preservation is itself a miracle.

📚 Al-Jalalayn

Al-Jalalayn explain "la ya'tihi al-batil" means the Quran does not contradict itself internally, does not conflict with confirmed historical truth, and cannot be replaced by a superior revelation — because it is final and perfect. It is invulnerable from every angle.

📚 As-Sa'di

As-Sa'di reflects on verse 46's closing statement — "your Lord is not ever unjust to His servants" — as the ultimate comfort in a world where injustice seems rampant. Whatever humans do to one another, Allah's accounting is perfect and His justice is total. No oppressor escapes; no oppressed one is forgotten.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • 📖 The Quran is "mighty" — invulnerable to falsehood from any direction, past or future.
  • 🔒 Its preservation for 1,400+ years is a standing miracle — the same words as the day of revelation.
  • 🙅 Those who reject it do not do so because it is unclear — their objections are excuses, not reasons.
  • ⚖️ "Allah is not unjust to His servants" — ultimate justice exists, even when we cannot see it now.
  • 🔄 Good deeds return to us; evil deeds return to us — our Lord gains nothing and loses nothing from our choices.

📜 Juz 24 — Overarching Themes

Three surahs, one coherent message — a call to sincere worship, divine mercy, and prophetic continuity.

🤲

The Centrality of Du'a

40:60 establishes prayer as worship itself. The believer's lifeline to Allah is always open — "Call upon Me, I will respond."

💛

Hope Amid Sin

39:53 is the most hope-filled verse in the Quran — Allah forgives all sins for those who sincerely repent. Despair is not permitted.

👼

The Unseen Support

The angels carry the Throne while making du'a for believers (40:7). At death they comfort the steadfast (41:30). We are never alone.

🏛️

Tyranny Always Fails

Pharaoh, Haman, Qarun — political, religious, economic power aligned against truth. All three failed. The believing man of Pharaoh's court prevailed.

📖

The Quran's Inviolability

Fussilat 41:42 — falsehood cannot approach the Quran from any direction. Its preservation is a standing divine miracle.

⚖️

Divine Justice

Every deed returns to its doer. Pharaoh's family suffers morning and evening. The believers' families will meet them in Paradise. Allah is never unjust.

🌍

Creation as Evidence

The night, day, sun, moon, mountains, provisions — all are ayat (signs) pointing to Allah. Look around; He has surrounded us in proof.

🦁

Courage of the Believer

The unnamed believer in Pharaoh's court spoke truth at mortal risk. Allah protected him completely (40:45). Speaking truth is its own protection.

🔚

The Urgency of Now

39:55 commands following the Quran before the punishment arrives. Past nations regretted when it was too late. The door is still open — for now.
