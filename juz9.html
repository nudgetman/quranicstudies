Surah Al-A'raf — The Heights
Makkan · 206 verses · 7th Chapter · Juz 9 begins at verse 88
🌄 Overview: Al-A'raf takes its name from "the Heights" — a place between Paradise and Hell mentioned in verse 46. The Juz 9 portion continues the long narrative of Prophet Musa (Moses) ﷺ with Pharaoh, then pivots to universal lessons about divine covenant, human nature, and the signs of Allah. It closes with timeless commands to the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ about patience and worship.
🏔️

Shu'ayb and the People of Madyan

Surah Al-A'raf · 7:88–93
قَالَ الْمَلَأُ الَّذِينَ اسْتَكْبَرُوا مِن قَوْمِهِ لَنُخْرِجَنَّكَ يَا شُعَيْبُ وَالَّذِينَ آمَنُوا مَعَكَ مِن قَرْيَتِنَا أَوْ لَتَعُودُنَّ فِي مِلَّتِنَا
"The arrogant leaders of his people said: 'We will drive you out, O Shu'ayb, and those who believe with you, from our town — or you must return to our religion.'" (7:88)

🕌 Juz 9 opens mid-story, in the final confrontation between Prophet Shu'ayb ﷺ and his people in the land of Madyan (likely near modern-day northwest Arabia/Jordan). Shu'ayb — known as "the preacher of the prophets" (khatīb al-anbiyā') for his eloquence — had been calling them to abandon fraud, dishonesty in trade, and idol worship.

The arrogant elites gave him a cruel ultimatum: leave our city or abandon your faith. This is the timeless tactic of oppressors — make the truth-teller choose between exile and apostasy.

📚 Ibn Kathir notes: Shu'ayb's response was magnificent in its logic: "How can we return to your religion after Allah has saved us from it? We would be fabricating a lie against Allah if we did so." He made clear that returning to shirk (polytheism) after guidance is a spiritual impossibility for the sincere believer.
وَمَا كَانَ لَنَا أَن نَّعُودَ فِيهَا إِلَّا أَن يَشَاءَ اللَّهُ رَبُّنَا
"It is not for us to return to it unless Allah our Lord wills." (7:89)

Notice Shu'ayb's beautiful tawakkul (trust in Allah) — he didn't say "we will never return" with human arrogance, but tied everything to the will of Allah. This is the correct Islamic formula: firmness in principle + humility about the future.

📖 Verses 90–93: The people accused the believers of being losers who would regret following Shu'ayb. Then Allah sent His punishment — a violent earthquake (ar-rajfah) that destroyed them. They became like those who never existed.

📚 Tafsir As-Sa'di: The destruction of Madyan teaches that arrogance toward messengers — especially when combined with economic oppression (their crime was cheating in weights and measures) — brings complete worldly annihilation. The honest trader is beloved to Allah; the fraudulent one brings ruin upon their community.
💡 Key Lessons (7:88–93)
✅ Oppressors always offer false choices to truth-tellers — but believers refuse both exile of the heart and exile of the body
✅ Saying "inshā' Allah" in matters of future resolve reflects proper Islamic theology, not weakness
✅ Economic dishonesty is a communal sin that invites divine punishment — business ethics matter deeply in Islam
⚠️

Universal Lessons from Destroyed Nations

Surah Al-A'raf · 7:94–102
وَمَا أَرْسَلْنَا فِي قَرْيَةٍ مِّن نَّبِيٍّ إِلَّا أَخَذْنَا أَهْلَهَا بِالْبَأْسَاءِ وَالضَّرَّاءِ لَعَلَّهُمْ يَضَّرَّعُونَ
"We never sent a prophet to any town without afflicting its people with hardship and suffering, so that they might humble themselves." (7:94)

After the stories of 'Ad, Thamud, Madyan, and Lut's people, Allah now draws out the universal pattern: whenever a prophet was sent, Allah would first test the people with hardship to humble them. If they humbled themselves, mercy followed. If they didn't — if they became arrogant even in suffering — punishment completed what hardship started.

📖 Verse 95: Then Allah tests them the other way — with prosperity and ease. The most dangerous test! The arrogant say, "Our ancestors also had good and bad times — this is just the natural cycle of life." They refuse to connect ease with divine mercy or hardship with divine warning.

📖 Verses 96–99: "Had the people of those towns believed and been God-fearing, We would have showered them with blessings from heaven and earth." This is one of the most profound economic and social statements in the Qur'an — collective righteousness brings collective blessing. The converse: "Do they feel safe from Allah's plan? None feel safe from Allah's plan except the losing people."

📚 Ibn Kathir on 7:96: The scholars explain this verse means: had they truly believed with their hearts AND put their faith into righteous action (taqwa), the skies would send rain and the earth would yield its crops. It's a promise that righteous societies prosper — not just spiritually, but materially. Ibn Kathir connects this to the hadith that rain is withheld from a people when they cheat in their transactions.
📚 As-Sa'di on the "security from Allah's plan" (7:99): The word makr here means Allah's precise, hidden arrangement of consequences for a transgressing people. It's described as coming to them when they are distracted in pleasures — like a sudden arrest. Feeling "safe" while disobeying Allah is itself a sign of spiritual destruction.
💡 Key Lessons (7:94–102)
✅ Both hardship AND prosperity are tests — prosperity is the harder one
✅ Collective faith and taqwa is connected to collective wellbeing — this is a societal, not just personal, principle
✅ "It's just the natural cycle" is the arrogant person's way of denying divine signs in daily events
✅ Never feel spiritually "safe" while far from Allah — that very feeling is the warning sign

Moses, Pharaoh, and the Magicians' Faith

Surah Al-A'raf · 7:103–126
ثُمَّ بَعَثْنَا مِن بَعْدِهِم مُّوسَىٰ بِآيَاتِنَا إِلَىٰ فِرْعَوْنَ وَمَلَئِهِ فَظَلَمُوا بِهَا
"Then after them We sent Moses with Our signs to Pharaoh and his chiefs, but they wrongfully rejected them." (7:103)

The story of Prophet Musa ﷺ (Moses) is the most frequently told story in the Qur'an — appearing in over 36 surahs — because it carries the richest lessons about faith, power, oppression, and divine victory.

📖 Verses 104–108: Musa presents himself: "I am a messenger from the Lord of all worlds. It is right that I should say nothing about Allah except the truth. I have come to you with a clear sign from your Lord — send the Children of Israel with me."

Pharaoh challenges: "Show me this sign." Musa throws his staff — it becomes a real, moving serpent 🐍. He pulls out his hand — it shines bright white (the miracle of the radiant hand ☀️).

📚 Tafsir Al-Jalalayn on the two miracles: The staff becoming a serpent (tha'ban — a large, fast-moving snake) and the radiant hand (baydāʾ — glowing white, not from disease) were chosen because the Egyptians were masters of illusion and snake-charming. Allah gave miracles that surpassed their greatest expertise, leaving no rational excuse for denial.

📖 Verses 109–119: Pharaoh's court accuses Musa of being a skilled sorcerer who wants to take over Egypt. He assembles the greatest magicians of the land for a showdown. The magicians cast their ropes and staffs — through their craft, it appeared they became snakes. Then Musa's staff swallowed them all. Truth devours falsehood completely.

📖 Verses 120–126 — The Magicians' Conversion: This is one of the most dramatic reversals in the Qur'an. The very magicians brought to humiliate Musa instantly fall into sujood (prostration): "We believe in the Lord of all worlds — the Lord of Moses and Aaron!"

Pharaoh explodes in fury: "How dare you believe before I give you permission! You must have conspired with him!" He threatens to amputate their hands and feet on opposite sides and crucify them. Their response? Completely fearless: "We will never prefer you over the clear signs that have come to us... Our Lord, pour patience upon us, and take our souls as Muslims."

📚 Ibn Kathir on the magicians' courage: The scholars note that in the morning these men were professionals of falsehood, by midday they were believers, and by evening they were willing martyrs. Faith that comes through direct recognition of truth (baṣīra) is immediately deep and unshakeable. The same eyes that crafted deception now saw through all deception the moment truth appeared.
💡 Key Lessons (7:103–126)
✅ True miracles speak for themselves — those with honest hearts recognize them immediately
✅ "Truth swallows falsehood" is a Qur'anic metaphor with historical backing — it always happens eventually
✅ The magicians' transformation shows that even those deep in sin can have the most profound, instant faith
✅ When someone threatens you for your faith, the correct response is: "Do what you will — we return to Allah"

The Nine Plagues & Crossing the Sea

Surah Al-A'raf · 7:127–141
وَلَقَدْ أَخَذْنَا آلَ فِرْعَوْنَ بِالسِّنِينَ وَنَقْصٍ مِّنَ الثَّمَرَاتِ لَعَلَّهُمْ يَذَّكَّرُونَ
"We afflicted Pharaoh's people with years of drought and shortage of fruits, so that they might take heed." (7:130)

Pharaoh refused to release the Children of Israel (Bani Isra'il), so Allah sent a series of escalating signs — each one a chance to repent:

  • 🌾 Years of drought and failed crops
  • 🐸 Floods, locusts, lice, frogs
  • 🩸 Blood (water turning to blood)

Yet every time a plague struck, they cried to Musa: "Pray to your Lord to remove this from us — and we will believe and send the Children of Israel with you!" Each time the plague lifted, they broke their promise. This is the psychology of insincere repentance — only pain motivates, not genuine change of heart.

📚 As-Sa'di on the Pattern of Broken Promises: The repeated cycle of affliction → plea → relief → betrayal is not just historical. It is the inner reality of the heart that only seeks Allah in crisis. The Qur'an presents this so believers examine themselves: do I remember Allah only when in hardship? Do I make promises to Him I don't keep when ease returns?

📖 Verses 137–141: After Pharaoh's army drowned in the sea, the Children of Israel were freed — but their test was just beginning. Passing by a people who worshipped idols, they said: "O Moses, make a god for us like their gods!" 😮

Musa's response was sharp: "You are indeed an ignorant people. These people — their religion will be destroyed, and what they do is worthless." Yet this incident foreshadows what comes next in Juz 9 — the golden calf.

💡 Key Lessons (7:127–141)
✅ Sincere repentance changes behavior, not just feelings — Pharaoh's people show what false repentance looks like
✅ Freedom from one oppressor doesn't guarantee freedom from the oppression of one's own desires
✅ The newly freed Israelites asking for idols shows how deeply environment shapes the soul — transformation takes time
⛰️

Moses at Mount Sinai — The Tablets & The Vision

Surah Al-A'raf · 7:142–153
وَوَاعَدْنَا مُوسَىٰ ثَلَاثِينَ لَيْلَةً وَأَتْمَمْنَاهَا بِعَشْرٍ فَتَمَّ مِيقَاتُ رَبِّهِ أَرْبَعِينَ لَيْلَةً
"We appointed thirty nights for Moses, then added ten more — so his Lord's appointed time was forty nights in all." (7:142)

Allah summoned Musa to Mount Sinai (Tur Sina) for 40 nights — a period of intense spiritual seclusion, fasting, and divine communication. He received the Tablets (al-Alwah) containing the Torah — guidance, law, and mercy for Bani Isra'il.

Before departing, Musa appointed his brother Harun (Aaron) ﷺ as his deputy over the people, commanding them to remain righteous and avoid corruption.

وَلَمَّا جَاءَ مُوسَىٰ لِمِيقَاتِنَا وَكَلَّمَهُ رَبُّهُ قَالَ رَبِّ أَرِنِي أَنظُرْ إِلَيْكَ
"When Moses came to Our appointed time and his Lord spoke to him, he said: 'My Lord, show Yourself to me, so that I may look at You.'" (7:143)

This is one of the most theologically rich moments in the Qur'an. Musa — having spoken directly with Allah — asked for the ultimate: to see Allah with his own eyes. This was not arrogance; this was the overwhelming love and longing of a prophet who wanted the highest of all experiences.

Allah's response: "You will never see Me." But then: "Look at the mountain — if it stays in place when My light touches it, then you will see Me." The light struck the mountain. The mountain crumbled to dust. Musa fell unconscious. When he came to: "Glory be to You! I turn to You in repentance, and I am the first of the believers."

📚 Ibn Kathir on the ru'yah (vision of Allah): The scholars of Ahl al-Sunnah affirm from this verse that seeing Allah in this world is impossible — the mountain, far stronger than any human, couldn't withstand even a fraction of the divine manifestation. But in the Hereafter, the believers WILL see their Lord, as established by hadith. The mountain's destruction is not punishment — it's illustration of the absolute disproportion between created things and the Creator's direct manifestation.
📚 Al-Jalalayn on "I am the first of the believers" (7:143): Scholars note Musa's statement "awwalu al-mu'minīn" (first of the believers) means he was the first to believe in this specific truth — that Allah cannot be seen in this worldly life — or that he was first among his people in renewing and affirming his faith after this overwhelming experience.
💡 Key Lessons (7:142–153)
✅ The longing to see Allah is the highest longing — it will be fulfilled for believers in Paradise (the greatest reward)
✅ Even mountains cannot bear a direct divine manifestation — this contextualizes Allah's absolute transcendence
✅ Even the greatest prophets respond to awe of Allah with immediate tasbih and tawbah — worship and repentance

The Golden Calf — Worship in Musa's Absence

Surah Al-A'raf · 7:148–156
وَاتَّخَذَ قَوْمُ مُوسَىٰ مِن بَعْدِهِ مِنْ حُلِيِّهِمْ عِجْلًا جَسَدًا لَّهُ خُوَارٌ
"In Moses's absence, his people made a calf from their jewelry — a physical form that produced a lowing sound." (7:148)

While Musa was receiving divine guidance on the mountain, a man named al-Samiri (mentioned by name in Surah Taha) fashioned a golden calf idol from the people's jewelry. It made a mooing sound — through hollow chambers that channeled wind, or through some other means. The people declared: "This is your god and the god of Moses!"

Harun tried to stop them and failed — the people overpowered him, nearly killing him. He waited for Musa's return.

📖 Verses 150–153 — Musa Returns: Musa descended the mountain with the Tablets. Seeing what had happened, he was furious and grief-stricken. He threw down the Tablets. He grabbed Harun by the beard and head — "What prevented you from following me when you saw them going astray?!"

Harun explained he had tried. Musa then turned to Allah: "My Lord, forgive me and my brother..." Then he confronted the people and al-Samiri, destroyed the calf, and scattered its ashes.

📚 Ibn Kathir on throwing the Tablets: Musa did not throw the Tablets in anger at Allah — he threw them out of the overwhelming grief of what his people had done, in a state of shock. When he calmed, he gathered them. The Tablets contained guidance and mercy. Ibn Kathir notes that the letters of the Tablets were visible by Allah's light — but when thrown, some content was lifted as a consequence of the sin of the calf worshippers.
📚 As-Sa'di on verse 155-156: Musa's dua (supplication) after the calf incident is a model: "You are our Protector, forgive us and have mercy on us — You are the best of those who forgive. Decree for us good in this world and the next." Allah's response: "My punishment — I afflict with it whom I will. But My mercy encompasses all things. I will decree it for those who fear Me..."
💡 Key Lessons (7:148–156)
✅ Idle communities without strong leadership drift toward worshipping things they can see — the root of shirk is insecurity
✅ A leader's anger at wrongdoing is righteous — but must be followed by wisdom, not just emotion
✅ Allah's mercy "encompasses all things" (wasi'at kulla shay') — this is one of the greatest statements of divine mercy in the Qur'an

The Prophecy of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ in the Torah

Surah Al-A'raf · 7:157–158
الَّذِينَ يَتَّبِعُونَ الرَّسُولَ النَّبِيَّ الْأُمِّيَّ الَّذِي يَجِدُونَهُ مَكْتُوبًا عِندَهُمْ فِي التَّوْرَاةِ وَالْإِنجِيلِ
"Those who follow the Messenger — the unlettered Prophet — whom they find written in the Torah and the Gospel..." (7:157)

This verse is remarkable — Allah is speaking to Bani Isra'il in the context of Moses's story, but suddenly pivots to announce the coming of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, described as:

  • 🔹 An-Nabiyy al-Ummiy — the "unlettered prophet" who could not read or write, making the Qur'an an undeniable miracle
  • 🔹 Found written in the Torah and Gospel — the People of the Book had descriptions of him in their own scriptures
  • 🔹 Commands good and forbids evil — his entire mission summarized
  • 🔹 Makes lawful the good things, forbids the evil — brings natural, universal ethics
  • 🔹 Removes their heavy burdens — the Mosaic law had many restrictions; Islam simplified and universalized
📚 Ibn Kathir on the "unlettered prophet": The word ummiy is mentioned twice in this verse — deliberate emphasis. A man who could not read or write, who never studied under a teacher, producing the most linguistically sublime, historically accurate, scientifically consistent text known as the Qur'an — this IS the miracle. The source cannot be human learning.
قُلْ يَا أَيُّهَا النَّاسُ إِنِّي رَسُولُ اللَّهِ إِلَيْكُمْ جَمِيعًا
"Say: O mankind! I am truly the Messenger of Allah to you all." (7:158)

This is a global declaration. Every previous prophet was sent to a specific people — Nuh to his generation, Ibrahim to his, Musa to Bani Isra'il. Muhammad ﷺ was sent to all of humanity until the Day of Judgement. This universality is unique in prophetic history.

💡 Key Lessons (7:157–158)
✅ The Prophet ﷺ was not an innovation — he was foretold, expected, and described in earlier scriptures
✅ Islam's ethics: command good, forbid evil, make clean things permissible, make harmful things forbidden — a balanced system
✅ Being "unlettered" was not a deficiency — it was the very proof of divine origin of the Qur'an

The Primordial Covenant — "Am I not your Lord?"

Surah Al-A'raf · 7:172–174
وَإِذْ أَخَذَ رَبُّكَ مِن بَنِي آدَمَ مِن ظُهُورِهِمْ ذُرِّيَّتَهُمْ وَأَشْهَدَهُمْ عَلَىٰ أَنفُسِهِمْ أَلَسْتُ بِرَبِّكُمْ ۖ قَالُوا بَلَىٰ
"When your Lord took from the children of Adam — from their loins — their descendants, and made them testify about themselves: 'Am I not your Lord?' They said: 'Yes! We bear witness.'" (7:172)

This is one of the most profound, soul-stirring verses in the entire Qur'an — the Covenant of Alast (named after the Arabic "Alastu" — "Am I not?").

Before any of us were born into this world, before Adam's children walked the earth — every human soul was gathered in a pre-worldly realm. Allah extracted all future generations from Adam's loins and asked them a single question: "Am I not your Lord?" Every soul — yours, mine, every human who ever lived — answered: "Yes! We bear witness."

This is why, Allah explains, no one can say on the Day of Judgment: "We had no idea," or "We were just following what our ancestors taught us." The knowledge of the Lord is not learned — it is remembered. Fitrah (the innate disposition toward God) is the echo of this ancient "yes."

📚 Ibn Kathir on the nature of this covenant: The covenant was real, not metaphorical — actual souls were gathered and testified. This is the basis of the concept of fitrah: every human is born with an innate recognition of a Creator. No child is born an atheist — disbelief is taught or learned. The Prophet ﷺ said: "Every child is born on the fitrah; it is the parents who make them a Jew, Christian, or Magian." (Bukhari)
📚 As-Sa'di on the wisdom of the covenant: Allah took the covenant as a proof against humanity — not to burden us, but so that the excuse "we didn't know" is permanently removed. The longing that every human feels — even the atheist — for something beyond themselves, for meaning, for the transcendent — is the residual memory of this covenant.
💡 Key Lessons (7:172–174)
✅ You were created knowing Allah — faith is not a new acquisition, it is a return
✅ The universal human longing for the divine has a basis: we have all already met our Creator
✅ "No excuse" is not a threat — it's a reminder that the truth is built into us, not foreign to us

The Scholar Who Abandoned His Knowledge

Surah Al-A'raf · 7:175–179
وَاتْلُ عَلَيْهِمْ نَبَأَ الَّذِي آتَيْنَاهُ آيَاتِنَا فَانسَلَخَ مِنْهَا فَأَتْبَعَهُ الشَّيْطَانُ فَكَانَ مِنَ الْغَاوِينَ
"Recite to them the story of the one We gave Our signs — but he stripped himself of them. So Shaytan followed him, and he became of those who went astray." (7:175)

Allah tells one of the most chilling stories in the Qur'an — a man given divine knowledge who deliberately abandoned it for the world. The majority of classical scholars (Ibn Kathir, Al-Qurtubi) identify him as Bal'am ibn Ba'ura, a scholar of Bani Isra'il who knew the Greatest Name of Allah (Ism al-A'zam) and was famous for having his prayers answered.

He knew the signs of Allah. He was given this sacred knowledge. But he "stripped himself of it" — the Arabic insakhalakha minha means to shed something like a skin, to molt out of it. He chose the world, used his knowledge for the wrong side, and Shaytan attached to him permanently.

فَمَثَلُهُ كَمَثَلِ الْكَلْبِ إِن تَحْمِلْ عَلَيْهِ يَلْهَثْ أَوْ تَتْرُكْهُ يَلْهَثْ
"His likeness is that of a dog — if you drive it away, it pants; if you leave it alone, it still pants." (7:176)

This stark simile is sobering. The person who abandons truth for the world is like a panting dog — always chasing, never satisfied, never at rest. Whether things go well or badly for him, he remains in this restless state. There is no contentment outside of alignment with the truth you were given.

📖 Verse 179: "We have created for Hell many jinn and humans. They have hearts with which they do not understand, eyes with which they do not see, ears with which they do not hear. They are like cattle — in fact, more astray. They are the heedless."

This verse makes clear: the faculties of understanding, sight, and hearing are not merely physical — they are spiritual. You can see with your eyes but be blind in your heart. Knowledge without action = blind scholarship.

📚 As-Sa'di on the Scholar Who Fell: The greatest danger is not ignorance — it is knowledge combined with worldly desire. A person who knows the truth and chooses against it is worse than one who never knew, because they have willfully extinguished their own light. This passage is a warning specifically for scholars, imams, and educated Muslims.
💡 Key Lessons (7:175–179)
✅ Knowledge of the deen without acting on it is the most dangerous spiritual state
✅ "Restlessness" is the sign of a heart that has abandoned truth — contentment only comes with truth
✅ The heart can be spiritually blind even while the eyes see perfectly — spiritual vision must be cultivated

Allah's Beautiful Names — Al-Asma ul-Husna

Surah Al-A'raf · 7:180–186
وَلِلَّهِ الْأَسْمَاءُ الْحُسْنَىٰ فَادْعُوهُ بِهَا وَذَرُوا الَّذِينَ يُلْحِدُونَ فِي أَسْمَائِهِ
"To Allah belong the Most Beautiful Names — so call on Him by them. And leave those who distort His Names." (7:180)

After warning of those who misuse knowledge, Allah shifts to the highest knowledge: knowing Allah through His Names and Attributes. The Beautiful Names (al-Asma' al-Husna) are the means by which we know, call upon, and relate to our Creator.

The command is: call upon Allah by His Names. This means:

  • Call upon Ar-Rahman (The Most Merciful) when seeking mercy
  • Call upon Al-Ghaffar (The Oft-Forgiving) when seeking forgiveness
  • Call upon Al-Qadir (The All-Powerful) when seeking help in difficulty
  • Call upon Al-Razzaq (The Provider) when seeking sustenance
📚 Ibn Kathir on "those who distort His Names": Three types of distortion: (1) Applying divine Names to idols (they called idols al-Lat from al-Ilah, al-Uzza from al-Aziz); (2) Denying the Names altogether (the Jahmiyyah sect); (3) Claiming the Names have the same nature as human attributes (anthropomorphism). The correct belief (Ahl al-Sunnah): affirm the Names and their meanings as befitting Allah's majesty, without comparison to creation.

📖 Verses 185–186: "Do they not look at the kingdom of the heavens and earth, and all the things Allah has created?" The study of creation is itself an act of worship — looking at the universe with the eyes of a believer is dhikr (remembrance of Allah).

"Whoever Allah sends astray — there is no guide for them. He leaves them wandering in their transgression." (7:186) — This refers to those who willfully choose darkness after being given light. Allah does not oppress; He seals the hearts of those who have already sealed them against Him.

💡 Key Lessons (7:180–186)
✅ Learn Allah's Names — this is the highest knowledge in Islam and transforms your du'a
✅ Nature is a book of signs — looking at creation with reflective eyes is a form of worship
✅ Guidance is not forced — those who persistently close their hearts eventually find them sealed
⏳

The Hour — Kept Hidden by Design

Surah Al-A'raf · 7:187–188
يَسْأَلُونَكَ عَنِ السَّاعَةِ أَيَّانَ مُرْسَاهَا ۖ قُلْ إِنَّمَا عِلْمُهَا عِندَ رَبِّي
"They ask you about the Hour — when will it arrive? Say: Knowledge of it is with my Lord alone." (7:187)

People asked the Prophet ﷺ: when is the Day of Judgement? Allah's answer is clear and absolute: no one knows. Not the Prophet, not the angels, not any human. Only Allah knows the exact moment the Hour will arrive.

Why is it hidden? The scholars give beautiful wisdom: if we knew exactly when death or the Hour would arrive, human behavior would be completely distorted — people would sin until the last moment and "repent" at the last second. The uncertainty is itself the mercy — it keeps every moment potentially the last, every action potentially the most important.

📚 Al-Jalalayn on "heavy in the heavens and earth" (7:187): The phrase thaqulat fī al-samāwāti wal-arḍ (heavy upon the heavens and earth) means: its coming will be tremendous, its signs will be catastrophic, and the knowledge of when it comes is too weighty — too significant — to be carried by any created being. Only Allah can bear that knowledge.
قُل لَّا أَمْلِكُ لِنَفْسِي نَفْعًا وَلَا ضَرًّا إِلَّا مَا شَاءَ اللَّهُ
"Say: I have no power to bring benefit or harm to myself, except what Allah wills." (7:188)

This is the Prophet's ﷺ own declaration of his human limitation — and it is also one of the clearest statements against excessive veneration of the Prophet that leads people to ask him for things only Allah can provide. He himself declares: I don't control my own benefit or harm without Allah's will — so turn your requests to Allah, not to any created being.

💡 Key Lessons (7:187–188)
✅ Not knowing when the Hour comes is a design feature, not a flaw — live as if every moment could be your last
✅ Even the Prophet ﷺ had no autonomous power — all power and knowledge belongs to Allah alone
✅ Du'a must ultimately go to Allah — no prophet or saint "controls" outcomes
👫

Creation of Humanity & The Futility of Idols

Surah Al-A'raf · 7:189–198
هُوَ الَّذِي خَلَقَكُم مِّن نَّفْسٍ وَاحِدَةٍ وَجَعَلَ مِنْهَا زَوْجَهَا لِيَسْكُنَ إِلَيْهَا
"It is He who created you from a single soul, and made from it its spouse, that he might find comfort in her." (7:189)

The single soul (nafs wāhidah) is Adam ﷺ; his spouse is Hawwa' (Eve). The word for "comfort" (yaskuna ilayha) shares its root with sakīnah — peace, tranquility. Marriage in Islam is designed to be a place of soul-rest, not just a legal or social contract.

📖 Verses 190–198: When Allah blessed them with a child, they associated partners with Allah — a reference to the gradual drift of humanity from pure monotheism. Iblis (Shaytan) is then challenged: can he and his followers hear, see, walk, or do anything for themselves? No — "Do those you call upon beside Allah create anything? They create nothing and are themselves created."

📚 As-Sa'di on the nature of idols (7:191-197): The Qur'an's argument against idols is devastatingly simple: if your god cannot see, hear, walk, or respond — it cannot help you. Asking help from something that cannot help itself is the definition of absurdity. The modern equivalent: putting ultimate trust in money, power, fame, or people — things that cannot guarantee your wellbeing — is a form of this same misdirection.
💡 Key Lessons (7:189–198)
✅ Marriage is designed for sakīnah (tranquility) — this is its Qur'anic purpose
✅ Modern "idols" — money, status, relationships — fail the same test: they cannot truly help you
✅ Gratitude to Allah must be the response to every blessing — not superstition or attribution to luck
🤲

Mercy, Patience, and the Closing Prostration Verse

Surah Al-A'raf · 7:199–206
خُذِ الْعَفْوَ وَأْمُرْ بِالْعُرْفِ وَأَعْرِضْ عَنِ الْجَاهِلِينَ
"Show forgiveness, command what is right, and turn away from the ignorant." (7:199)

Three divine commands in one verse — a complete social ethics guide for the Prophet ﷺ and for every believer:

  • 🔹 Khudh al-'afwa — take the easier path with people, be forgiving, don't demand perfection
  • 🔹 Wa'mur bil-'urf — command what is recognized as good, what is universally accepted as righteous
  • 🔹 Wa a'rid 'an al-jahilin — ignore and walk away from the ignorant; don't engage with every fool
📚 Ibn Kathir quotes Jabir ibn 'Abdullah: When this verse was revealed, the Prophet ﷺ asked Jibreel what it meant. Jibreel said: "I do not know until I ask." He then came back: "O Muhammad, your Lord commands you to forgive those who wrong you, give to those who withhold from you, and maintain ties with those who cut you off." The Prophet ﷺ said: "I will do that." This verse contains the essence of prophetic character.
وَإِمَّا يَنزَغَنَّكَ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ نَزْغٌ فَاسْتَعِذْ بِاللَّهِ ۚ إِنَّهُ سَمِيعٌ عَلِيمٌ
"If an evil impulse from Shaytan stirs you, seek refuge in Allah — He is All-Hearing, All-Knowing." (7:200)

📖 Verses 200–202: The contrast: when Shaytan whispers to the God-fearing, they immediately remember Allah and see clearly. But Shaytan pulls his "brothers" (those who have given him access) further and further into error without stopping.

📖 Verses 203–204: The mushrikun (pagans) ask why the Prophet ﷺ doesn't bring new "miracles" on demand. Allah responds: the Qur'an itself IS the miracle — it is guidance and mercy. When the Qur'an is recited, listen carefully and be silent — so that you might receive mercy.

۩ إِنَّ الَّذِينَ عِندَ رَبِّكَ لَا يَسْتَكْبِرُونَ عَنْ عِبَادَتِهِ وَيُسَبِّحُونَهُ وَلَهُ يَسْجُدُونَ
۩ "Those who are with your Lord are never too proud to worship Him. They glorify Him and prostrate to Him." (7:206)

This is the sajdah (prostration) verse — one of 15 in the Qur'an. When this verse is recited or heard, it is Sunnah to perform a prostration of recitation (sajdat at-tilawah). The verse describes the angels — who have no needs, no ego, no fatigue — and still they prostrate to Allah continuously. If the angels do this, how much more should we?

💡 Key Lessons (7:199–206)
✅ The three-part formula: forgiveness + commanding good + ignoring the ignorant = prophetic social ethics
✅ The immediate antidote to Shaytan's whispers is seeking refuge in Allah (ta'awwudh)
✅ Listening to Qur'an recitation with presence and silence is commanded — it's not background noise
✅ If the angels never stop praising and prostrating to Allah — our 5 prayers are the bare minimum of gratitude

سُورَةُ الأَنْفَال
Surah Al-Anfal — The Spoils of War
Madinan · 75 verses · 8th Chapter · Juz 9 covers verses 1–40
⚔️ Overview: Al-Anfal was revealed after the Battle of Badr (2 AH / 624 CE) — the first major military confrontation between the Muslims and the Quraysh. 313 ill-equipped Muslims defeated over 1,000 Meccans. This surah addresses the aftermath: who gets the war spoils, the spiritual qualities of the true believer, divine help in battle, and warnings to those who plot against Islam. It is both a battle report and a masterclass in faith under pressure.
💎

The Definition of True Believers

Surah Al-Anfal · 8:1–4
يَسْأَلُونَكَ عَنِ الْأَنفَالِ ۖ قُلِ الْأَنفَالُ لِلَّهِ وَالرَّسُولِ
"They ask you about the war gains. Say: the war gains belong to Allah and the Messenger." (8:1)

After Badr, a dispute arose: who gets the spoils? Those who fought on the front lines? Those who guarded the Prophet ﷺ? Those who chased fleeing enemies? Allah settled it simply: it belongs to Allah and His Messenger — they will distribute it with justice. (Later in this surah, the rule of 1/5 to Allah and 4/5 distributed to fighters is established.)

Allah then immediately redirects: "So have taqwa (God-consciousness) and fix the relations between yourselves." — The real concern is not who gets what, but that the community remains united.

إِنَّمَا الْمُؤْمِنُونَ الَّذِينَ إِذَا ذُكِرَ اللَّهُ وَجِلَتْ قُلُوبُهُمْ
"The true believers are those whose hearts tremble when Allah is mentioned..." (8:2)

Allah now gives the definitive profile of a mu'min (true believer) — 5 characteristics in verses 2-4:

  • 💓 Hearts tremble at the mention of Allah — not from fear alone, but from awe and love
  • 📖 Faith increases when Qur'anic verses are recited — the Qur'an doesn't just inform, it transforms
  • 🙏 They put their trust in Allah — tawakkul: they plan, then surrender outcomes
  • 🕌 They establish prayer — salah is the pillar; not just performed but "established" (iqamah)
  • 💰 They give from what Allah provided them — their wealth is not theirs; they give from what they were trusted with

Verse 4: "Those are the true believers. They have ranks with their Lord, forgiveness, and a generous provision."

📚 As-Sa'di on "hearts that tremble": The wajal (trembling) of the heart at Allah's mention is the sign of a heart that is alive. A hard, dead heart feels nothing when Allah's name is mentioned. The trembling is not chronic anxiety — it is the appropriate response of the created to the Creator: a mix of reverence, love, and awareness of one's own shortcomings before the Most Great.
💡 Key Lessons (8:1–4)
✅ Community unity matters more than material distribution — Allah settled the dispute by pointing upward
✅ True faith has measurable, observable characteristics — not just a claim or a feeling
✅ Salah "established" means regular, on-time, with presence of heart — not just performed mechanically
✅ Increasing iman during Qur'an recitation is a sign of a healthy heart — check your response to the Qur'an
⚔️

The Battle of Badr — Divine Help in the Darkest Odds

Surah Al-Anfal · 8:5–19
كَمَا أَخْرَجَكَ رَبُّكَ مِن بَيْتِكَ بِالْحَقِّ وَإِنَّ فَرِيقًا مِّنَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ لَكَارِهُونَ
"Just as your Lord brought you out of your home for the truth, though a party of the believers disliked it." (8:5)

Some companions initially disliked the decision to intercept the Quraysh caravan — they hadn't prepared for a full battle. Allah compares this to their dislike of the command to leave Makkah — sometimes what feels uncomfortable is divinely ordained and leads to tremendous good.

📖 Verses 7–9: Allah tells the believers He had promised them victory over one of two things — either the caravan (easy wealth) or the army (the test). He chose the army for them. The believers called out: "O Allah, if this is the truth from You, rain stones on us from the sky or bring a painful punishment." Instead, Allah answered: "I will reinforce you with 1,000 angels following one after another."

إِذْ يُوحِي رَبُّكَ إِلَى الْمَلَائِكَةِ أَنِّي مَعَكُمْ فَثَبِّتُوا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا
"When your Lord revealed to the angels: 'I am with you, so make the believers stand firm.'" (8:12)

📖 Verses 10–13: Allah describes what happened that day at Badr:

  • 😴 Allah sent sleep upon the believers — a sign of divine reassurance (not fleeing fear; those planning harm cannot sleep before battle)
  • 🌧️ Rain was sent — to purify the Muslims, remove the whispers of Shaytan, firm the ground under their feet, and fill their hearts with calm
  • 😇 Angels fought alongside them — not just as a reward but as a lesson: divine help arrives for those who prepare and trust
وَمَا رَمَيْتَ إِذْ رَمَيْتَ وَلَٰكِنَّ اللَّهَ رَمَىٰ
"You did not throw when you threw — it was Allah who threw." (8:17)

This verse refers to the Prophet ﷺ throwing a handful of dust or pebbles toward the Quraysh at Badr, saying "May their faces be disfigured!" The dust reached every enemy fighter. The action was the Prophet's — the result was Allah's. This encapsulates the entire theology of asbab (causes) and tawakkul (reliance): do your part fully, then attribute outcomes entirely to Allah.

📚 Ibn Kathir on the Battle of Badr: Badr was not just a military victory — it was a theological statement. 313 believers with 2 horses and 70 camels, many without weapons, defeated 1,000 fully armed Quraysh warriors. 70 Quraysh leaders were killed, 70 captured. The prophetic statement: "This day, the truth will be clearly distinguished from falsehood (yawm al-furqan)." Allah later called Badr "Yawm al-Furqan" — the Day of Distinction.
💡 Key Lessons (8:5–19)
✅ Divine wisdom sometimes chooses the harder path for us — because the greater gain lies there
✅ Sleep before danger is a sign of divine reassurance; the righteous find peace even in crisis
✅ "You did not throw — Allah threw" — credit outcomes to Allah, take responsibility for effort
✅ Divine help doesn't come to the idle — the Muslims prepared, marched, and fought; then the angels came
👂

Obedience, Betrayal & The Living vs The Dead

Surah Al-Anfal · 8:20–29
يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا أَطِيعُوا اللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُ وَلَا تَوَلَّوْا عَنْهُ وَأَنتُمْ تَسْمَعُونَ
"O believers! Obey Allah and His Messenger, and do not turn away from him while you hear." (8:20)

After the spiritual high of Badr, Allah issues a grounding command: obedience must be active, not passive. "While you hear" means: you cannot claim you didn't know. Hearing the command and not acting is a form of turning away. This addresses the internal hypocrites who claimed to believe but selectively obeyed.

وَلَا تَكُونُوا كَالَّذِينَ قَالُوا سَمِعْنَا وَهُمْ لَا يَسْمَعُونَ
"Do not be like those who say 'We hear' while they do not truly hear." (8:21)
إِنَّ شَرَّ الدَّوَابِّ عِندَ اللَّهِ الصُّمُّ الْبُكْمُ الَّذِينَ لَا يَعْقِلُونَ
"The worst of living creatures in Allah's sight are the deaf and dumb who do not reason." (8:22)

Theologically charged: those who refuse to hear truth and refuse to speak it, while refusing to use their intellect — are described as the worst of creatures. Not the most sinful in a conventional sense, but the most wasted — they have been given the tools of guidance (ears, tongue, mind) and returned them unused or misused.

📖 Verses 24-25: One of the most famous and frequently cited verses:

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا اسْتَجِيبُوا لِلَّهِ وَلِلرَّسُولِ إِذَا دَعَاكُمْ لِمَا يُحْيِيكُمْ
"O believers! Respond to Allah and to the Messenger when he calls you to that which gives you life." (8:24)

The Qur'an and Sunnah are described as that which gives life — not metaphorically, but literally. The soul is only truly alive when connected to divine guidance. Without it, the body functions but the person is spiritually dead. This verse reframes Islamic practice — prayer, fasting, Qur'an, halal living — as life support, not restriction.

📖 Verse 28: "Know that your wealth and your children are a trial, and that with Allah is a great reward." The greatest tests in life often come in the form of what we love most — wealth and family. They are not to be rejected, but held with open hands, not clenched fists.

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا إِن تَتَّقُوا اللَّهَ يَجْعَل لَّكُمْ فُرْقَانًا
"O believers! If you have taqwa, Allah will grant you a Furqan." (8:29)

Furqan — the ability to distinguish truth from falsehood, right from wrong, the real from the fake. This is not an intellectual gift — it's a spiritual one. Taqwa gives you discernment. The God-fearing person sees through deception that blinds others. They sense when something is wrong even before they can articulate why. This is the "sixth sense" of the believer — earned through God-consciousness.

💡 Key Lessons (8:20–29)
✅ Hearing commands and not acting is itself a form of disobedience — not a neutral state
✅ Islam's commands are described as "that which gives you life" — they are not restrictions but vital sustenance
✅ Wealth and children are beautiful — and tests. Hold them loosely, with gratitude, not possessiveness
✅ Taqwa is the source of furqan (discernment) — the most practically useful spiritual gift in daily life
🕊️

The Quraysh's Plots & The Call to Truth

Surah Al-Anfal · 8:30–40
وَإِذْ يَمْكُرُ بِكَ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لِيُثْبِتُوكَ أَوْ يَقْتُلُوكَ أَوْ يُخْرِجُوكَ ۚ وَيَمْكُرُونَ وَيَمْكُرُ اللَّهُ ۖ وَاللَّهُ خَيْرُ الْمَاكِرِينَ
"When the disbelievers plotted against you to imprison, kill, or expel you — they plotted, and Allah plotted; and Allah is the best of planners." (8:30)

This refers to the famous Dar al-Nadwa meeting — the night before the Hijrah (migration to Madinah) when the Quraysh leaders assembled to decide how to stop Muhammad ﷺ once and for all. Three proposals: imprison him, kill him (one from each tribe to diffuse blood guilt), or exile him. Abu Jahl pushed for assassination — each tribe sending a warrior to strike simultaneously.

Allah warned the Prophet that night. He made the miraculous escape — Ali ibn Abi Talib ؓ slept in his bed as decoy. The Prophet ﷺ walked out past the guards unseen, reciting the beginning of Surah Yasin.

📚 Ibn Kathir on "Allah is the best of planners": The Arabic khayr al-mākirīn — Allah's "plotting" is not deceptive in the negative sense. It means His hidden wisdom and arrangement of events outmatches every human scheme. The more sophisticated the plot against the truth, the more dramatically Allah arranges its failure. This is the sunan (pattern) of Allah throughout history.
وَقَاتِلُوهُمْ حَتَّىٰ لَا تَكُونَ فِتْنَةٌ وَيَكُونَ الدِّينُ كُلُّهُ لِلَّهِ
"Fight them until there is no more fitnah, and all religion is for Allah." (8:39)

The word fitnah here refers specifically to the persecution, religious oppression, and forced apostasy that the Quraysh were inflicting on Muslims — preventing people from practicing Islam freely, torturing converts, and making the religion of truth unavailable. This verse commanded fighting to end religious persecution, not to force conversion.

The next verse (8:39b) makes this clear: "But if they stop — then indeed Allah is All-Seeing of what they do." The goal is not annihilation — the moment oppression stops, fighting stops.

وَإِن جَنَحُوا لِلسَّلْمِ فَاجْنَحْ لَهَا وَتَوَكَّلْ عَلَى اللَّهِ
"And if they incline to peace, then incline to it [also], and put your trust in Allah." (8:61 — referenced for context)

📖 Verses 38–40: The surah closes Juz 9 with a final, generous call to the disbelievers: "If they stop, they will be forgiven for what has passed." The door of repentance is always open — even for those who plotted the Prophet's assassination. But if they refuse: "Know that Allah is your Protector — the best Protector and the best Helper."

📚 As-Sa'di on the ending of Juz 9: The closing of these verses contains a profound structural beauty: it begins (8:1) with the question of worldly spoils and ends with the declaration that Allah is your Wali (Protector) and Naṣīr (Helper). The surah reorients us — from what we gain materially to Who we have spiritually. The greatest spoil of Badr was not the equipment captured, but the clarity that when Allah is with you, no number, no weapon, no plot can overcome you.
💡 Key Lessons (8:30–40)
✅ The more sophisticated the plot against truth, the more dramatic Allah's response — He is the best of planners
✅ Fighting in Islam was commanded to end oppression and religious persecution, not to force belief
✅ The door of forgiveness remains open even to the worst enemies — until death
✅ Allah as Protector and Helper — this is the greatest comfort and the ultimate security

🌟 Juz 9 — Overarching Themes

📜 Prophetic Continuity

Every prophet faced the same resistance — but Allah's truth always prevails. Shu'ayb, Musa, and Muhammad ﷺ faced identical oppression with identical divine support.

💫 Divine Covenant

Every soul has already met Allah and testified to His Lordship (7:172). Faith is remembrance, not new information. The fitrah is our compass home.

⚖️ Knowledge Demands Action

The scholar who abandoned his knowledge became like a panting dog. The magicians who recognized truth acted immediately. Knowledge without action is the most dangerous state.

🛡️ Divine Help Has Conditions

Badr's miracle came after preparation, migration, and the believers' complete surrender. Divine help is earned through genuine effort + sincere tawakkul — not passive waiting.

"If you support Allah, He will support you and make your feet firm." — (47:7)
