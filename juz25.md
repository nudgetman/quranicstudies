## Surah 41 · Fussilat — "Explained in Detail"

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Meccan |
| **Total Verses** | 54 |
| **This Juz** | Verses 47–54 (closing section) |

Juz 25 opens with the powerful closing verses of Fussilat, a surah that takes its name from the phrase "explained in detail" — describing how Allah has made the Qur'an clear and eloquent. These final eight verses address the unseen knowledge of Allah, the transformation of the disbelievers on the Day of Judgement, and a stirring assurance that the truth of Allah's signs will become undeniably manifest.

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ

---

### 41:47–48 — Knowledge of the Hour

**Arabic:**

> إِلَيْهِ يُرَدُّ عِلْمُ السَّاعَةِ ۚ وَمَا تَخْرُجُ مِن ثَمَرَاتٍ مِّنْ أَكْمَامِهَا وَمَا تَحْمِلُ مِنْ أُنثَىٰ وَلَا تَضَعُ إِلَّا بِعِلْمِهِ ۚ وَيَوْمَ يُنَادِيهِمْ أَيْنَ شُرَكَائِي قَالُوا آذَنَّاكَ مَا مِنَّا مِن شَهِيدٍ ﴿٤٧﴾ وَضَلَّ عَنْهُم مَّا كَانُوا يَدْعُونَ مِن قَبْلُ ۖ وَظَنُّوا مَا لَهُم مِّن مَّحِيصٍ ﴿٤٨﴾

**Translation (Saheeh International):**

> To Him [alone] is attributed knowledge of the Hour. And fruits emerge not from their coverings, nor does a female conceive or give birth, except with His knowledge. And the Day He calls to them, "Where are My partners?" they will say, "We announce to You that none of us is a witness [to that]." And lost from them will be what they had been invoking before, and they will be certain that they have no place of escape.

**Explanation:**

These verses establish one of the most profound truths in the Qur'an: Allah alone possesses absolute, complete knowledge. The example of a fruit bursting from its sheath, or a mother carrying a child in her womb — these seemingly small events are all within the direct, immediate knowledge of the Creator. No algorithm, no instrument, no prophet knows when the Last Hour will come; this knowledge is an exclusive divine prerogative.

On the Day of Judgement, Allah will confront the polytheists about their false gods. The idols they worshipped will be entirely absent, and the people themselves will desperately deny ever having believed in them — such will be their panic and regret.

**Scholar Callouts:**

> **📚 Ibn Kathir:** Ibn Kathir notes that the five matters mentioned here — the Hour, rainfall, what is in the womb, the sustenance for tomorrow, and the place of death — are the *Mafatih al-Ghayb* (Keys of the Unseen), preserved exclusively in divine knowledge. No human, angel, or prophet was granted these secrets.

> **📚 Tafsir Al-Jalalayn:** Al-Jalalayn comments that the phrase "We announce to You that none of us is a witness to that" reflects the polytheists' complete disavowal on that terrifying Day — they who once boasted of their idols will refuse to acknowledge them at all.

> **📚 Tafsir As-Sa'di:** As-Sa'di emphasises that linking knowledge of the Hour with knowledge of the fruit in the womb is deliberate — it shows that Allah's knowledge is not only grand and cosmic but intimate and precise, reaching into the smallest detail of every created thing.

**✦ Key Lessons:**

- 🔑 The five keys of the unseen belong exclusively to Allah — no creature shares in this knowledge.
- 🌿 Even the sprouting of a seed from its covering is an act witnessed and willed by Allah directly.
- ⚖️ Shirk (associating partners with Allah) will be utterly abandoned and denied by its own practitioners on Judgement Day.
- 😰 Those who deny the truth will have no escape route — realising this should motivate sincere repentance now.

### 41:49–54 — Impatience of Man & the Promise of Divine Signs

**Arabic:**

> لَا يَسْأَمُ الْإِنسَانُ مِن دُعَاءِ الْخَيْرِ وَإِن مَّسَّهُ الشَّرُّ فَيَئُوسٌ قَنُوطٌ ﴿٤٩﴾ وَلَئِنْ أَذَقْنَاهُ رَحْمَةً مِّنَّا مِن بَعْدِ ضَرَّاءَ مَسَّتْهُ لَيَقُولَنَّ هَٰذَا لِي ﴿٥٠﴾ سَنُرِيهِمْ آيَاتِنَا فِي الْآفَاقِ وَفِي أَنفُسِهِمْ حَتَّىٰ يَتَبَيَّنَ لَهُمْ أَنَّهُ الْحَقُّ ﴿٥٣﴾

**Translation (Saheeh International):**

> Man is not weary of supplication for good [things], but if adversity touches him, he is hopeless and despairing. And if We let him taste mercy from Us after an adversity which had touched him, he will surely say, "This is [due] to me." … We will show them Our signs in the horizons and within themselves until it becomes clear to them that it is the truth.

**Explanation:**

These closing verses of Fussilat describe the fundamental spiritual disease of human ingratitude. When hardship strikes, man despairs completely; when ease returns, he attributes it to his own cleverness rather than to divine mercy. The Qur'an diagnoses this as a failure of *tawakkul* (trust in Allah) and *shukr* (gratitude).

Verse 53 is one of the most celebrated promises in the Qur'an — that Allah will show humanity His signs both on the grand cosmic horizon (the stars, the ecosystems, the laws of nature) and within their own selves (the miracle of human biology, consciousness, the soul). This promise continues to unfold in every scientific discovery that points to an intelligent, compassionate Creator.

**Scholar Callouts:**

> **📚 Ibn Kathir:** Ibn Kathir says regarding v. 53: "This is a great promise from Allah — that He will make His signs manifest on the horizons and within the selves of people, so that it becomes evident to every rational person that the Qur'an is the truth revealed by the Lord of all Worlds." He cites many companion reports that this refers to the spreading of Islam and the intellectual victories of the believers as proof of Qur'anic truth.

> **📚 Tafsir As-Sa'di:** As-Sa'di draws a beautiful contrast: Man prays endlessly for good, never tiring of asking — yet when trials come, he collapses into despair. The believer, however, understands that both ease and hardship are from Allah, and neither causes him to abandon gratitude or hope. This verse is a call to cultivate the character of the believer.

**✦ Key Lessons:**

- 🙏 Gratitude in ease and patience in hardship are the twin pillars of a believer's relationship with Allah.
- 🔭 The universe itself — from galaxies to cells — is full of signs (*ayat*) pointing to the Creator.
- 💫 Every scientific discovery that unveils the order of creation is, in a real sense, a fulfilment of Allah's promise in 41:53.
- ⚠️ Attributing blessings to oneself rather than Allah is a subtle form of ingratitude that corrupts the heart.

## Surah 42 · Ash-Shura — "The Consultation"

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Meccan |
| **Total Verses** | 53 |
| **This Juz** | Verses 1–53 (complete) |

Ash-Shura takes its name from verse 38, which mentions that the believers conduct their affairs through mutual consultation (*shura*). It is one of the seven surahs that begin with Muqatta'at (disconnected letters), here opening with Ḥā Mīm ʿAyn Sīn Qāf — the longest such combination. The surah is a masterful dialogue between divine transcendence and divine closeness: Allah is beyond human comprehension, yet closer to us than our jugular vein.

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ

---

### 42:1–7 — The Opening Letters & Purpose of Revelation

**Arabic:**

> حم ﴿١﴾ عسق ﴿٢﴾ كَذَٰلِكَ يُوحِي إِلَيْكَ وَإِلَى الَّذِينَ مِن قَبْلِكَ اللَّهُ الْعَزِيزُ الْحَكِيمُ ﴿٣﴾ لَهُ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَمَا فِي الْأَرْضِ ۖ وَهُوَ الْعَلِيُّ الْعَظِيمُ ﴿٤﴾

**Translation (Saheeh International):**

> Ḥā Mīm. ʿAyn Sīn Qāf. Thus does Allah, the Exalted in Might, the Wise, send revelation to you, [O Muhammad], and to those before you. To Him belongs what is in the heavens and what is in the earth, and He is the Most High, the Most Great.

**Explanation:**

The five mysterious letters Ḥā Mīm ʿAyn Sīn Qāf immediately arrest attention. The scholars are unanimous that their precise meaning is known only to Allah, but their function is clear: they challenge the Arab masters of eloquence — this Book, composed of the very letters they know, is far beyond human authorship. The revelation is then framed as continuous: the same divine message given to Muhammad ﷺ was given to the prophets before him. Revelation is not a new phenomenon — it is Allah's perpetual act of mercy toward humanity.

**Scholar Callouts:**

> **📚 Ibn Kathir:** Ibn Kathir notes that the combination of five letters here is unique in the Qur'an, found only in this surah. He cites Ibn Abbas's view that these letters are among the names of Allah, assembled as a divine challenge to those who denied the Qur'an's miraculous origin.

> **📚 Tafsir Al-Jalalayn:** Al-Jalalayn connects the phrase "to you and to those before you" as establishing the unity of prophetic mission — every messenger received inspiration from the same Source, the All-Mighty, the All-Wise. This dismantles any claim that Islam is a new or foreign religion.

**✦ Key Lessons:**

- 🔤 The Muqatta'at remind us that Allah's Word transcends human categories — we submit to what we cannot fully decode.
- 🕊️ All prophets received the same essential message: Tawhid (divine oneness), moral uprightness, and accountability.
- 👑 True authority (*al-ʿAliyy al-ʿAẓīm*) belongs to Allah — no earthly power shares in this reality.

### 42:11 — The Incomparable Creator

**Arabic:**

> فَاطِرُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ ۚ جَعَلَ لَكُم مِّنْ أَنفُسِكُمْ أَزْوَاجًا وَمِنَ الْأَنْعَامِ أَزْوَاجًا ۖ يَذْرَؤُكُمْ فِيهِ ۚ لَيْسَ كَمِثْلِهِ شَيْءٌ ۖ وَهُوَ السَّمِيعُ الْبَصِيرُ ﴿١١﴾

**Translation (Saheeh International):**

> [He is] Creator of the heavens and the earth. He has made for you from yourselves, mates, and among the cattle, mates; He multiplies you thereby. There is nothing like unto Him, and He is the All-Hearing, the All-Seeing.

**Explanation:**

*Laysa ka-mithlihi shay'* — "There is nothing like unto Him" — is the cornerstone verse of Islamic theology regarding Allah's attributes. Known in Aqeedah discussions as the verse of *Tanzih* (divine transcendence), it affirms that while Allah has names and attributes (Hearing, Seeing, Knowing, Living…), none of them resemble in any way the attributes of His creation. This verse is the cure for both *ta'til* (denial of attributes) and *tashbih* (likening Allah to creation).

**Scholar Callouts:**

> **📚 Tafsir As-Sa'di:** As-Sa'di beautifully explains that the verse first establishes divine incomparability, then immediately affirms two attributes: *al-Samīʿ* (All-Hearing) and *al-Baṣīr* (All-Seeing). This sequence is deliberate — Allah is unlike creation, yet He hears and sees in a real, perfect way befitting His Majesty. We affirm the attribute while negating any resemblance to created hearing or sight.

> **📚 Ibn Kathir:** Ibn Kathir cites Imam Ahmad's famous response to theological debates: "He described Himself as Hearing and Seeing — so we affirm what He affirmed without asking 'how?', and we negate what He negated without falling into likening Him to anything."

**✦ Key Lessons:**

- 🌌 Allah's essence is utterly unique — no analogy, metaphor, or mental image can capture who He is.
- 👂 Yet He is not absent — He hears our prayers and sees our struggles in a way perfect and befitting His Majesty.
- 🧬 The system of reproduction and pairing in creation reflects divine wisdom and mercy, not randomness.

### 42:13 — The Unified Religion of the Prophets

**Arabic:**

> شَرَعَ لَكُم مِّنَ الدِّينِ مَا وَصَّىٰ بِهِ نُوحًا وَالَّذِي أَوْحَيْنَا إِلَيْكَ وَمَا وَصَّيْنَا بِهِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَمُوسَىٰ وَعِيسَىٰ ۖ أَنْ أَقِيمُوا الدِّينَ وَلَا تَتَفَرَّقُوا فِيهِ ﴿١٣﴾

**Translation (Saheeh International):**

> He has ordained for you of religion what He enjoined upon Noah and that which We have revealed to you, [O Muhammad], and what We enjoined upon Abraham and Moses and Jesus — to establish the religion and not be divided therein.

**Explanation:**

This is one of the most powerful verses for understanding Islam's self-concept. The *Deen* given to Muhammad ﷺ is identical in essence to what was given to Noah, Abraham, Moses, and Jesus — complete submission to the one God, moral living, and community without schism. Scholars note the five prophets mentioned are the *Ulu al-ʿAzm* — the prophets of immense determination who brought the greatest messages in human history.

**Scholar Callouts:**

> **📚 Tafsir As-Sa'di:** As-Sa'di explains that while the *Sharia* (specific laws) differed between prophets — dietary laws, ritual forms, etc. — the *Usul* (foundational principles) of every prophet's message were identical: Tawhid, sincerity in worship, moral integrity, accountability. This verse refutes any claim that Islam introduced something foreign to the Abrahamic tradition.

> **📚 Ibn Kathir:** Ibn Kathir emphasises "and do not be divided therein" as a direct prohibition of sectarianism. He notes that division in religion was the great failure of previous communities — the Muslims are warned not to repeat this error. Unity in the essentials is obligatory; difference in subsidiary matters is acceptable but should never lead to enmity.

**✦ Key Lessons:**

- 🤝 Islam is not a new religion — it is the completion and preservation of the one eternal message.
- ⚓ The five great prophets — Noah, Abraham, Moses, Jesus, Muhammad ﷺ — shared the same fundamental call.
- 🚫 Division and sectarianism in religion is explicitly forbidden — unity is a divine command.
- 📖 Islam respects the original teachings of all previous prophets, while acknowledging the final scripture's preservation.

### 42:23 — The Prophet's Reward: Love of the Family

**Arabic:**

> قُل لَّا أَسْأَلُكُمْ عَلَيْهِ أَجْرًا إِلَّا الْمَوَدَّةَ فِي الْقُرْبَىٰ ۗ وَمَن يَقْتَرِفْ حَسَنَةً نَّزِدْ لَهُ فِيهَا حُسْنًا ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ غَفُورٌ شَكُورٌ ﴿٢٣﴾

**Translation (Saheeh International):**

> Say, "I do not ask you for this message any payment [but] only good will through kinship." And whoever commits a good deed — We will increase for him good therein. Indeed, Allah is Forgiving and Appreciative.

**Explanation:**

The Prophet ﷺ is commanded to declare that his only "reward" for delivering this message is that his people maintain bonds of kinship and love. This verse is famous for inspiring the Islamic emphasis on *silat al-rahm* (maintaining family ties). It also contains the profound promise that even one sincere good deed draws divine grace that multiplies the deed manifold. Allah's name here — *al-Shakūr* (the Appreciative) — is especially tender: the Creator thanks His servants for their small acts of goodness.

**Scholar Callouts:**

> **📚 Ibn Kathir:** Ibn Kathir records that some scholars understood "love of kinship" (*mawadda fil-qurba*) as referring to loving the Prophet's family (Ahlul Bayt) specifically. Others, including Ibn Abbas, said it means: "Do not harm me through your mutual animosity — maintain goodness in your familial ties." Both meanings are complementary and carry great importance.

> **📚 Tafsir Al-Jalalayn:** Al-Jalalayn notes that Allah describing Himself as *al-Shakūr* (the Appreciative) in this context is particularly moving — the Creator of the universe thanks His servants for their small acts of goodness and rewards them abundantly. No deed done sincerely for Allah is ever wasted.

**✦ Key Lessons:**

- ❤️ The Prophet ﷺ asked for no worldly reward — his mission was entirely for our salvation, not personal gain.
- 👨‍👩‍👧 Maintaining family ties (*silat al-rahm*) is so beloved to Allah it is linked to love of the Prophet himself.
- 🌱 Allah is *al-Shakūr* — He multiplies every good deed and never forgets even a moment of sincerity.

### 42:36–39 — The Qualities of the True Believers & Shura

**Arabic:**

> فَمَا أُوتِيتُم مِّن شَيْءٍ فَمَتَاعُ الْحَيَاةِ الدُّنْيَا ۖ وَمَا عِندَ اللَّهِ خَيْرٌ وَأَبْقَىٰ لِلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَلَىٰ رَبِّهِمْ يَتَوَكَّلُونَ ﴿٣٦﴾ وَالَّذِينَ يَجْتَنِبُونَ كَبَائِرَ الْإِثْمِ وَالْفَوَاحِشَ وَإِذَا مَا غَضِبُوا هُمْ يَغْفِرُونَ ﴿٣٧﴾ وَالَّذِينَ اسْتَجَابُوا لِرَبِّهِمْ وَأَقَامُوا الصَّلَاةَ وَأَمْرُهُمْ شُورَىٰ بَيْنَهُمْ وَمِمَّا رَزَقْنَاهُمْ يُنفِقُونَ ﴿٣٨﴾

**Translation (Saheeh International):**

> Whatever thing you have been given — it is but [for] enjoyment of worldly life. But what is with Allah is better and more lasting for those who have believed and upon their Lord rely. And those who avoid the major sins and immoralities, and when they are angry, they forgive. And those who have responded to their Lord and established prayer and whose affair is [determined by] consultation among themselves, and from what We have provided them they spend.

**Explanation:**

These four verses paint a portrait of the ideal believing community — one that prioritises the eternal over the temporary, exercises self-restraint, forgives easily, prays consistently, consults in communal affairs, and gives generously. The word *shura* (consultation) in v. 38 gave this surah its name and established a foundational principle of Islamic governance: community decisions should not be dictatorial but should emerge from deliberation, wisdom-sharing, and collective counsel.

**Scholar Callouts:**

> **📚 Tafsir As-Sa'di:** As-Sa'di beautifully unpacks v. 37: "When they are angry, they forgive." He says this represents the highest virtue — not the inability to get angry, but the mature capacity to choose forgiveness even when anger is justified. This is the opposite of the tyrant who acts on every impulse of rage. The believer's power lies in restraint and mercy.

> **📚 Ibn Kathir:** On shura, Ibn Kathir cites the example of the Prophet ﷺ who regularly consulted his companions even though he received revelation — showing that consultation is not a sign of weakness but of wisdom and respect. The great battles of Badr, Uhud, and the Trench all involved extensive prophetic shura with the believers.

> **📚 Tafsir Al-Jalalayn:** Al-Jalalayn notes the pairing of Salah (prayer — the individual's vertical relationship with Allah) immediately with Shura (consultation — the community's horizontal relationships). Both are essential; the believer who neglects either is incomplete in their practice of the Deen.

**✦ Key Lessons:**

- 🌍 Worldly possessions are temporary enjoyments — the true investment is in deeds that benefit us in the Hereafter.
- 😤 Forgiveness when you have the power to retaliate is one of the greatest qualities a believer can cultivate.
- 🗳️ Shura (consultation) is a Qur'anic principle — good leadership involves collective wisdom, not autocracy.
- 🕌 Prayer and charity are constants of a believer's life — not seasonal acts but perpetual habits.

### 42:51–53 — The Three Modes of Divine Communication

**Arabic:**

> وَمَا كَانَ لِبَشَرٍ أَن يُكَلِّمَهُ اللَّهُ إِلَّا وَحْيًا أَوْ مِن وَرَاءِ حِجَابٍ أَوْ يُرْسِلَ رَسُولًا فَيُوحِيَ بِإِذْنِهِ مَا يَشَاءُ ۚ إِنَّهُ عَلِيٌّ حَكِيمٌ ﴿٥١﴾

**Translation (Saheeh International):**

> And it is not for any human being that Allah should speak to him except by revelation or from behind a partition or that He sends a messenger to reveal, by His permission, what He wills. Indeed, He is Most High and Wise.

**Explanation:**

This theologically rich verse describes the three channels through which Allah communicates with His servants: (1) direct inspiration in the heart (*wahī*) — like the Prophet's heart receiving sudden divine understanding; (2) from behind a veil — as with Moses who heard Allah speaking directly at the Sacred Valley; (3) through an angelic messenger — as Jibreel (Gabriel) brought the Qur'an to Muhammad ﷺ. This verse closes the surah by reinforcing that all revelation comes from the Most High — elevating and wise in how He chooses to communicate.

**Scholar Callouts:**

> **📚 Ibn Kathir:** Ibn Kathir documents that the Prophet ﷺ described revelation as sometimes coming like the ringing of a bell — the most intense form — and sometimes as a man speaking to him directly. Both are covered by this verse. The "veil" mode is exclusive in its most direct sense to Moses, upon him be peace, who spoke with Allah directly on Mount Sinai.

> **📚 Tafsir As-Sa'di:** As-Sa'di concludes that this verse is a perfect close to Ash-Shura — the surah began by establishing that Allah reveals to the prophets, and ends by clarifying exactly how that revelation occurs. The closing attributes — *ʿAliyy* (Most High) and *Ḥakīm* (Most Wise) — confirm that every choice of how and when and to whom Allah communicates is an expression of absolute wisdom.

**✦ Key Lessons:**

- 📡 Revelation is a real, precise, categorised phenomenon — not vague spiritual feeling or hallucination.
- 🌟 Moses' unique status as *Kalimullah* (the one whom Allah spoke to directly) is affirmed and honoured.
- 🦅 The angel Jibreel was the primary vehicle for transmitting the Qur'an — his role was one of the greatest in creation.

## Surah 43 · Az-Zukhruf — "The Ornaments of Gold"

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Meccan |
| **Total Verses** | 89 |
| **This Juz** | Verses 1–89 (complete) |

Az-Zukhruf — named after the gold ornaments (*zukhruf*) that the Quraysh idolaters valued over truth — is a profound meditation on how material obsession blinds people to divine guidance. The surah revisits the stories of the people of Abraham and Moses, examines the perverse logic of those who despised the Prophet for not being "rich enough," and ends with a vision of paradise and a final admonition to hold firm to revelation.

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ

---

### 43:1–4 — The Qur'an: Written in the Preserved Tablet

**Arabic:**

> حم ﴿١﴾ وَالْكِتَابِ الْمُبِينِ ﴿٢﴾ إِنَّا جَعَلْنَاهُ قُرْآنًا عَرَبِيًّا لَّعَلَّكُمْ تَعْقِلُونَ ﴿٣﴾ وَإِنَّهُ فِي أُمِّ الْكِتَابِ لَدَيْنَا لَعَلِيٌّ حَكِيمٌ ﴿٤﴾

**Translation (Saheeh International):**

> Ḥā Mīm. By the clear Book, Indeed, We have made it an Arabic Qur'an that you might understand. And indeed it is, in the Mother of the Book with Us, exalted and full of wisdom.

**Explanation:**

*Umm al-Kitab* — "the Mother of the Book" — refers to *al-Lawh al-Mahfuz*, the Preserved Tablet in which all of Allah's decrees and all divine scriptures are recorded before their earthly revelation. The Qur'an existed in this elevated, pristine form long before the words descended into human language. The choice of Arabic was deliberate and merciful — so that those who first received it could fully grasp and apply it. "Exalted" and "full of wisdom" describe the Qur'an's essential character: nobility of status and perfection of guidance.

**Scholar Callouts:**

> **📚 Ibn Kathir:** Ibn Kathir explains that "*al-ʿAliyy al-Ḥakīm*" as attributes of the Qur'an — not of Allah — means the Book itself is exalted in status above all other speech, and its content is profoundly wise, never contradicting itself, never erring in any ruling or narrative.

> **📚 Tafsir As-Sa'di:** As-Sa'di notes the extraordinary honour in "*lada-yna*" (with Us) — the Qur'an's original form is preserved with Allah Himself, making it the most protected book in existence. No human corruption can reach the original, no matter what changes enemies might introduce to earthly copies.

**✦ Key Lessons:**

- 📜 The Qur'an was decreed and written in divine knowledge before creation — it is not a product of human history.
- 🌐 Arabic was chosen to make the message accessible — clarity and comprehension are divine priorities.
- 🛡️ The Preserved Tablet is the ultimate protection of the Qur'an — no power can corrupt the original divine text.

### 43:26–28 — Ibrahim's Declaration of Tawhid

**Arabic:**

> وَإِذْ قَالَ إِبْرَاهِيمُ لِأَبِيهِ وَقَوْمِهِ إِنَّنِي بَرَاءٌ مِّمَّا تَعْبُدُونَ ﴿٢٦﴾ إِلَّا الَّذِي فَطَرَنِي فَإِنَّهُ سَيَهْدِينِ ﴿٢٧﴾ وَجَعَلَهَا كَلِمَةً بَاقِيَةً فِي عَقِبِهِ لَعَلَّهُمْ يَرْجِعُونَ ﴿٢٨﴾

**Translation (Saheeh International):**

> And [mention] when Abraham said to his father and his people, "Indeed, I am disassociated from that which you worship. Except for He who created me; and indeed, He will guide me." And he made it a word remaining among his descendants that they might return [to it].

**Explanation:**

Ibrahim's declaration — "I am disassociated from all that you worship, except the One who created me" — is the very essence of the Islamic creed: *al-bara' min al-shirk* (disavowal of polytheism) paired with *al-iman bi-Allah* (faith in Allah alone). His confidence in "He will guide me" shows that the first step — sincerely turning to Allah — draws divine guidance in return. The word "remaining" (*baqiyatan*) in v. 28 indicates that Tawhid, declared by Ibrahim, became the legacy of his descendants, including the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ who came from his lineage.

**Scholar Callouts:**

> **📚 Tafsir As-Sa'di:** As-Sa'di considers v. 28 one of the most hope-filled verses for the Quraysh, who were the descendants of Ibrahim. Allah says this legacy was left among them "so that they might return" — meaning the very polytheists to whom the Qur'an was first sent had the lineage and the spiritual DNA of Tawhid within them. The message was a call home, not an alien imposition.

> **📚 Ibn Kathir:** Ibn Kathir identifies the "word remaining" as the testimony of *La ilaha illa Allah* — "There is no god but Allah" — which Ibrahim established in his lineage and which the Prophet ﷺ came to revive and perfect after centuries of deviation.

**✦ Key Lessons:**

- 🔥 Ibrahim's courage in rejecting his entire community's beliefs models the believer's willingness to stand alone for truth.
- 🧭 "He will guide me" — sincerity in turning to Allah is the guaranteed path to divine guidance.
- 🌳 Good words and righteous legacies outlive their speakers — Ibrahim's declaration echoes in every mosque today.

### 43:67–73 — True Friendship in the Hereafter

**Arabic:**

> الْأَخِلَّاءُ يَوْمَئِذٍ بَعْضُهُمْ لِبَعْضٍ عَدُوٌّ إِلَّا الْمُتَّقِينَ ﴿٦٧﴾ يَا عِبَادِ لَا خَوْفٌ عَلَيْكُمُ الْيَوْمَ وَلَا أَنتُمْ تَحْزَنُونَ ﴿٦٨﴾ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا بِآيَاتِنَا وَكَانُوا مُسْلِمِينَ ﴿٦٩﴾

**Translation (Saheeh International):**

> Close friends, that Day, will be enemies to each other, except for the righteous. [To whom Allah will say], "O My servants, no fear will there be concerning you this Day, nor will you grieve — [You] who believed in Our verses and were Muslims."

**Explanation:**

Every worldly friendship built on shared sins, shared falsehood, or mutual love of the *dunya* will transform into mutual enmity on the Day of Judgement. The only exception: friends who loved each other for the sake of Allah and righteousness — their bond survives the End of Days. Then comes the most tender of divine addresses: "O My servants, no fear and no grief upon you today." This is the greeting the believers will receive — an assurance that their eternal life will be free from anxiety and sorrow.

**Scholar Callouts:**

> **📚 Tafsir As-Sa'di:** As-Sa'di writes that this verse should make every Muslim carefully audit their friendships. Who are we spending our time with? Are our companions helping us come closer to Allah or drawing us further away? Friendships are investments — on the Day of Judgement, you will either be elevated or burdened by those you chose.

> **📚 Ibn Kathir:** Ibn Kathir records a saying of Ali ibn Abi Talib that the verse "O My servants, no fear will there be concerning you" is the most hopeful verse in the Qur'an for the believers — it is Allah Himself addressing His servants with safety and honour in the most terrifying moment ever to occur.

**✦ Key Lessons:**

- 👥 Choose your companions wisely — on Judgement Day, the wrong friends become enemies.
- 💚 Friendship for Allah's sake is the only bond that survives death and brings benefit in the next life.
- 🌸 "No fear, no grief" — the ultimate emotional freedom awaits the believers who were patient in this world.

## Surah 44 · Ad-Dukhan — "The Smoke"

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Meccan |
| **Total Verses** | 59 |
| **This Juz** | Verses 1–59 (complete) |

Ad-Dukhan is one of the surahs that the Prophet ﷺ recommended reciting on Friday nights, describing it as having "seventy thousand angels seeking forgiveness for its reciter until morning." The surah centres on the revelation of the Qur'an on a blessed night, the sign of a great Smoke that will precede the Day of Judgement, and the story of Pharaoh as a warning to those who persist in arrogance.

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ

---

### 44:1–6 — The Blessed Night of the Qur'an's Descent

**Arabic:**

> حم ﴿١﴾ وَالْكِتَابِ الْمُبِينِ ﴿٢﴾ إِنَّا أَنزَلْنَاهُ فِي لَيْلَةٍ مُّبَارَكَةٍ ۚ إِنَّا كُنَّا مُنذِرِينَ ﴿٣﴾ فِيهَا يُفْرَقُ كُلُّ أَمْرٍ حَكِيمٍ ﴿٤﴾ أَمْرًا مِّنْ عِندِنَا ۚ إِنَّا كُنَّا مُرْسِلِينَ ﴿٥﴾ رَحْمَةً مِّن رَّبِّكَ ۚ إِنَّهُ هُوَ السَّمِيعُ الْعَلِيمُ ﴿٦﴾

**Translation (Saheeh International):**

> Ḥā Mīm. By the clear Book. Indeed, We sent it down during a blessed night. Indeed, We were to warn [mankind]. On that night is made distinct every precise matter — [Every] matter [proceeding] from Us. Indeed, We were to send [a messenger] as mercy from your Lord. Indeed, He is the All-Hearing, the All-Knowing.

**Explanation:**

"The blessed night" is *Laylat al-Qadr* — the Night of Power in Ramadan. The Qur'an began its descent on this night from the Preserved Tablet to the lowest heaven (*bayt al-ʿizza*), after which it was revealed progressively to the Prophet ﷺ over 23 years. On this night, the decrees for the coming year are "distinguished" — angels descend with the decisions of Allah regarding rain, life, death, provision, and all affairs until the next such night. The purpose? Mercy — not punishment, not mere law, but an act of divine compassion toward a stumbling humanity.

**Scholar Callouts:**

> **📚 Ibn Kathir:** Ibn Kathir affirms the position of the majority of scholars that the "blessed night" is *Laylat al-Qadr* in Ramadan, harmonising this with Surah Al-Qadr (97) and Al-Baqarah (2:185). He also addresses the alternate view (held by some early scholars) that it is the 15th of Sha'ban, but concludes the stronger position is that it refers to Ramadan's Night of Power.

> **📚 Tafsir As-Sa'di:** As-Sa'di reflects on the astonishing mercy of this night — that on a single night, every decree for the year is finalised and set in motion. He encourages the believer to approach *Laylat al-Qadr* not with mere ritual but with genuine supplication, knowing that one's *rizq* (provision), lifespan, and major events of the coming year may be being written as they pray.

**✦ Key Lessons:**

- 🌙 *Laylat al-Qadr* is when annual decrees are finalised — make your du'a and worship on this night exceptional.
- 📖 The Qur'an's descent on this night links the Book forever with the most blessed night of the Islamic year.
- 💖 Revelation is described as "mercy" — every word of the Qur'an is an act of love from the Creator to His creation.
- 📅 Ramadan is not just a month of fasting — it is the month of divine decrees and the most intensive spiritual opportunity of the year.

### 44:10–16 — The Great Smoke: A Sign Before the Hour

**Arabic:**

> فَارْتَقِبْ يَوْمَ تَأْتِي السَّمَاءُ بِدُخَانٍ مُّبِينٍ ﴿١٠﴾ يَغْشَى النَّاسَ ۖ هَٰذَا عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌ ﴿١١﴾ رَّبَّنَا اكْشِفْ عَنَّا الْعَذَابَ إِنَّا مُؤْمِنُونَ ﴿١٢﴾

**Translation (Saheeh International):**

> Then watch for the Day when the sky will bring a visible smoke. Covering the people; this is a painful torment. [They will say], "Our Lord, remove from us the torment; indeed, we are believers."

**Explanation:**

The great Smoke (*Dukhan*) is one of the ten Major Signs of the Hour recorded in prophetic tradition (alongside the Mahdi, Dajjal, the descent of Jesus, the rising of the sun from the west, the Beast, and others). The Qur'an describes it as a visible phenomenon that will envelope humanity — so severe that people will cry out for mercy. Tragically, their faith at that moment — forced by the sight of punishment — will be of no spiritual benefit, for that moment has passed the threshold where sincere free-choice belief was possible.

**Scholar Callouts:**

> **📚 Ibn Kathir:** Ibn Kathir quotes the hadith in Sahih Muslim where the Prophet ﷺ described the Dukhan as one of the ten major signs before the Day of Judgement. He also records that Ibn Masud and others interpreted this initially as referring to the severe famine that struck Mecca during the Prophet's time — where people saw smoke before their eyes due to extreme hunger — though the primary meaning is eschatological.

> **📚 Tafsir As-Sa'di:** As-Sa'di uses this passage to draw an important practical lesson: deathbed repentance and last-moment faith are often not accepted because they lack the element of free, informed choice. Just as Pharaoh's declaration of faith at the moment of drowning was rejected, faith forced by visible punishment will be too. The time to believe is now.

**✦ Key Lessons:**

- 💨 The Dukhan (Smoke) is a confirmed major sign of the approaching Hour — the Qur'an and Sunnah both affirm it.
- ⏰ Believing before the Signs appear is what counts — faith forced by visible punishment comes too late.
- 🙏 The habit of sincere du'a — "Our Lord, remove from us the torment" — should be cultivated now, not saved for crises.

### 44:43–57 — The Tree of Zaqqum and the Gardens of Paradise

**Arabic:**

> إِنَّ شَجَرَتَ الزَّقُّومِ ﴿٤٣﴾ طَعَامُ الْأَثِيمِ ﴿٤٤﴾ كَالْمُهْلِ يَغْلِي فِي الْبُطُونِ ﴿٤٥﴾ إِنَّ الْمُتَّقِينَ فِي مَقَامٍ أَمِينٍ ﴿٥١﴾ فِي جَنَّاتٍ وَعُيُونٍ ﴿٥٢﴾ يَلْبَسُونَ مِن سُندُسٍ وَإِسْتَبْرَقٍ مُّتَقَابِلِينَ ﴿٥٣﴾

**Translation (Saheeh International):**

> Indeed, the tree of Zaqqum is food for the sinful. Like murky oil, it boils within bellies … Indeed, the righteous will be in a secure place — in gardens and springs, wearing [garments of] fine silk and brocade, facing each other.

**Explanation:**

In powerful contrast, the surah describes two eternal realities side by side: the agony of the wicked eating from Zaqqum (a tree of boiling, bitter fruit described as growing "from the bottom of the Hellfire"), and the bliss of the righteous in gardens of peace. The contrast is deliberately sharp — the same person cannot have both; every choice made in this life is casting a vote for one destination or the other. The description of paradise with flowing water, silk garments, and the companionship of purified spouses is designed to make the believer's heart long for what is coming.

**Scholar Callouts:**

> **📚 Ibn Kathir:** Ibn Kathir records the confusion of the Quraysh idolaters when the Zaqqum tree was mentioned — they mockingly said, "How can a tree grow in fire?" Ibn Abbas reported that Allah revealed in response that this is a tree unlike anything in the *dunya* — created specifically for the Hellfire and sustained by a reality beyond our physical laws.

> **📚 Tafsir Al-Jalalayn:** Al-Jalalayn comments on "*maqam ameen*" (a secure place) for the righteous — every dimension of paradise is characterised by security. No fear of loss, no anxiety about the future, no envy, no disease. *Ameen* here conveys an all-encompassing safety that the human soul has always craved and never fully found on earth.

**✦ Key Lessons:**

- 🌳 The Zaqqum tree is a real, supernatural creation in the Hellfire — not a metaphor. The Qur'an uses it to make the consequences of sin visceral and real.
- 🌊 Paradise is described with sensory detail because Allah knows the human heart needs hope it can feel and imagine.
- ⚖️ The contrast technique — Hell then Heaven — is a Qur'anic literary device that forces the reader to choose a side consciously.
- 🕊️ "*Maqam ameen*" — a place of complete security — is what every anxious human heart is truly longing for.

## Surah 45 · Al-Jathiyah — "The Crouching"

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Meccan |
| **Total Verses** | 37 |
| **This Juz** | Verses 1–37 (complete) |

Al-Jathiyah — "the crouching" — takes its name from the image in verse 28: every nation will be seen crouching on its knees before Allah on the Day of Judgement. This powerful image frames the entire surah, which opens with an appeal to signs in the natural world as proofs of divine power, then addresses the crime of following desires over revealed guidance, and closes with a majestic declaration of Allah's sovereignty. It is the fifth consecutive Ḥā Mīm surah (41–46), known collectively as the Ḥawāmīm.

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ

---

### 45:1–6 — Creation as a Book of Signs

**Arabic:**

> حم ﴿١﴾ تَنزِيلُ الْكِتَابِ مِنَ اللَّهِ الْعَزِيزِ الْحَكِيمِ ﴿٢﴾ إِنَّ فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ لَآيَاتٍ لِّلْمُؤْمِنِينَ ﴿٣﴾ وَفِي خَلْقِكُمْ وَمَا يَبُثُّ مِن دَابَّةٍ آيَاتٌ لِّقَوْمٍ يُوقِنُونَ ﴿٤﴾ وَاخْتِلَافِ اللَّيْلِ وَالنَّهَارِ وَمَا أَنزَلَ اللَّهُ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ مِن رِّزْقٍ فَأَحْيَا بِهِ الْأَرْضَ بَعْدَ مَوْتِهَا وَتَصْرِيفِ الرِّيَاحِ آيَاتٌ لِّقَوْمٍ يَعْقِلُونَ ﴿٥﴾

**Translation (Saheeh International):**

> Ḥā Mīm. The revelation of the Book is from Allah, the Exalted in Might, the Wise. Indeed, within the heavens and earth are signs for the believers. And in the creation of yourselves and what He disperses of moving creatures are signs for people who are certain [in faith]. And in the alternation of night and day and [in] what Allah sends down from the sky of provision and gives life thereby to the earth after its lifelessness and [in His] directing of the winds are signs for a people who reason.

**Explanation:**

In four verses, the Qur'an describes a universe alive with divine communication. The heavens, the earth, the human body, the animals, the rotation of night and day, the rain reviving a dead earth, the behaviour of winds — all are *ayat* (signs/verses). Notice the three different audiences: the believers (who see through faith), the certain (who look with conviction), and those who reason (who think logically). The signs of Allah speak different languages — to the spiritual, the experiential, and the intellectual alike.

**Scholar Callouts:**

> **📚 Tafsir As-Sa'di:** As-Sa'di calls this passage a curriculum in Islamic epistemology — knowledge of Allah through His creation is not just permitted but commanded. The Qur'an never asks people to "just believe without thinking" — it directs them to observe, to reason, to reflect. The natural world is a second revelation alongside the Qur'anic text, and both point to the same Author.

> **📚 Ibn Kathir:** Ibn Kathir notes that the wind is singled out in v. 5 as a particular sign — it cannot be seen, only felt and observed in its effects. The wind pollinating plants, carrying rain clouds, cooling the earth — all without human engineering. Every time we feel a breeze, we are in the presence of a divine sign.

**✦ Key Lessons:**

- 🌍 The entire universe is a Qur'an made visible — creation and revelation are two books from the same Author.
- 🧠 Islam invites intellectual engagement with the natural world — observation and reasoning are acts of worship.
- 🌧️ Rain reviving dead soil is the Qur'an's favourite metaphor for resurrection — if Allah can do one, the other is easy.
- 💨 Every natural phenomenon — winds, tides, seasons — is in divine service; only humans are given the choice whether to recognise this.

---

### 45:18–21 — Divine Law vs Following Desires

**Arabic:**

> ثُمَّ جَعَلْنَاكَ عَلَىٰ شَرِيعَةٍ مِّنَ الْأَمْرِ فَاتَّبِعْهَا وَلَا تَتَّبِعْ أَهْوَاءَ الَّذِينَ لَا يَعْلَمُونَ ﴿١٨﴾ إِنَّهُمْ لَن يُغْنُوا عَنكَ مِنَ اللَّهِ شَيْئًا ۚ وَاللَّهُ وَلِيُّ الْمُتَّقِينَ ﴿١٩﴾

**Translation (Saheeh International):**

> Then We put you, [O Muhammad], on an ordained way concerning the matter [of religion]; so follow it and do not follow the inclinations of those who do not know. Indeed, they will never avail you against Allah at all. And indeed, the wrongdoers are allies of one another; but Allah is the Protector of the righteous.

**Explanation:**

*Sharia* literally means "a path to water" — the source of life. Allah tells the Prophet ﷺ (and by extension, all believers) that He has placed them on a clear, life-giving path. The temptation to abandon it for the "inclinations of those who do not know" — i.e., the whims of society, the fashions of culture, the desires of the ego — is a real and recurring test. The warning is stark: those who pressure you to abandon revelation will be of no use to you on the Day you stand before Allah alone.

**Scholar Callouts:**

> **📚 Tafsir As-Sa'di:** As-Sa'di explains that "those who do not know" are not ignorant in the academic sense — they may be intellectuals and leaders — but they lack knowledge of Allah, the purpose of life, and the reality of the Hereafter. Without this foundational knowledge, all other knowledge is like a sophisticated map with the destination removed.

> **📚 Tafsir Al-Jalalayn:** Al-Jalalayn notes the beautiful contrast: the wrongdoers have each other as allies (*awliya'*), forming a coalition of mutual rationalisation. But the righteous have Allah Himself as their *Wali* (Guardian, Protector, Friend). No alliance of humans compares to the protection of the Creator of the universe.

**✦ Key Lessons:**

- 🛤️ The Sharia is not a burden — it is literally a "path to water," a gift of guidance in a desert of confusion.
- 👥 Peer pressure from those who "do not know" (the purpose of life) is a recurring test for every believer in every era.
- 🛡️ "Allah is the Wali of the Muttaqeen" — the God-conscious have the ultimate protector; they need not fear any earthly alliance.

### 45:23–25 — The One Who Takes Desire as His God

**Arabic:**

> أَفَرَأَيْتَ مَنِ اتَّخَذَ إِلَٰهَهُ هَوَاهُ وَأَضَلَّهُ اللَّهُ عَلَىٰ عِلْمٍ وَخَتَمَ عَلَىٰ سَمْعِهِ وَقَلْبِهِ وَجَعَلَ عَلَىٰ بَصَرِهِ غِشَاوَةً فَمَن يَهْدِيهِ مِن بَعْدِ اللَّهِ ۚ أَفَلَا تَذَكَّرُونَ ﴿٢٣﴾

**Translation (Saheeh International):**

> Have you seen he who has taken as his god his [own] desire, and Allah has sent him astray due to knowledge and has set a seal upon his hearing and his heart and put over his vision a veil? So who will guide him after Allah? Then will you not be reminded?

**Explanation:**

One of the most psychologically profound verses in the Qur'an. The one who "takes desire as his god" is not necessarily an atheist — he may nominally believe in Allah — but in practice, whatever his desires dictate becomes his governing principle. Halal and haram are overridden by "what I feel like." This is a subtle but real form of shirk — placing the *nafs* (ego) in the position that belongs to Allah. The consequence: Allah seals his hearing (he can't absorb guidance), his heart (he can't feel it), and covers his sight (he can't see the truth). Who can reverse this divine sealing?

**Scholar Callouts:**

> **📚 Tafsir As-Sa'di:** As-Sa'di calls this "the most dangerous idol" — the idol of the self. External idols can be seen and destroyed; the idol of desire is invisible, internal, and rationalised. Many people who would never prostrate before a statue have made their own whims the final authority in their lives — which is precisely what this verse describes.

> **📚 Ibn Kathir:** Ibn Kathir quotes the powerful saying of Imam Al-Shafi'i: "The most difficult thing I have struggled against is my own *nafs* — it opposes me sometimes and sometimes it agrees with me." The greatest jihad is the war against the enslaving power of unrestrained desire.

**✦ Key Lessons:**

- ⚠️ Making one's own desires the ultimate authority — over revelation — is a subtle, modern, and deeply dangerous form of shirk.
- 🔒 Persistently ignoring divine guidance leads to a heart that literally cannot receive guidance anymore — a terrifying spiritual state.
- 🧎 The *nafs* (ego) is the hardest of all idols to defeat — it requires daily discipline, prayer, and sincere seeking of divine help.
- 🤲 Asking Allah for guidance (*Hidayah*) daily in Al-Fatihah is our protection against the sealed heart described in this verse.

### 45:27–28 — Every Nation Crouching: The Day of Reckoning

**Arabic:**

> وَلِلَّهِ مُلْكُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ ۚ وَيَوْمَ تَقُومُ السَّاعَةُ يَوْمَئِذٍ يَخْسَرُ الْمُبْطِلُونَ ﴿٢٧﴾ وَتَرَىٰ كُلَّ أُمَّةٍ جَاثِيَةً ۚ كُلُّ أُمَّةٍ تُدْعَىٰ إِلَىٰ كِتَابِهَا ﴿٢٨﴾

**Translation (Saheeh International):**

> And to Allah belongs the dominion of the heavens and the earth. And the Day the Hour appears — that Day the falsifiers will lose. And you will see every nation kneeling [in submission]. Every nation will be called to its record.

**Explanation:**

The verse that names this surah: *Jathiya* — crouching, kneeling. Every community that ever existed — the powerful Roman Empire, the vast Mongol nation, every civilisation from the earliest humans to the last — will be on its knees before Allah on this Day. No nation will stand proud. No army, no empire, no economic power will matter. Each community will be called to its own record (*kitab*) — the comprehensive account of what they believed and how they acted. The "falsifiers" (*mubtilun*) are identified as the ultimate losers.

**Scholar Callouts:**

> **📚 Tafsir As-Sa'di:** As-Sa'di notes the powerful pedagogical use of the word "you will see" — as if addressing the Prophet ﷺ and every reader personally, inviting us to visualise this scene. If we could actually see every great nation brought to its knees, our relationship with *dunya* and *deen* would change overnight.

> **📚 Ibn Kathir:** Ibn Kathir comments that the phrase "every nation will be called to its record" refers to the Book of Deeds — the perfect, complete record kept by the angels. Not a single atom of good or evil will be missing. Nations are accountable collectively for the cultures they built and the values they institutionalised.

**✦ Key Lessons:**

- 👑 No empire survives eternity — every earthly power will kneel before the true Sovereign on the Final Day.
- 📋 Every community, every nation, every individual has a complete record — nothing is lost, nothing goes unaccounted.
- 🌐 National pride is fine in moderation, but the Qur'an repeatedly dismantles any notion that national identity exempts us from individual moral accountability.

### 45:36–37 — The Closing Glorification

**Arabic:**

> فَلِلَّهِ الْحَمْدُ رَبِّ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَرَبِّ الْأَرْضِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ ﴿٣٦﴾ وَلَهُ الْكِبْرِيَاءُ فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ ۖ وَهُوَ الْعَزِيزُ الْحَكِيمُ ﴿٣٧﴾

**Translation (Saheeh International):**

> So to Allah belongs [all] praise — Lord of the heavens and Lord of the earth, Lord of the worlds. And to Him belongs [all] grandeur within the heavens and the earth, and He is the Exalted in Might, the Wise.

**Explanation:**

Al-Jathiyah closes with two of the most majestic verses in the Qur'an — a doxology (hymn of praise) that summarises the theology of the entire juz. All praise — every act of appreciation, every recognition of beauty, every expression of gratitude — ultimately belongs to and flows from Allah. He is the Lord of the heavens, the earth, and every world and dimension of existence. Grandeur (*kibria'*) belongs to Him alone. This is the Qur'an's final word in Juz 25: sovereignty and wisdom are Allah's, and all praise returns to Him.

**Scholar Callouts:**

> **📚 Tafsir As-Sa'di:** As-Sa'di writes that the surah ending with "*Rabb al-ʿAlameen*" (Lord of all worlds) after depicting all nations on their knees is profoundly intentional. Having seen the full scope of human history and divine justice in the surah, the reader's heart should naturally arrive at this conclusion: all praise belongs to the One who governed all of this with perfect wisdom and justice.

> **📚 Ibn Kathir:** Ibn Kathir reflects on "*kibria'*" (grandeur) as an exclusive divine attribute — the Prophet ﷺ said in a Qudsi hadith: "Grandeur is My cloak and Majesty is My garment; whoever disputes Me in either, I will punish him." This closing verse affirms that *kibria'* belongs entirely to Allah in the heavens and on earth.

**✦ Key Lessons:**

- 🎵 All praise in existence originates from and returns to Allah — every beautiful thing we love is a reflection of His beauty.
- ♾️ "Lord of all worlds" — Allah's lordship is not limited to Earth or to humans; He governs every dimension of existence.
- 🌟 Ending Juz 25 with Tawhid and Tahmid (praise) is a reminder: every journey in the Qur'an brings us back to the same truth.
- 🤲 Begin and end every day with *al-Hamdulillah* — it is the proper orientation of a creation that has been given existence by the Most Generous.

## Overarching Themes of Juz 25

*From the closing of Fussilat to the majestic end of Al-Jathiyah, five great surahs weave together a unified tapestry of divine truth.*

| Theme | Description |
|---|---|
| 🔑 **Exclusive Divine Knowledge** | The unseen (*ghayb*), the Hour, the contents of the womb — only Allah holds these keys. Fussilat 47 establishes this as the juz opens. |
| 🕊️ **Unity of Prophetic Message** | Noah, Ibrahim, Moses, Jesus, Muhammad ﷺ — all preached the same Tawhid. Ash-Shura 13 and Az-Zukhruf 26–28 make this explicit. |
| 🗳️ **Shura — Consultative Leadership** | The believers govern themselves through mutual consultation (Ash-Shura 38). Autocracy is foreign to the Qur'anic model of leadership. |
| 🌌 **Creation as Living Signs** | Al-Jathiyah 3–5 invites believers to read the universe as a second revelation — every phenomenon is an *ayah* from the same Author. |
| ⚠️ **The Danger of Following Desires** | Al-Jathiyah 23 identifies the ego-deity as the most subtle and dangerous idol. Following desire over revelation is a form of shirk. |
| 🌙 **Laylat al-Qadr** | Ad-Dukhan 3–4 connects the Blessed Night to divine decrees for the year — making Ramadan's Night of Power a moment of cosmic significance. |
| 🌍 **Material Obsession Blinds** | Az-Zukhruf's "gold ornaments" theme warns that valuing wealth and status over truth leads to the same end as every arrogant nation before. |
| ⚖️ **Universal Accountability** | Al-Jathiyah 28: every nation — however powerful — will kneel before Allah. Individual and communal records are both reviewed and judged. |
| 🙌 **All Praise Returns to Allah** | The juz closes with *Fa-lillahil hamd* — having surveyed creation, revelation, justice, and mercy, the only fitting response is gratitude and praise. |
