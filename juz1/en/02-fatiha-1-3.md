# Al-Fatiha 1–3 · Basmalah, Praise & The Names of Allah

**Surah:** Al-Fatiha (1) · **Verses:** 1–3  
**Theme:** The opening praise — establishing who Allah is before we ask anything of Him

---

## Arabic Text

> بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ ﴿١﴾ الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ ﴿٢﴾ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ ﴿٣﴾

---

## Translation

> *In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful. (1) All praise is due to Allah, Lord of all the worlds. (2) The Most Gracious, the Most Merciful. (3)*

---

## Explanation

The surah opens with the *Basmalah* — the sacred formula **Bismillāh ir-Raḥmān ir-Raḥīm**. Three key names/attributes are presented:

- **Allāh** — the proper name of God. It is not derived from any descriptive quality but is the name He chose for Himself alone. No human, idol, or created being may share this name.
- **Ar-Raḥmān** (the Most Gracious) — His all-encompassing mercy that reaches every created being: Muslim, non-Muslim, animal, insect, even the smallest organism.
- **Ar-Raḥīm** (the Most Merciful) — the special, sustained mercy He reserves specifically for the believers in the Hereafter.

Verse 2 opens with **Al-Hamd** — complete, perfect praise — and assigns it solely to Allah. The word *Rabb* (Lord) is beautifully comprehensive: it means the One who creates, sustains, nurtures, manages, and perfects everything.

**Al-'Ālamīn** (all the worlds) is vast — scholars enumerate it as every type of creation: worlds of humans, jinn, angels, animals, plants, and galaxies beyond our knowledge.

The repetition of *ar-Raḥmān ar-Raḥīm* in verse 3, after it appeared in the Basmalah, is intentional emphasis — drilling into our hearts that the Lord of all creation is, above all else, defined by His mercy.

---

## Scholar Callouts

### 📖 Tafsir Ibn Kathir
Ibn Kathir explains that *Ar-Raḥmān* is a name exclusive to Allah and cannot be given to any created being, while *Ar-Raḥīm* can also describe a merciful human. He quotes the hadith:

> *"Allah has one hundred mercies, and He has sent down one of them between jinn, mankind, animals, and insects — by which they show affection to one another and by which wild animals care for their offspring. Allah has kept ninety-nine mercies with which He will show mercy to His servants on the Day of Resurrection."*

### 📖 Tafsir Al-Jalalayn
Al-Jalalayn notes that *Al-Hamd* encompasses both praise (*thanā'*) and gratitude (*shukr*). By attributing it exclusively to Allah, the verse establishes that every good, every blessing, every beauty in existence ultimately originates from Him — so all genuine praise belongs to Him alone.

### 📖 Tafsir As-Sa'di
Shaykh As-Sa'di beautifully observes that beginning the Qur'an with *Bismillah* teaches us that all important matters should begin with Allah's name. He also notes that the conjunction of *Rabb al-'ālamīn* with *Ar-Raḥmān* combines both the divine attribute of supreme power (lordship) and overflowing compassion — so we approach Allah neither through fear alone, nor through hope alone, but through **both together**.

---

## ✨ Key Lessons

- 🌿 Begin all important actions with *Bismillah* — this is a Sunnah that connects every deed to Allah.
- 🌊 Allah's mercy vastly outweighs His wrath — His mercy precedes and encompasses everything.
- 👑 *Rabb* means Allah is not just Creator but active Sustainer — He is involved in every moment of your life.
- 🌍 *Al-'Ālamīn* reminds us of our smallness and the vastness of Allah's dominion.
- 🙏 All praise belongs to Allah because He is the ultimate source of all good.
