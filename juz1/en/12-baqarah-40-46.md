# Al-Baqarah 40–46 · Children of Israel I — The Covenant Renewed

**Surah:** Al-Baqarah (2) · **Verses:** 40–46  
**Theme:** Allah's address to Banī Isrā'īl — reminding them of their covenant and pointing them to the Prophet ﷺ

---

## Arabic Text

> يَا بَنِي إِسْرَائِيلَ اذْكُرُوا نِعْمَتِيَ الَّتِي أَنْعَمْتُ عَلَيْكُمْ وَأَوْفُوا بِعَهْدِي أُوفِ بِعَهْدِكُمْ وَإِيَّايَ فَارْهَبُونِ ﴿٤٠﴾

---

## Translation

> *O Children of Israel, remember My favour which I have bestowed upon you and fulfil My covenant; I will fulfil your covenant — and be afraid of none but Me. (40)*

---

## Explanation

With verse 40, the Qur'an begins an extended address to Banī Isrā'īl that spans over 100 verses. Allah reminds them of an extraordinary history of divine favour: prophets, scriptures, miracles, and divine protection.

**The Covenant** referenced here is the covenant Allah made with Banī Isrā'īl: worship Allah alone, follow His prophets, establish justice. Allah promises to fulfil His side — guidance, victory, and the Hereafter — if they fulfil theirs. But their history is a story of repeatedly breaking this covenant.

**Verse 41** makes a pointed appeal: the Qur'an *confirms* the Torah — it does not contradict it. The Madinan Jews had prophecies of the coming prophet in their own scriptures. Selling those truths for "a small price" — positions of religious authority, financial gain, tribal loyalty — was a betrayal of their own heritage.

**The Prescription (2:45–46)**

> *"Seek help through patience and prayer; indeed, it is burdensome except for the humbly submissive — who are certain that they will meet their Lord and that they will return to Him."*

This is a universal divine prescription: when faced with hardship, the twin anchors are *ṣabr* (patience) and *ṣalāh* (prayer).

---

## Scholar Callouts

### 📖 Tafsir Ibn Kathir
Ibn Kathir explains that the long section on Banī Isrā'īl serves as a mirror for the Muslim community: as you read about their failures — breaking covenants, preferring worldly gain over truth, worshipping false gods after witnessing miracles — you are meant to ask yourself if you are doing the same. The Qur'an tells their story not to condemn a people but to warn all believers against the same traps.

### 📖 Tafsir Al-Jalalayn
On "selling My verses for a small price," Al-Jalalayn notes this refers to the rabbis who changed the description of the Prophet ﷺ in their scriptures to maintain their religious authority and income. They gained the world's approval and lost the Hereafter — the worst possible exchange.

### 📖 Tafsir As-Sa'di
As-Sa'di focuses on verses 45–46: patience grounds you in the present; prayer connects you to Allah for strength. The key is *yaqīn* — certainty that you will meet Allah. Without that certainty, neither patience nor prayer has deep roots.

---

## ✨ Key Lessons

- 📜 Covenants with Allah are serious — failing to honour them has consequences in this life and the next.
- 💸 Never trade truth for worldly gain — religious scholars especially carry a grave responsibility to speak truth regardless of cost.
- 🔮 The story of Banī Isrā'īl is a mirror — every sin they fell into is a sin Muslims can fall into too.
- 🧘 When life is hard: seek help through *ṣabr* and *ṣalāh* — this is a divine prescription.
- ⏳ Certainty in the meeting with Allah transforms how you live — it puts every test in perspective.
