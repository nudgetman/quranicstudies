# Al-Fatiha 6–7 · The Straight Path — The Ultimate Supplication

**Surah:** Al-Fatiha (1) · **Verses:** 6–7  
**Theme:** Asking Allah for guidance, and understanding the three paths humanity walks

---

## Arabic Text

> اهْدِنَا الصِّرَاطَ الْمُسْتَقِيمَ ﴿٦﴾ صِرَاطَ الَّذِينَ أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ غَيْرِ الْمَغْضُوبِ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا الضَّالِّينَ ﴿٧﴾

---

## Translation

> *Guide us to the straight path — (6) the path of those upon whom You have bestowed favour, not of those who have evoked anger, nor of those who are astray. (7)*

---

## Explanation

The surah climaxes with the most comprehensive supplication a human being can make: **"Guide us to the straight path."**

The Arabic word *ihdināa* is remarkably deep — it means not just "show us the path" but "grant us success on it, keep us firm upon it, enable us to traverse it fully." We are asking for guidance as a **continuous, living gift** — not a one-time event.

*As-ṣirāṭ al-mustaqīm* (the straight path) is then defined as the way of those whom Allah has blessed — the prophets, the truthful, the martyrs, the righteous (see 4:69). It is the path of correct belief (*aqīdah*), sincere worship, good character, and righteous deeds.

The final verse identifies **two paths to avoid**:

| Path | Arabic Term | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Those who earned anger | *al-maghḍūb 'alayhim* | Knew the truth but rejected and disobeyed it |
| Those who went astray | *aḍ-ḍāllīn* | Followed religion without proper knowledge, fell into deviation despite sincerity |

The congregation says *Āmeen* after this verse — meaning "O Allah, answer our supplication." The Prophet ﷺ said: *"When the imam says Āmeen, say Āmeen, for whoever's Āmeen coincides with the angels' Āmeen, all his past sins are forgiven."* (Bukhari)

---

## Scholar Callouts

### 📖 Tafsir Ibn Kathir
Ibn Kathir records the authentic narration that identifies *al-maghḍūb 'alayhim* as those who had knowledge but acted contrary to it — such as certain groups among the People of the Book who knew the Prophet ﷺ was true but rejected him out of arrogance. *Aḍ-ḍāllīn* are those who acted out of ignorance without sound knowledge.

He emphasises that both errors — arrogant rejection of truth and ignorant deviation — are dangers **for this ummah too**, not just for past nations.

### 📖 Tafsir Al-Jalalayn
Al-Jalalayn highlights that asking for guidance to the straight path — even when one is already a believer — is a request for *increase*, firmness, and consistency. The Muslim needs guidance constantly, not just at the beginning of their journey. This is why we ask seventeen or more times every single day.

### 📖 Tafsir As-Sa'di
Shaykh As-Sa'di beautifully writes:

> *"The fact that Allah commanded us to make this supplication seventeen times a day in prayer — and more for those who pray sunnah prayers — shows how desperately our hearts need constant guidance, and how Allah loves that His servants return to Him in this need again and again."*

He also notes that the straight path requires both **correct knowledge AND sincere action** — one without the other leads to deviation. Knowledge without action = path of anger. Action without knowledge = path of straying.

---

## ✨ Key Lessons

- 🛤️ Guidance (*hidāyah*) is the greatest gift — we ask for it at least seventeen times daily.
- 📚 Knowledge without action leads to anger; action without knowledge leads to going astray. We need both.
- 🤝 The path is not walked alone — it's the path of prophets, companions, and the righteous throughout history.
- 🔁 Even those who are already guided need to keep asking for guidance — faith needs constant renewal.
- 😇 Saying *Āmeen* loudly in prayer connects us to the angels' supplication and earns forgiveness.
