# Al-Baqarah 87–101 · A Chain of Prophets Rejected

**Surah:** Al-Baqarah (2) · **Verses:** 87–101  
**Theme:** The tragic human pattern of rejecting divine guidance even when it comes repeatedly and clearly

---

## Arabic Text

> وَلَقَدْ آتَيْنَا مُوسَى الْكِتَابَ وَقَفَّيْنَا مِن بَعْدِهِ بِالرُّسُلِ ۖ وَآتَيْنَا عِيسَى ابْنَ مَرْيَمَ الْبَيِّنَاتِ وَأَيَّدْنَاهُ بِرُوحِ الْقُدُسِ ﴿٨٧﴾

---

## Translation

> *And We did give Moses the Scripture and followed up after him with messengers. And We gave Jesus, son of Mary, clear proofs and supported him with the Pure Spirit. (87)*

---

## Explanation

This passage surveys the entire prophetic chain — from Musa to the later prophets to 'Isa عليه السلام. Each prophet came with clear signs. Each was rejected — some were called liars, some were killed. The verse captures the tragic pattern: divine mercy sends guidance; human arrogance rejects it.

**Verse 88** contains the chilling claim:
> *"Our hearts are wrapped"* — meaning, nothing more will penetrate them, we have heard enough.

Allah's response: *"Rather, Allah has cursed them for their disbelief, so little is it that they believe."*

**The Angel Jibrīl (2:97–98)**

Some among the Jews of Madinah claimed Jibrīl was their "enemy." Allah responds:
> *"Say, whoever is an enemy to Jibrīl — it is he who has brought the Qur'an down upon your heart by permission of Allah."*

Hostility to the angel who brought the final revelation is hostility to the revelation itself. You cannot accept Allah while rejecting His messenger.

**Verse 101** — When the Prophet ﷺ came as the fulfilment of all their scriptures had promised, *"a party of the People of the Book threw the book of Allah behind their backs, as though they do not know."*

---

## Scholar Callouts

### 📖 Tafsir Ibn Kathir
Ibn Kathir records that some Jewish scholars in Madinah said they were enemies of Jibrīl because he always brought punishment and severity, while they preferred Mika'il who brought rain and mercy. Allah's response clarifies: you cannot selectively accept the angels based on what they bring — all are honoured messengers of Allah.

### 📖 Tafsir As-Sa'di
As-Sa'di notes that the pattern of rejecting prophets is presented not just as historical information but as a warning to the Muslim community: do not repeat this pattern. Do not reject parts of the Sunnah you find inconvenient. Do not throw the Qur'an "behind your backs" by learning it without living it. The danger is always present.

---

## ✨ Key Lessons

- 🕊️ All prophets came with the same core message — rejecting any prophet is rejecting them all.
- 💬 Saying "our hearts are wrapped" — claiming you've heard enough — is spiritual arrogance of the worst kind.
- 📚 Reading the Qur'an without living it is its own form of "throwing it behind your back."
- ⚖️ You cannot select which parts of revelation to accept and which to reject — faith requires comprehensive submission.
- 🤲 All the angels work in service of Allah — respect for His messengers, visible and invisible, is part of faith.
