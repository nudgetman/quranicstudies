# Al-Baqarah 102–112 · Harūt & Marūt — Magic vs. True Faith

**Surah:** Al-Baqarah (2) · **Verses:** 102–112  
**Theme:** The prohibition of magic, the limits of harm, and the universal criterion of true salvation

---

## Arabic Text

> وَاتَّبَعُوا مَا تَتْلُو الشَّيَاطِينُ عَلَىٰ مُلْكِ سُلَيْمَانَ ۖ وَمَا كَفَرَ سُلَيْمَانُ وَلَٰكِنَّ الشَّيَاطِينَ كَفَرُوا يُعَلِّمُونَ النَّاسَ السِّحْرَ ﴿١٠٢﴾

---

## Translation

> *And they followed what the devils had recited during the reign of Solomon. It was not Solomon who disbelieved, but the devils disbelieved, teaching people magic. (102)*

---

## Explanation

**Clearing Sulaymān عليه السلام**

A slander circulated that Sulaymān (Solomon) was a sorcerer. The Qur'an categorically clears his name: Solomon did not disbelieve; the devils disbelieved by teaching people magic against his will. His extraordinary dominion over wind, animals, and jinn was a divine gift, not sorcery.

**Hārūt and Mārūt**

Two beings (majority of scholars say angels) sent to Babylon as a divine test — they taught magic, but warned every single person: *"We are only a trial; do not disbelieve."* Those who pursued this forbidden knowledge harmed themselves.

Key principle: *"They could not harm anyone except by permission of Allah."*

No power in the universe — including magic — operates outside Allah's absolute sovereignty.

**Verse 112 — The Universal Criterion:**

> *"Whoever submits himself to Allah while doing good — his reward is with his Lord. No fear shall come upon them, nor shall they grieve."*

*Aslama wajhahu* — to "submit the face" — means holding nothing back. The face is the most expressive, most exposed part of the body. To submit your face to Allah means complete, humble surrender. Combined with *muḥsin* (doing good): this is the complete definition of one who attains eternal success.

---

## Scholar Callouts

### 📖 Tafsir Ibn Kathir
Ibn Kathir extensively discusses magic (*siḥr*), concluding that learning and practising magic is forbidden (*ḥarām*) and in certain forms constitutes disbelief. He emphasises: "they could not harm anyone except by permission of Allah." No power in the universe operates outside Allah's absolute sovereignty — a source of both comfort and perspective for believers.

### 📖 Tafsir Al-Jalalayn
Al-Jalalayn focuses on the warning given to every person who approached Hārūt and Mārūt: "We are only a trial." The knowledge was available but the choice was the person's own. Those who pursued what they knew was forbidden have no one to blame. This principle applies broadly: temptation presents itself; the choice to follow it is ours.

### 📖 Tafsir As-Sa'di
As-Sa'di beautifully explains that submitting one's face (*wajh*) to Allah means total, humble surrender. Combined with *iḥsān* (excellence in action), this combination of correct belief and righteous action is the complete definition of the one who attains eternal success — not tribal membership, not inherited tradition.

---

## ✨ Key Lessons

- 🪄 Magic is forbidden in Islam — it involves seeking power from other than Allah and causes real harm.
- 🛡️ No harm can befall you except by Allah's permission — this is a shield of faith against fear of evil forces.
- 🌍 Paradise is not the exclusive property of any group — it belongs to those who sincerely submit and do good.
- 🎭 Every temptation is labelled "trial" — the choice to pursue forbidden paths is always ours.
- ☀️ True submission (*islām*) means giving your entire self — face, heart, and actions — to Allah.
