# Al-Baqarah 21–25 · O Humanity — The Universal Call & The Qur'anic Challenge

**Surah:** Al-Baqarah (2) · Verses 21–25  
**Theme:** The logical argument for worship, and the eternal challenge that has never been met

---

## Arabic Text

> يَا أَيُّهَا النَّاسُ اعْبُدُوا رَبَّكُمُ الَّذِي خَلَقَكُمْ وَالَّذِينَ مِن قَبْلِكُمْ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَتَّقُونَ ﴿٢١﴾ الَّذِي جَعَلَ لَكُمُ الْأَرْضَ فِرَاشًا وَالسَّمَاءَ بِنَاءً وَأَنزَلَ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ مَاءً فَأَخْرَجَ بِهِ مِنَ الثَّمَرَاتِ رِزْقًا لَّكُمْ ۖ فَلَا تَجْعَلُوا لِلَّهِ أَندَادًا وَأَنتُمْ تَعْلَمُونَ ﴿٢٢﴾

---

## Translation

> *O mankind, worship your Lord, who created you and those before you, that you may become righteous — (21) He who made for you the earth a resting place and the sky a canopy, and sent down from the sky rain and brought forth thereby fruits as provision for you. So do not attribute to Allah equals while you know. (22)*

---

## Explanation

After addressing three specific groups, the Qur'an now addresses all of humanity: *Yā ayyuha an-nās*. This universality is a hallmark of Islam — its message is not for one tribe, one nation, or one era, but for **every human being ever born**.

The argument for worship is beautifully logical:
- Allah created you and every generation before you
- He laid the earth as a resting place
- Stretched the sky as a canopy
- Sent rain
- Provided fruit and food

Every breath, every meal, every sunrise is a gift from Him. You owe your very existence to Allah. How can you then give the devotion that belongs to Him to stones, idols, desires, or anything else?

**The *Taḥaddī* — The Eternal Challenge (2:23–24)**

> *"And if you are in doubt about what We have sent down upon Our Servant, then produce a surah the like thereof and call upon your witnesses other than Allah, if you should be truthful. But if you do not — and you will never be able to — then fear the Fire."*

This challenge has stood for **1,400+ years**. Every poet, linguist, and civilisation has been unable to meet it. This is one of the most powerful proofs of the Qur'an's divine origin.

## Scholar Callouts

### 📖 Tafsir Ibn Kathir
Ibn Kathir notes that verse 22 lists the foundations of physical life that Allah provides — a stable earth, a protective sky, life-giving rain, nourishing food — to establish that the God who sustains you in every material need is the same God deserving all your devotion. He also notes that the Arabs of Quraysh — the greatest masters of eloquence in history — were rendered completely silent in the face of the Qur'an despite their desperate motivation to disprove it.

### 📖 Tafsir Al-Jalalayn
Al-Jalalayn emphasises that the phrase 'while you know' (*wa antum ta'lamūn*) at the end of verse 22 is a powerful rebuke: setting up partners with Allah is not mere ignorance — it is committed *despite knowing better*. The argument from creation is so clear that disbelief becomes a wilful choice against one's own intellect.

### 📖 Tafsir As-Sa'di
On the eternal Qur'anic challenge, As-Sa'di writes that the Arabs tried every means to counter the Prophet ﷺ — ridicule, torture, war, assassination, economic boycott. The one thing they never attempted was to produce something like the Qur'an — because they knew, better than anyone, that it was impossible. Their silence on this point was itself the loudest testimony to its divine origin.

## ✨ Key Lessons

- 🌍 Islam's call is universal — not for Arabs, not for a tribe, but for every human soul.
- 🌧️ Count your blessings: air, water, food, earth — all from Allah. Gratitude naturally leads to worship.
- 📜 The Qur'an's inimitability (*i'jāz*) is an eternal proof of its divine origin — a challenge never met.
- 🚫 Shirk is not just religiously wrong — it is logically incoherent given all Allah has given us.
- 🌱 The goal of worship is *taqwā* — not ritual performance but a life transformed by God-consciousness.
