# Al-Baqarah 113–123 · Jewish & Christian Claims & Allah's Universal Sovereignty

**Surah:** Al-Baqarah (2) · **Verses:** 113–123  
**Theme:** Sectarian arrogance rebuked; the cosmic scale of Allah's dominion over all directions and peoples

---

## Arabic Text

> وَقَالَتِ الْيَهُودُ لَيْسَتِ النَّصَارَىٰ عَلَىٰ شَيْءٍ وَقَالَتِ النَّصَارَىٰ لَيْسَتِ الْيَهُودُ عَلَىٰ شَيْءٍ وَهُمْ يَتْلُونَ الْكِتَابَ ﴿١١٣﴾

---

## Translation

> *The Jews say "The Christians have nothing to stand on," and the Christians say "The Jews have nothing to stand on," although they both recite the Scripture. (113)*

---

## Explanation

**The Sectarian Trap**

This verse captures an eternal human dynamic: religious communities claiming exclusive ownership of truth while dismissing one another — even when they share a common scripture. The irony: they both read the same Book (the Old Testament), yet each dismisses the other. Their mutual condemnation is mirrored by those with no knowledge at all.

**The Divine Correction (2:115–117)**

> *"To Allah belongs the east and the west. Whithersoever you turn, there is the face of Allah. Indeed Allah is All-Encompassing, All-Knowing."*

No single group, no single building, no single direction contains Allah. He transcends all human categories and tribal claims.

> *"The Originator of the heavens and the earth. When He decrees a matter, He only says to it, 'Be!' — and it is."*

*Kun fa-yakūn* — The One who speaks and galaxies appear is far above any petty human dispute about who "owns" religion.

**Verse 120 — The Critical Warning:**

> *"And never will the Jews or the Christians approve of you until you follow their religion."*

A powerful reminder: seeking human approval at the cost of divine approval is the deepest form of loss. Those who demand you compromise your faith will always demand more.

---

## Scholar Callouts

### 📖 Tafsir Ibn Kathir
Ibn Kathir notes this verse was revealed in the context of Madinan interfaith disputes. The universal lesson: any religious community that dismisses all others while reading a common scripture is falling into the same trap. The criterion of truth is not "which community are you from?" but "what does the revelation actually say, and are you following it sincerely?"

### 📖 Tafsir As-Sa'di
As-Sa'di comments powerfully on verse 120: "Never seek their approval over Allah's approval. The one who chases human satisfaction at the cost of divine satisfaction has lost both — people are never fully satisfied, and Allah's pleasure has been forfeited." This applies to any believer who dilutes their faith to please surrounding culture.

---

## ✨ Key Lessons

- 🌐 Allah is not confined to one direction, one building, or one group — He encompasses everything.
- ⚡ *Kun fa-yakūn* — remember the One you worship. He speaks and galaxies obey.
- 🤐 Sectarian arrogance — claiming only my group is right — is the same as speaking without knowledge.
- 🎯 Never sacrifice truth to win approval. Those who demand you compromise will demand more, endlessly.
- 📖 The measure of truth is the revelation — not tribal membership or inherited tradition.
