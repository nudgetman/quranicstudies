# Al-Baqarah 26–29 · Allegories in the Qur'an & Creation of the Universe

**Surah:** Al-Baqarah (2) · Verses 26–29  
**Theme:** Why Allah uses humble examples, and the cosmic scale of His creation

---

## Arabic Text

> إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَا يَسْتَحْيِي أَن يَضْرِبَ مَثَلًا مَّا بَعُوضَةً فَمَا فَوْقَهَا ۚ فَأَمَّا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا فَيَعْلَمُونَ أَنَّهُ الْحَقُّ مِن رَّبِّهِمْ ۖ وَأَمَّا الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا فَيَقُولُونَ مَاذَا أَرَادَ اللَّهُ بِهَٰذَا مَثَلًا ﴿٢٦﴾

---

## Translation

> *Indeed, Allah is not shy to present an example — that of a mosquito or what is smaller than it. And those who have believed know that it is the truth from their Lord. But as for those who disbelieve, they say, 'What did Allah intend by this as an example?' (26)*

---

## Explanation

This passage responds to a criticism raised: why does the Qur'an use examples of lowly things like flies and spiders?

The Qur'an's response is majestic: Allah is not "shy" — He uses whatever example best conveys the truth, regardless of whether the object is grand or humble. In fact, modern science has revealed that the mosquito, with its astounding biological complexity, is one of the most sophisticated organisms on earth — a living proof of Allah's craftsmanship in the miniature.

The same example **separates believers from disbelievers**: the believer sees wisdom and truth; the doubter sees only mockery. The same sun that melts butter hardens clay. The response to the Qur'an is a test of the heart's condition.

**Verse 29 — A Cosmological Statement:**

> *"It is He who created for you all of that which is on the earth. Then He directed Himself to the heaven, and made them seven heavens, and He is Knowing of all things."*

The earth was created **for us** — an enormous divine gift. Then Allah turned to the heaven and fashioned the cosmos in seven layers. The full meaning of "seven heavens" extends beyond what science can measure, though it invites reflection on the layered vastness of Allah's creation.

---

## Scholar Callouts

### 📖 Tafsir Ibn Kathir
Ibn Kathir records that the mushrikīn and People of the Book mocked the Qur'an for mentioning a fly and a spider. This verse was revealed in response. He notes the great wisdom: even the smallest creature contains infinite signs of Allah's power and precision. The believer extracts lessons from everything; the doubter finds fault in everything.

### 📖 Tafsir As-Sa'di
As-Sa'di writes powerfully: 'The Qur'an's parables are not random — they are perfectly calibrated to the truth being conveyed. A mosquito's example may carry greater wisdom than an example of a mountain, depending on the lesson. Those who object to Qur'anic parables reveal only their own arrogance, not any deficiency in the Book.' He also draws our attention to verse 29: the earth was created *for us* — an enormous divine gift that demands gratitude.

---

## ✨ Key Lessons

- 🦟 Never dismiss the small as insignificant — the greatest wisdom can be wrapped in the humblest example.
- 🔭 The Qur'an mentions 'seven heavens' — an invitation to reflect on the vastness and precision of Allah's creation.
- 🌏 The earth was created for humanity as a gift and a test — we are stewards, not owners.
- 💡 How you react to the Qur'an reflects the state of your heart — guidance or hardening depends on what you bring to it.
