# Al-Baqarah 75–86 · Corruption of Scripture, Hardened Hearts & False Security

**Surah:** Al-Baqarah (2) · **Verses:** 75–86  
**Theme:** The grave sin of distorting divine revelation and the danger of complacency about one's spiritual standing

---

## Arabic Text

> أَفَتَطْمَعُونَ أَن يُؤْمِنُوا لَكُمْ وَقَدْ كَانَ فَرِيقٌ مِّنْهُمْ يَسْمَعُونَ كَلَامَ اللَّهِ ثُمَّ يُحَرِّفُونَهُ مِن بَعْدِ مَا عَقَلُوهُ وَهُمْ يَعْلَمُونَ ﴿٧٥﴾

---

## Translation

> *Do you expect them to believe you when a group of them used to hear the words of Allah and then distort it after they had understood it while they knew? (75)*

---

## Explanation

**The Deliberate Distortion of Revelation**

Some among the religious scholars of Banī Isrā'īl would hear Allah's words clearly, understand them correctly, and then *consciously alter them* to serve their interests — changing rulings, removing prophecies of the Prophet ﷺ, adjusting laws that were inconvenient.

Verse 79 delivers a devastating warning:
> *"So woe to those who write the 'scripture' with their own hands, then say, 'This is from Allah,' in order to exchange it for a small price."*

This applies to any person who attributes their own fabrications to divine authority for worldly gain — one of the most serious sins in Islamic theology.

**False Certainty (2:80–86)**

Some held the belief: "The Fire will only touch us for a few days." Allah's response: "Have you taken a covenant with Allah? — for Allah never breaks His covenant. Or do you say about Allah what you do not know?"

False certainty about one's spiritual standing is a dangerous form of arrogance. The Muslim tradition warns against this through the concept of *khashyah* — reverent fear that keeps one vigilant even while maintaining hope.

---

## Scholar Callouts

### 📖 Tafsir Ibn Kathir
Ibn Kathir warns that this sin is not exclusive to Banī Isrā'īl — any religious scholar who changes religious rulings to please rulers, to earn money, or to avoid social consequences is committing the same crime. The Qur'an was protected by Allah's promise (15:9) from such corruption — a unique divine guarantee that distinguishes it from all previous scriptures.

### 📖 Tafsir Al-Jalalayn
On the false claim that the Fire would only touch them for a few days, Al-Jalalayn explains this was a fabricated belief with no basis in divine revelation — a self-comforting theology invented to justify continued sin. Without a covenant from Allah, no such guarantee exists. Wishful thinking about the afterlife does not change divine justice.

### 📖 Tafsir As-Sa'di
As-Sa'di draws the lesson for religious authorities: the scholar who changes rulings for a "small price" — financial benefit, political safety, or social acceptance — has committed a sin graver than the layperson who simply sins out of weakness. The abuse of religious authority multiplies harm across an entire community. True scholarship demands courage.

---

## ✨ Key Lessons

- ✍️ Attributing fabrications to Allah is one of the gravest sins — never speak of religion without knowledge.
- 🛡️ The Qur'an is divinely preserved (15:9) — unlike previous scriptures, it has been protected from corruption.
- ⚠️ False certainty about your spiritual state is dangerous — maintain healthy fear alongside hope.
- 👨‍🏫 Religious scholars bear a heavier burden — they must speak truth even when it costs them.
- 🏦 Trading divine truth for worldly gain is the worst possible transaction.
