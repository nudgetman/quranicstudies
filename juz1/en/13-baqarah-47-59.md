# Al-Baqarah 47–59 · Divine Favours Bestowed on Banī Isrā'īl & Their Ingratitude

**Surah:** Al-Baqarah (2) · **Verses:** 47–59  
**Theme:** An inventory of Allah's extraordinary gifts — and the pattern of human forgetfulness

---

## Arabic Text

> يَا بَنِي إِسْرَائِيلَ اذْكُرُوا نِعْمَتِيَ الَّتِي أَنْعَمْتُ عَلَيْكُمْ وَأَنِّي فَضَّلْتُكُمْ عَلَى الْعَالَمِينَ ﴿٤٧﴾

---

## Translation

> *O Children of Israel, remember My favour that I bestowed upon you and that I preferred you over the worlds. (47)*

---

## Explanation

These verses recount the extraordinary favours given to Banī Isrā'īl:

| Favour | Reference |
|---|---|
| Rescue from Pharaoh's persecution | 2:49 |
| Parting of the Red Sea | 2:50 |
| Guidance through Musa عليه السلام and the Torah | 2:53 |
| Miraculous manna and quail in the desert | 2:57 |
| Water from twelve springs | 2:60 |

Each favour was followed by ingratitude, testing, and rebellion.

**The Golden Calf (2:51–54)**

While Musa was on Mount Sinai receiving the Torah, his people fashioned a golden calf and worshipped it — just weeks after witnessing the parting of the Red Sea. This is a profound illustration of how quickly the human heart can drift from gratitude to forgetfulness without constant renewal of faith.

**Verse 48 — The Day No One Can Help Another:**

> *"And fear a Day when no soul will suffice for another soul at all, and no intercession will be accepted from it, nor will justice be taken from it, nor will they be aided."*

This sobering reminder directly challenged the false security some in Banī Isrā'īl felt — assuming their ancestry from Ibrāhīm guaranteed salvation. On the Day of Judgement, every person stands alone.

---

## Scholar Callouts

### 📖 Tafsir Ibn Kathir
Ibn Kathir recounts the Golden Calf episode in detail. He notes that Sāmirī, the instigator, told them: "This is your god and the god of Moses." Musa's fury on his return and his grief are deeply moving. Allah forgave them on condition of genuine repentance — demonstrating that even the gravest sins can be forgiven with sincere return to Allah.

### 📖 Tafsir As-Sa'di
As-Sa'di reflects: "Banī Isrā'īl were privileged above all nations of their time — yet their privileges did not protect them from sin. This teaches us that proximity to knowledge and religious tradition is not immunity from spiritual failure. What protects is genuine *taqwā*, not pedigree or position." He also notes that verse 48's description of the Day of Judgement directly corrects any group that believes shared identity provides automatic salvation.

---

## ✨ Key Lessons

- 🙈 The human heart forgets quickly — gratitude must be actively renewed through remembrance (*dhikr*) and prayer.
- 🐄 Even eyewitnesses of miracles can fall into the worst sins — miracles don't substitute for a living faith.
- 🏛️ Religious privilege and family background mean nothing on the Day of Judgement — only deeds matter.
- 🤝 No one can carry your spiritual burden — your relationship with Allah is personal and non-transferable.
- 🔁 Even the greatest sins can be forgiven with sincere tawbah — Allah's forgiveness surpasses our worst failures.
