# Al-Baqarah 136–141 · The Muslim Declaration of Universal Faith & The Qiblah

**Surah:** Al-Baqarah (2) · **Verses:** 136–141  
**Theme:** Islam's unique declaration of faith in all prophets, the primordial nature of submission, and individual accountability

---

## Arabic Text

> قُولُوا آمَنَّا بِاللَّهِ وَمَا أُنزِلَ إِلَيْنَا وَمَا أُنزِلَ إِلَىٰ إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَإِسْمَاعِيلَ وَإِسْحَاقَ وَيَعْقُوبَ وَالْأَسْبَاطِ وَمَا أُوتِيَ مُوسَىٰ وَعِيسَىٰ وَمَا أُوتِيَ النَّبِيُّونَ مِن رَّبِّهِمْ لَا نُفَرِّقُ بَيْنَ أَحَدٍ مِّنْهُمْ وَنَحْنُ لَهُ مُسْلِمُونَ ﴿١٣٦﴾

---

## Translation

> *Say: "We have believed in Allah and what has been revealed to us and what was revealed to Abraham, Ishmael, Isaac, Jacob, and the Descendants, and what was given to Moses and Jesus and what was given to the prophets from their Lord. We make no distinction between any of them, and we are Muslims in submission to Him." (136)*

---

## Explanation

**The Muslim Declaration of Universal Prophetic Faith**

Verse 136 contains something unique: Islam requires belief in **all** prior prophets — from Adam to Ibrāhīm, from Musa to 'Isa — as part of its very definition. A Muslim who rejects Moses or Jesus as prophets has left the fold of Islam. *"We make no distinction between any of them"* — no prophet is demoted, none elevated beyond prophethood.

**Verse 137 — The Universal Standard:**

> *"If they believe in the same as you have believed in, then they have been rightly guided."*

Guidance is not cultural, ethnic, or historical. Anyone who sincerely believes in Allah and all His prophets is on the straight path.

**Ṣibghat Allāh — The Dyeing of Allah (2:138)**

> *"[We take our] colouring from Allah. And who is better than Allah in colouring? And we are worshippers of Him."*

*Ṣibghat Allāh* — the "dye of Allah" — refers to the *fiṭrah*: the natural, original colouring of the human soul shaped by divine guidance. Every human is born inclined toward monotheism, justice, and submission to their Creator. Islam does not impose an alien system — it calls people back to their truest selves.

**Verse 141 — Individual Accountability:**

> *"That was a nation that has passed on. It will have what it earned, and you will have what you have earned. You will not be asked about what they used to do."*

Every soul answers for itself. This is a consolation (you don't bear others' failures) and a responsibility (you can't coast on others' righteousness).

## Scholar Callouts

### 📖 Tafsir Ibn Kathir
Ibn Kathir explains that the Muslim's declaration of faith in all prophets is a defining feature of Islam. Islam is not a new religion but the final, complete form of the one eternal religion of submission to Allah — a chain of revelation that began with Adam and was perfected with Muhammad ﷺ.

### 📖 Tafsir Al-Jalalayn
On *ṣibghat Allāh* in verse 138, Al-Jalalayn explains this as the *fiṭrah* — the innate, original nature with which every human is born: inclined toward monotheism, justice, and submission to their Creator. Islam does not impose an alien system — it calls people back to their truest selves.

### 📖 Tafsir As-Sa'di
As-Sa'di reflects on verse 141's closing principle: "Do not rely on the righteousness of your parents, your shaykh, your community, or your nation. The Day of Judgement is an individual examination. This should inspire personal accountability in every believer and prevent the false security of group identity." Verse 141 is also a consolation: you are not responsible for the failures of those before you — only for your own response to the truth.

---

## ✨ Key Lessons

- ☪️✡️✝️ Muslims are unique in being required to honour ALL prophets — rejection of any prophet contradicts Islam itself.
- 🎨 *Ṣibghat Allāh* — you were born with an innate inclination to know and love Allah. Nurture it; don't suppress it.
- 🌿 Ibrāhīm, Musa, and 'Isa were all Muslims — submission to Allah alone predates every named religion.
- 📝 You answer for yourself — not for your ancestors, your community, or your group. Prepare your own account.
- 🤝 Guidance is universal — anyone anywhere who sincerely submits to Allah and follows His messengers is rightly guided.
