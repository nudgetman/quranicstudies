# Al-Baqarah 6–7 · The Disbelievers — Sealed Hearts

**Surah:** Al-Baqarah (2) · Verses 6–7  
**Theme:** The consequence of persistent rejection of truth — what it means for a heart to be 'sealed'

---

## Arabic Text

> إِنَّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا سَوَاءٌ عَلَيْهِمْ أَأَنذَرْتَهُمْ أَمْ لَمْ تُنذِرْهُمْ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ ﴿٦﴾ خَتَمَ اللَّهُ عَلَىٰ قُلُوبِهِمْ وَعَلَىٰ سَمْعِهِمْ ۖ وَعَلَىٰ أَبْصَارِهِمْ غِشَاوَةٌ ۖ وَلَهُمْ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌ ﴿٧﴾

---

## Translation

> *Indeed, those who disbelieve — it is all the same for them whether you warn them or do not warn them — they will not believe. (6) Allah has set a seal upon their hearts and upon their hearing, and over their vision is a veil. And for them is a great punishment. (7)*

---

## Explanation

This passage describes a specific category of disbelievers — those who have stubbornly, persistently, and consciously rejected faith after seeing clear evidence.

The "sealing" of hearts and hearing is **not** Allah arbitrarily blocking people from faith. It is the **consequence** of their own repeated choices to reject truth. When a person continually chooses darkness, light eventually cannot penetrate.

> Think of it like a lock: Allah does not lock the door without the person first inserting the key of rejection themselves. As another verse states: *"So when they deviated, Allah caused their hearts to deviate"* (61:5). The sealing is a result, not an initial cause.

The three faculties sealed represent the complete spiritual apparatus:

| Faculty | Role |
|---|---|
| Heart | Understanding and internalising truth |
| Hearing | Receiving revelation and advice |
| Sight | Witnessing signs of Allah in creation |

All three channels through which guidance could arrive are shut — as a divine consequence of wilful, prolonged rejection.

---

## Scholar Callouts

### 📖 Tafsir Ibn Kathir
Ibn Kathir clarifies that these verses were revealed about specific leaders of the Quraysh who had deep knowledge of the Prophet's truthfulness but rejected him out of pride and tribalism. The 'sealing' was the divine consequence of *their own* adamant refusal. He quotes Ibn 'Abbas: 'The seal on the heart means that it cannot accept or understand good.'

### 📖 Tafsir Al-Jalalayn
Al-Jalalayn notes the linguistic precision: on the heart and hearing there is a *khatm* (seal — like a stamp that prevents entry), while on the eyes there is a *ghishāwah* (covering — like a veil). The imagery describes complete spiritual impermeability on multiple levels.

### 📖 Tafsir As-Sa'di
As-Sa'di emphasises the comforting side for the Prophet ﷺ — the verse reassures him that his grief over those who reject Islam is misplaced in this case. Some have chosen their path so firmly that no effort will avail. For us today: this is a warning to never allow our hearts to become calloused through persistent sin and rejection of truth.

---

## ✨ Key Lessons

- 🔒 A heart can be sealed — not by Allah's initial choice but as the consequence of our own repeated rejection of truth.
- ⚠️ Guard your heart: every sin leaves a stain; consistent sin layers darkness until light can't enter.
- 💔 Do not grieve excessively over those who refuse guidance — we convey, Allah guides.
- 👂 Use your eyes, ears, and heart to receive guidance while they are still open.
- 🙏 Make du'a daily: 'O Turner of hearts, keep my heart firm upon Your deen.'
