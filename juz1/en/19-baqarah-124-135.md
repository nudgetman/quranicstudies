# Al-Baqarah 124–135 · Ibrāhīm — Father of Prophets & The Building of the Ka'bah

**Surah:** Al-Baqarah (2) · **Verses:** 124–135  
**Theme:** The trials and triumph of Ibrāhīm عليه السلام, the Ka'bah as the focal point of worship, and a prayer answered 2,500 years later

---

## Arabic Text

> وَإِذِ ابْتَلَىٰ إِبْرَاهِيمَ رَبُّهُ بِكَلِمَاتٍ فَأَتَمَّهُنَّ ۖ قَالَ إِنِّي جَاعِلُكَ لِلنَّاسِ إِمَامًا ۖ قَالَ وَمِن ذُرِّيَّتِي ۖ قَالَ لَا يَنَالُ عَهْدِي الظَّالِمِينَ ﴿١٢٤﴾

---

## Translation

> *And when Abraham was tried by his Lord with words and he fulfilled them. He said, "Indeed, I will make you a leader for the people." [Abraham] said, "And of my descendants?" He said, "My covenant does not include the wrongdoers." (124)*

---

## Explanation

**Ibrāhīm's Trials**

The "words" (trials) Allah tested Ibrāhīm عليه السلام with included:
- Leaving his homeland
- Building the Ka'bah
- Leaving his wife Hājar and infant Ismā'īl in the barren valley of Makkah
- Being commanded to sacrifice his son

He passed every test — earning the title **Imām lin-nās** (a leader for all humanity) and **Khalīlullāh** (the intimate friend of Allah).

**The Profound Principle (2:124)**

Ibrāhīm's request — "and of my descendants?" — is beautifully human. Allah's answer sets a fundamental rule: **divine leadership is not hereditary**. Righteousness is not transmitted through bloodlines. "My covenant does not include the wrongdoers." This directly undermines any claim of automatic spiritual privilege through ancestry.

**The Building of the Ka'bah (2:125–129)**

As Ibrāhīm and Ismā'īl raised the Ka'bah's foundations, they made one of the most beautiful prayers in the Qur'an:

> *"Our Lord, accept this from us. Indeed You are the Hearing, the Knowing. Our Lord, make us Muslims in submission to You and from our descendants a Muslim nation... Our Lord, and send among them a Messenger from themselves who will recite to them Your verses and teach them the Book and wisdom and purify them."*

This prayer — made thousands of years ago — was answered in the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. A du'a answered 2,500 years later. Never despair of your supplication.

---

## Scholar Callouts

### 📖 Tafsir Ibn Kathir
Ibn Kathir reflects on the prayer of Ibrāhīm for the Prophet ﷺ being answered 2,500+ years later — proof that the du'a of the prophets is always answered, even if the answer comes generations later. He also explains the Ka'bah: it is not a physical home for Allah — He is far above that — but a spiritual focal point, a qiblah that unifies the Muslim ummah in worship.

### 📖 Tafsir Al-Jalalayn
Al-Jalalayn notes that the tests placed before Ibrāhīm — including leaving his wife and infant in the barren valley of Makkah, and being asked to sacrifice that same son — are among the greatest trials ever placed before a human being. His consistent, calm, grateful submission through all of them is why he earned the title *Khalīlullāh* — the intimate friend of Allah.

### 📖 Tafsir As-Sa'di
As-Sa'di draws the lesson: even while doing one of the greatest acts of worship in human history — building the Ka'bah — Ibrāhīm prays "accept this from us." Even the greatest people are most aware of their dependence on Allah's mercy in their best moments. This is the opposite of arrogance. The truly devoted soul always approaches Allah with humility, however great their deeds.

---

## ✨ Key Lessons

- 🔥 Spiritual leadership is earned through trials and righteousness, not inherited through bloodlines.
- 🕌 The Ka'bah is the heart of Islamic unity — billions face it in prayer, connecting every Muslim across time and space.
- 🙏 Ibrāhīm's du'a while building the Ka'bah was answered 2,500 years later — never despair of your du'a!
- 👨‍👦 Even the greatest acts of worship need "O Allah, accept from us" — humility before Allah always.
- ⚡ The "tests" of Allah are gifts in disguise — they elevate those who pass them to stations otherwise unreachable.
