# Al-Baqarah 1–5 · The Muqaṭṭa'āt & Portrait of the True Believers

**Surah:** Al-Baqarah (2) · Verses 1–5  
**Theme:** The disconnected letters, the Qur'an's divine authority, and the defining qualities of the God-conscious

---

## Arabic Text

> الم ﴿١﴾ ذَٰلِكَ الْكِتَابُ لَا رَيْبَ ۛ فِيهِ ۛ هُدًى لِّلْمُتَّقِينَ ﴿٢﴾ الَّذِينَ يُؤْمِنُونَ بِالْغَيْبِ وَيُقِيمُونَ الصَّلَاةَ وَمِمَّا رَزَقْنَاهُمْ يُنفِقُونَ ﴿٣﴾ وَالَّذِينَ يُؤْمِنُونَ بِمَا أُنزِلَ إِلَيْكَ وَمَا أُنزِلَ مِن قَبْلِكَ وَبِالْآخِرَةِ هُمْ يُوقِنُونَ ﴿٤﴾ أُولَٰئِكَ عَلَىٰ هُدًى مِّن رَّبِّهِمْ ۖ وَأُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الْمُفْلِحُونَ ﴿٥﴾

---

## Translation

> *Alif. Lam. Mim. (1) This is the Book about which there is no doubt, a guidance for those conscious of Allah — (2) who believe in the unseen, establish prayer, and spend from what We have provided for them, (3) and who believe in what has been revealed to you, and what was revealed before you, and of the Hereafter they are certain. (4) Those are upon right guidance from their Lord, and it is those who are the successful. (5)*

---

## Explanation

**Alif. Lām. Mīm.** — These are the *muqaṭṭa'āt*, the "disconnected letters" that open several surahs. Their precise meaning is known only to Allah. Scholars note they serve as a challenge: the Qur'an is composed of the same Arabic letters you use daily — yet you cannot produce anything like it.

*Dhālika al-kitāb* — "That is the Book" — uses the distant demonstrative *dhālika* to indicate the elevated, lofty status of the Qur'an. **Lā rayba fīh** — there is no doubt in it — is an absolute, confident declaration.

The Qur'an then paints a vivid portrait of the *muttaqīn* — the God-conscious — in four qualities:

| Quality | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Believe in the unseen (*ghayb*) | Faith in Allah, angels, paradise, hell — realities the eyes cannot verify |
| Establish prayer | Praying properly, consistently, with presence of heart |
| Spend from Allah's provision | Acknowledging wealth is a trust, not ownership |
| Believe in all scriptures & the Hereafter | Universal prophetic faith with certainty of the afterlife |

---

## Scholar Callouts

### 📖 Tafsir Ibn Kathir
Ibn Kathir explains that the *muttaqīn* are those who place a protective shield between themselves and Allah's punishment by obeying His commands and avoiding His prohibitions — *taqwā* comes from the root *wiqāyah* (protection). He also notes that 'establishing prayer' means giving it its full rights: proper times, complete ablution, correct recitation, deep focus, and consistent performance.

### 📖 Tafsir Al-Jalalayn
Regarding *al-ghayb*, Al-Jalalayn explains this as everything beyond human perception that Allah has informed us of — the divine essence, angels, the events of Barzakh (the state between death and resurrection), paradise, hell, the preserved tablet, and divine decree. Faith in these unseen realities is the bedrock of Islamic belief.

### 📖 Tafsir As-Sa'di
As-Sa'di beautifully observes that the Qur'an guides specifically those who approach it with *taqwā* — an open, sincere, humble heart. Those who approach it with arrogance or an agenda will not benefit. The rain falls on both fertile soil and rock, but only the soil blooms.

---

## ✨ Key Lessons

- 📖 The Qur'an's authority is beyond doubt — approach it with conviction, not suspicion.
- 👁️ Believing in the unseen (*ghayb*) separates mere intellectual religion from living faith.
- 🕌 Prayer is listed first among actions — it is the pillar of the deen.
- 💰 Spending from what Allah gave you purifies your wealth and your heart from greed.
- 🌟 True success (*falāḥ*) belongs to those who combine belief with consistent action.
