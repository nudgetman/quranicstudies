## What Is Juz 1?

Juz 1 spans from **Surah Al-Fatiha (1:1)** through to **Al-Baqarah (2:141)**. It contains:

- The entirety of **Surah Al-Fatiha** — the Opening, 7 verses, recited in every unit of every prayer
- The first 141 verses of **Surah Al-Baqarah** — the longest surah in the Qur'an, covering the foundations of Islamic faith, the stories of Banī Isrā'īl, and the legacy of Ibrāhīm عليه السلام

## Scholarly Sources Used

This guide draws on three of the most authoritative and widely studied works of classical Qur'anic exegesis (tafsir):

### 📚 Tafsir Ibn Kathir
- **Full Title:** *Tafsīr al-Qur'ān al-'Aẓīm*
- **Author:** Imām Ismā'īl ibn 'Umar ibn Kathīr al-Dimashqī (700–774 AH / 1301–1373 CE)
- **Approach:** Primarily *tafsir bil-ma'thur* — interpretation through narration. Ibn Kathir rigorously relies on the Qur'an explaining itself, authentic hadith of the Prophet ﷺ, statements of the Companions, and their Successors. He is known for cross-referencing Qur'anic verses with one another and for his extensive verification of hadith chains.
- **Why It Matters:** Considered one of the most reliable and comprehensive classical tafsirs. It is the go-to reference for students and scholars seeking narration-based interpretation grounded in hadith sciences.

### 📚 Tafsir Al-Jalalayn
- **Full Title:** *Tafsīr al-Jalālayn* (Tafsir of the Two Jalals)
- **Authors:** Imām Jalāl al-Dīn al-Maḥallī (d. 864 AH / 1459 CE) and his student Imām Jalāl al-Dīn al-Suyūṭī (d. 911 AH / 1505 CE)
- **Approach:** Concise linguistic and grammatical tafsir. Al-Jalalayn excels at clarifying the precise Arabic meaning of Qur'anic words and phrases, making it an essential tool for understanding the linguistic dimensions of the text.
- **Why It Matters:** Despite its brevity, it is one of the most widely studied tafsirs in traditional Islamic seminaries worldwide. Its conciseness makes it an indispensable first layer of Qur'anic understanding.

### 📚 Tafsir As-Sa'di
- **Full Title:** *Taysīr al-Karīm al-Raḥmān fī Tafsīr Kalām al-Mannān*
- **Author:** Shaykh 'Abd al-Raḥmān ibn Nāṣir al-Sa'dī (1307–1376 AH / 1889–1956 CE)
- **Approach:** Contemporary classical tafsir emphasising practical lessons, spiritual reflection, and clear plain-language explanation. As-Sa'di bridges classical scholarship with accessible application for the modern Muslim.
- **Why It Matters:** Beloved for its clarity, warmth, and focus on drawing actionable lessons from each passage. It is particularly valued by those seeking to connect the Qur'an to daily life and spiritual growth.

## A Note on Transliteration

Arabic names and terms are transliterated in a simplified style for readability:
- Prophet names use the familiar English spellings (Moses = Musa, Abraham = Ibrāhīm, Jesus = 'Isa)
- Arabic terms are italicised on first use with a brief definition
- The symbol **ﷺ** after the Prophet's name = *ṣallallāhu 'alayhi wa sallam* (may Allah's peace and blessings be upon him)
- The symbol **عليه السلام** after a prophet's name = *'alayhi as-salām* (peace be upon him)
