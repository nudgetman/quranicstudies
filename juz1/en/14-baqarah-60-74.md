# Al-Baqarah 60–74 · Miracles of Musa & The Parable of the Slaughtered Cow

**Surah:** Al-Baqarah (2) · **Verses:** 60–74  
**Theme:** Divine provision, the danger of unnecessary questioning, and the hardened heart

---

## Arabic Text

> وَإِذِ اسْتَسْقَىٰ مُوسَىٰ لِقَوْمِهِ فَقُلْنَا اضْرِب بِّعَصَاكَ الْحَجَرَ ۖ فَانفَجَرَتْ مِنْهُ اثْنَتَا عَشْرَةَ عَيْنًا ۖ قَدْ عَلِمَ كُلُّ أُنَاسٍ مَّشْرَبَهُمْ ﴿٦٠﴾

---

## Translation

> *And when Moses prayed for water for his people, We said, "Strike with your staff the rock." And there gushed forth from it twelve springs, and every people knew its watering place. (60)*

---

## Explanation

**The Twelve Springs** — twelve springs from a single rock for twelve tribes. Yet even this extraordinary favour was met with complaint: *"We are tired of one kind of food; pray to your Lord to bring us what the earth produces"* (2:61). The reply: "Would you exchange what is better for what is less?"

**The Story of the Cow (2:67–74)** — the incident after which the surah is named.

When a man was murdered, dispute arose over the killer. Allah commanded: slaughter a cow and strike the dead man with part of it, and he will testify to his killer. But Banī Isrā'īl asked endless unnecessary questions:
- What age should the cow be?
- What colour?
- Has it ploughed?

Each question made the command more specific and the task more burdensome — **through their own complication**. The lesson: when Allah commands, the response should be immediate obedience, not endless interrogation.

**Verse 74 — The Hardened Heart:**

> *"Then your hearts hardened after that, being like stones or even harder. For indeed, there are rocks from which rivers burst forth, and there are some that split open and water comes out, and there are some that fall down out of the fear of Allah."*

Rocks respond to Allah; yet some human hearts do not. This is a devastating comparison.

---

## Scholar Callouts

### 📖 Tafsir Ibn Kathir
Ibn Kathir notes the profound legal lesson: had Banī Isrā'īl simply gone and slaughtered *any* cow, they would have fulfilled the command. Their excessive questioning made the obligation progressively harder. He quotes the hadith: *"If I had commanded you with something, do of it what you are able, and if I forbade you something, leave it."* Unnecessary questioning of divine commands complicates the straightforward path of obedience.

### 📖 Tafsir Al-Jalalayn
On verse 74, Al-Jalalayn explains that even inanimate creation — rivers, rocks, mountains — responds to Allah in its own manner. The rock that "falls down out of fear of Allah" is a profound image. If creation glorifies and responds to Allah, how much more should the human heart — the most honoured of creations — respond with awe and devotion?

### 📖 Tafsir As-Sa'di
As-Sa'di writes: "The comparison of hardened hearts to rocks — and then noting that rocks are *better*, because they still split with water and fall with fear of Allah — is a devastating indictment. To have a heart harder than rock is a catastrophic spiritual state. The antidote is constant engagement with the Qur'an, which softens what sin has hardened."

---

## ✨ Key Lessons

- ⚡ When Allah commands, act immediately — excessive questioning complicates what was simple and loses blessings.
- 🪨 Even rocks are better than a hardened heart — nature glorifies Allah; let us not fall below the level of stones.
- 🌊 The Qur'an is water for hard hearts — regular recitation and reflection is the cure for spiritual hardness.
- 😤 The worst ingratitude is complaining in the midst of blessing.
- 🔍 Seeking miracles instead of simply obeying is a sign of weak faith — faith acts on trust, not on proof.
